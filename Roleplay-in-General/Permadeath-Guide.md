# Permadeath Guide

Death in GTA V roleplay is complicated. On one hand, characters get shot, stabbed, and hit by cars regularly — and they walk out of the hospital the same day. On the other hand, some deaths are final. The character is gone forever. No respawn, no hospital, no second chance.

This is permadeath — also called a CK (Character Kill) — and it is one of the most powerful narrative tools available in roleplay. It is also one of the most misunderstood. This guide explains what permadeath is, when to use it, and how to do it in a way that creates stories people remember.

---

## What Is Permadeath?

Permadeath is the permanent, irreversible death of a character. Once a character is perma'd, they cannot be played again — not temporarily, not as a ghost, not through a flashback. They are gone from the world. Their story is over.

This is different from a regular death in RP. Normal deaths follow the New Life Rule — your character wakes up at the hospital, loses memory of the events surrounding their death, and continues living. Permadeath is the exception. It is the end.

---

## CK vs PK — Understanding the Difference

These two terms are often confused. They are fundamentally different.

| | CK (Character Kill) | PK (Player Kill) |
|---|---|---|
| **What happens** | The character dies permanently and can never be played again | The character dies temporarily — NLR applies, but they continue to exist |
| **Memory** | N/A — the character is gone | The character loses memory of the events around their death |
| **Can the character return?** | No | Yes — they respawn or wake up at the hospital |
| **Player impact** | The player must create a new character | The player continues with the same character |
| **When it happens** | By player choice or specific server rules | During normal RP deaths (shootouts, accidents, etc.) |
| **Permanence** | Permanent | Temporary |

**In short:** A PK is a bad day for your character. A CK is the last day for your character.

## When to Permadeath Your Character

Permadeath should never be a casual decision. It should be the culmination of a story arc — the natural and meaningful conclusion to your character's journey.

### Good Reasons to Permadeath

| Reason | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| **The story demands it.** | Your character's arc has reached a point where death is the most honest, compelling conclusion. They have been on a collision course and there is no realistic way out. |
| **You are ready to move on.** | You have told the stories you wanted to tell with this character. They have lived a full life. Rather than letting them fade into inactivity, you give them a final chapter. |
| **The scene calls for it.** | A scene reaches a point of such intensity and narrative weight that permadeath is the only response that does it justice. This is rare and should be organic, not premeditated. |
| **Consequence of choices.** | Your character made decisions — joining a war, betraying a dangerous person, refusing to back down — and the consequences have caught up. Accepting the CK honors the story. |
| **For the server's benefit.** | Sometimes a character's death creates ripple effects that generate dozens of new storylines for other players — investigations, grief arcs, power vacuums, legacy conflicts. |

### Bad Reasons to Permadeath

| Reason | Why It Does Not Work |
|---|---|
| **You are angry at the server or players.** | A rage-CK is not a story decision. It is an emotional reaction. Cool off first. |
| **You want attention or sympathy.** | Permadeath for shock value without narrative foundation is hollow. |
| **Someone pressured you into it.** | Permadeath should always be your choice (on consent-based servers). No one should guilt you into killing your character. |
| **You are bored.** | If you are bored, reinvent your character — new goals, new relationships, new problems. Boredom is not a narrative reason for death. |
| **You want to "win" a conflict by denying closure.** | Permaing your character to prevent someone else from getting an RP outcome they earned (a trial, a confrontation, a conversation) is poor form. |

## How to Give Your Character a Meaningful Death

A permadeath scene is the final performance of a character you may have played for weeks, months, or even years. It deserves care, intention, and collaboration.

### Planning the Scene

1. **Decide on the context.** How does your character die? In a shootout? A car accident? An illness? Suicide? Assassination? Each context creates a different kind of story.
2. **Involve the right people.** Who should be present? Close friends, enemies, the person responsible for their death, witnesses. Give key players the opportunity to be part of the scene.
3. **Communicate OOC.** Tell the people involved that this is a permadeath. Not everyone needs to know in advance, but the players most affected should have the chance to prepare emotionally and narratively.
4. **Set the stage.** Choose a location that means something — the place your character called home, the corner where they grew up, the office they built, or a desolate stretch of highway that matches the loneliness of the moment.

### During the Scene

- **Take your time.** Do not rush the death. Let the final moments breathe. Use detailed /me commands to describe every sensation — the pain, the fear, the acceptance, the fading.
- **Give other players moments.** If someone is holding your dying character, let them speak. Let EMS try to save you and fail. Let the murderer face what they have done.
- **Stay in character until the end.** Your character's last words, last actions, and last thoughts should be consistent with who they were.
- **Let it hurt.** If you have played this character for a long time, the scene should be emotional. That emotion is real and it is valid. Lean into it.

### Example — Final Moments

> */me slumps against the wall, a hand pressed against the wound in his side. Blood seeps between his fingers, dark and steady. His breathing is shallow — each exhale shorter than the last.*
>
> "Tell... tell her I tried. Tell her I tried to be better."
>
> */me's eyes drift upward, unfocused, staring at something no one else can see. His hand goes slack. The blood pools on the concrete. The city sounds continue as if nothing happened.*
>
> */do [Character Name] is dead.*

## The Emotional Impact

Permadeath affects more than just the player who loses their character. It affects everyone who interacted with that character — friends, enemies, coworkers, family members.

**For the player:**
- Grief is normal. You spent time building someone, and now they are gone. Allow yourself to feel that.
- Take a break if you need one. There is no rush to create a new character.
- Talk to friends about the experience — OOC debriefing after a major permadeath is healthy and important.

**For other players:**
- Respect the moment. Do not make jokes or minimize the scene.
- RP the grief. Your character lost someone. Let that affect them — changed behavior, sadness, anger, memorials.
- Reach out to the player OOC. A message like "That was incredible RP. I'm going to miss your character" goes a long way.

## Starting a New Character After Permadeath

After a permadeath, you start fresh. This can feel daunting, but it is also an opportunity.

### Tips for the Transition

- **Do not create a clone.** Your new character should be a different person — different personality, different background, different goals. Resist the urge to recreate what you lost.
- **Take lessons forward.** What did you learn from your last character? What worked? What do you want to explore this time? Use the experience to build someone even more interesting.
- **Start small.** Your new character does not need to be connected to your old one. In fact, it is usually better if they are not. Let them build their own story from scratch.
- **Give yourself time.** Your new character will not feel as developed as your old one right away. That is expected. Depth comes from experience, not creation.

### Can Your New Character Know About Your Old One?

This depends on the server, but the general rule is:

- Your new character can know about publicly known events (a prominent death that made the news)
- Your new character should not have insider knowledge about your old character's private life, secrets, or relationships
- Your new character should not seek out your old character's friends, enemies, or belongings unless there is a strong IC reason

## Legacy of Dead Characters

A permadeath character does not disappear from the world — their impact remains. Other characters remember them, talk about them, and are shaped by their absence.

### How Legacy Plays Out

| Legacy Element | Example |
|---|---|
| **Memorial RP** | Friends hold a funeral, build a memorial, or visit the place where the character died. Annual remembrance scenes on the anniversary. |
| **Power vacuum** | A gang leader's death creates a struggle for control. A business owner's death leaves an enterprise without direction. |
| **Grief arcs** | Characters who were close to the deceased change — depression, anger, recklessness, withdrawal, renewed purpose. |
| **Investigation** | If the death was a murder, a detective arc follows — evidence, suspects, interviews, trial. |
| **Will and estate** | The deceased character's property, money, and possessions are distributed — or fought over. |
| **Reputation** | The character becomes a legend, a cautionary tale, or a forgotten name depending on who tells the story. |

### Will and Estate RP

If your character accumulated significant assets — property, a business, money, vehicles — plan for what happens to those assets after death.

- Write an IC will before the permadeath if your character would realistically have one
- Designate beneficiaries for meaningful items
- If no will exists, the estate becomes contested — which creates excellent legal RP
- Work with admins to facilitate asset transfers through the server's economy system

## Server Rules Around Permadeath

Permadeath rules vary significantly between servers. Know your server's rules before making any decisions.

### Consent-Based Permadeath

The most common system. A character can only be permanently killed if the player who owns the character agrees to it. No one — not another player, not an admin — can force a permadeath on you.

**How it works:**
- During a scene where your character faces death, you choose whether this is a PK (temporary death with NLR) or a CK (permanent)
- The choice is yours and no one can pressure you
- You can decide in the moment or after the scene

### Rule-Based Permadeath

Some servers have specific circumstances where permadeath is automatic or can be enforced:

| Rule | Description |
|---|---|
| **CK on execution** | If your character is formally executed (by a gang, cartel, or in a structured scene with admin approval), it is a CK. |
| **CK on third strike** | After a certain number of "deaths," the next one is permanent. |
| **CK on specific crimes** | Certain high-level offenses (snitching on a cartel, for example) carry a CK clause. |
| **Faction CK rules** | Some factions have internal CK rules — joining the gang means accepting that betrayal can result in a CK. |
| **Admin CK** | In extreme cases, admins may CK a character as a consequence of repeated rule violations or story disruption. |

### Hybrid Systems

Many servers use a combination — consent-based for most situations, but with specific rule-based triggers that the player agreed to when they joined a faction or committed certain acts.

## What Not to Do

| Mistake | Why It Hurts |
|---|---|
| Permaing in anger | Robs your character of a meaningful ending and burns bridges with other players |
| Refusing to perma when the story clearly demands it | Your character surviving impossible situations strains believability and frustrates other players |
| Permaing without telling anyone | Denies closure to players whose characters had relationships with yours |
| Creating a new character who is secretly the old one | This is metagaming and most servers will ban for it |
| Using permadeath as leverage in OOC arguments | "If you don't give me what I want, I'll CK my character" is manipulative, not storytelling |

---

## Final Thought

Permadeath is not the failure of a character. It is the completion of one. The best deaths in roleplay are not the ones with the most explosions or the most dramatic last words. They are the ones that feel inevitable — the ones that make everyone who was part of the story pause and think about what that character meant to them.

When it is time, let them go. Then build someone new and start telling a different story.
