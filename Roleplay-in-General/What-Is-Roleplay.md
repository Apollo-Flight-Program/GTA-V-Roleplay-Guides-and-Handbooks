# What is Roleplay?

Roleplay (RP) is a collaborative form of interactive storytelling where participants take on the role of a fictional character and act, speak, and make decisions as that character would — not as themselves.

Think of it like an improvised TV show. Every player is both an actor and a writer. There is no script, no predetermined outcome. The story is created in real time by everyone involved.

---

## The Core Idea

In roleplay, you are not playing a video game. You are playing a **character** inside a video game world.

Your character has:
- A name (not your real name)
- A backstory
- A personality
- Goals, fears, relationships
- A job or role in the world

Everything you say and do in the game should come from your **character's perspective**, not your own.

---

## Why Do People Roleplay?

- To tell stories and experience drama, comedy, or tension collaboratively
- To experience life as someone completely different from themselves
- To be part of a living, breathing world with other players
- To create cinematic moments that feel genuine and unscripted
- Because it is genuinely fun and creative

---

## Roleplay vs. Regular Gaming

| Regular Gaming | Roleplay |
|---|---|
| You are the player | You are the character |
| Win/lose mentality | Story-driven, no "winning" |
| Game mechanics first | Immersion first |
| Talk as yourself | Talk as your character |
| React to game events | React as your character would |

---

## A Simple Example

**Regular gaming:**
> *"Bro the cop is chasing me, I'm going to lose him by driving into the tunnel."*

**Roleplay:**
> *"Come in dispatch, I've got a pursuit heading northbound. Suspect is not complying. Requesting backup."*

Same situation. Completely different experience.

---

## What Makes Good Roleplay?

Good roleplay is not about being the best actor. It is about:

1. **Consistency** — Your character behaves the same way regardless of who is watching
2. **Reaction** — You respond to what happens around you as your character would
3. **Respect** — You treat other players' characters and stories with the same seriousness you want for yours
4. **Patience** — Not every moment is action. Slow, quiet scenes build the best stories
