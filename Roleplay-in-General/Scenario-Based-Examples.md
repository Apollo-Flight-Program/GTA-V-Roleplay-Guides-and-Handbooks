# Scenario-Based Roleplay Examples

Reading about roleplay concepts is useful. Seeing them in action is better. This guide presents a collection of fully written roleplay scenarios — complete with dialogue, /me commands, /do descriptions, and explanations of why each action works.

These examples are not scripts to memorize. They are templates to study. Observe the pacing, the detail in the /me commands, the way each player gives the other room to respond, and how the scenes create stories rather than just outcomes.

---

## Scenario 1 — Traffic Stop Gone Wrong

**Context:** A patrol officer pulls over a civilian for a broken taillight. During the stop, the officer discovers an outstanding warrant. The situation escalates.

**Characters:** Officer (Lincoln-41), Driver

---

> **Officer:** */me activates overhead lights and chirps the siren once, pulling up behind the sedan on the shoulder of the road*
>
> **Officer (radio):** "Dispatch, Lincoln-41, 10-38 on a blue four-door sedan, Vinewood Boulevard and Alta Street. Running the plate now."
>
> */me exits the patrol vehicle, adjusting the body camera on his chest. He approaches the driver's side with one hand resting on the holstered weapon, flashlight in the other.*
>
> "Evening. I'm Officer Martinez with the Los Santos Police Department. The reason I pulled you over tonight is your passenger-side taillight is out. Can I see your license, registration, and insurance please?"
>
> **Driver:** */me grips the steering wheel tighter, knuckles whitening slightly. He glances at the rearview mirror before reaching slowly toward the glove compartment.*
>
> "Yeah... yeah, sure. One second."
>
> */me opens the glove compartment with deliberate slowness, pulling out a folded registration paper. He reaches into his back pocket for a wallet, fumbling slightly.*
>
> "Here you go, officer."
>
> **Officer:** */me takes the documents, scanning them under the flashlight. He notices the driver's hands are trembling.*
>
> "Thank you. Sit tight for me — I'll be right back."
>
> */me returns to the patrol car and runs the name through CAD*
>
> **Officer (radio):** "Dispatch, Lincoln-41. I've got a hit on a 10-29 — outstanding warrant for a Marcus Webb, failure to appear. Requesting backup to my 10-20."
>
> **Dispatch:** "Copy, Lincoln-41. Adam-12 en route to your location. ETA three minutes."
>
> */me approaches the vehicle again, hand now positioned closer to the weapon*
>
> "Mr. Webb, I need you to step out of the vehicle for me. Keep your hands where I can see them."
>
> **Driver:** */me's breath catches. He stares straight ahead for a long moment, his jaw working like he's calculating something.*
>
> "Officer, look — that warrant, it's a misunderstanding. I missed a court date because my kid was in the hospital. I can explain—"
>
> **Officer:** "I understand, and you'll have a chance to sort that out. Right now I need you to step out of the vehicle. Slowly."
>
> **Driver:** */me exhales sharply and reaches for the door handle. He opens the door and steps out with his hands raised to shoulder height.*
>
> "Alright. Alright, I'm stepping out. I'm not armed, I don't have anything on me."
>
> **Officer:** */me guides the man to the rear of the vehicle, maintaining a safe distance*
>
> "Turn around and place your hands on the trunk for me. I'm going to pat you down for my safety."

**Why this works:** The stop escalates naturally. The officer follows procedure — identifying himself, explaining the reason for the stop, running records, calling for backup. The driver shows fear and attempts to explain, which creates tension without resorting to running or fighting. Both players give each other room to respond at every step.

## Scenario 2 — Bank Robbery from All Perspectives

**Context:** Two criminals rob a Fleeca Bank. One hostage is taken. Police respond. Negotiation follows.

**Characters:** Robber 1 (leader), Robber 2, Hostage (bank teller), Negotiator (Sergeant Torres), Patrol Officers

---

### Inside the Bank

> **Robber 1:** */me bursts through the front door wearing a black ski mask, leveling a pistol at the teller behind the counter*
>
> "Nobody move! Hands where I can see them — NOW!"
>
> **Robber 2:** */me follows closely, locking the front door behind them and pulling the blinds down. He sweeps the room, checking for other customers.*
>
> */do The bank lobby is empty except for one teller behind the counter and a security guard near the entrance who is unarmed.*
>
> **Hostage (teller):** */me stumbles backward, her back hitting the counter behind her. Her hands come up immediately, palms open, shaking visibly.*
>
> "Oh my God — please, please don't hurt me. I'll do whatever you want."
>
> **Robber 1:** "Good. You listen to me, you don't get hurt. Start filling a bag. Cash drawers, all of them. Don't touch the dye packs or the alarm. We'll know."
>
> **Hostage:** */me nods rapidly, tears forming. She pulls a deposit bag from under the counter with trembling hands and begins pulling bills from the first drawer.*

### Outside — Police Response

> **Lincoln-41 (radio):** "Dispatch, Lincoln-41, 10-23 at Fleeca Bank, Great Ocean Highway. Front door appears locked, blinds drawn. I have no visual on the interior. Setting a perimeter on the north side."
>
> **Adam-12 (radio):** "Adam-12, 10-23 south side. I can see movement through the side window — at least two suspects, one hostage visible."
>
> **Sergeant Torres (radio):** "All units, this is Sergeant Torres. I'm taking command of the scene. Hold the perimeter. No one approaches the building. I'm initiating contact with the suspects."

### Negotiation

> **Sergeant Torres:** */me picks up the bank's external phone line and dials the interior number*
>
> **Robber 1:** */me hears the phone ring. He looks at it for a moment, then picks up.*
>
> "Yeah?"
>
> **Torres:** "This is Sergeant Torres with the LSPD. I'm outside. I want to make sure everyone in there is safe. Is everyone okay?"
>
> **Robber 1:** "Everyone's fine as long as you stay outside. We want a car — running, full tank — parked at the front door. And I want this street cleared. You have twenty minutes."
>
> **Torres:** "I hear you. I can work on that. But I need something from you first. Let me talk to the hostage — I need to know she's okay. That's how this works. We help each other."
>
> **Robber 1:** */me pauses, looking at the teller, then back at the phone*
>
> "Fine. Thirty seconds."
>
> */me holds the phone out toward the teller*
>
> **Hostage:** */me takes the phone with both hands, pressing it against her ear*
>
> "H-hello? I'm here. I'm okay. They haven't hurt me. Please — please just do what they want."
>
> **Torres:** "You're going to be okay. We're going to get you out of there. What's your name?"
>
> **Hostage:** "Sarah. My name is Sarah."
>
> **Torres:** "Sarah, we're going to get you home. Hang in there."

**Why this works:** Each player has a defined role. The robbers are menacing but controlled. The hostage shows genuine fear and cooperates (Fear RP). The police follow procedure — perimeter, command structure, negotiation. No one rushes. The scene creates tension through pacing, not through chaos.

## Scenario 3 — Bar Fight Escalation

**Context:** Two characters with a history of tension run into each other at a bar. Words escalate to a physical confrontation.

**Characters:** Jake, Marcus, Bartender

---

> **Jake:** */me is sitting at the end of the bar, nursing a whiskey. He spots Marcus walking in and his expression hardens. He turns back to his drink, saying nothing.*
>
> **Marcus:** */me walks to the bar, not noticing Jake at first. He orders a beer, then catches sight of him out of the corner of his eye. He stiffens but says nothing.*
>
> */do The bar is half-full. Music plays from a jukebox in the corner. Two other patrons sit at a booth near the door.*
>
> **Marcus:** "Huh. Didn't think they let your kind in here."
>
> **Jake:** */me doesn't look up. His finger traces the rim of the glass slowly.*
>
> "Walk away, Marcus."
>
> **Marcus:** "Or what? You gonna run to your boss again? Tell him how you can't handle your own problems?"
>
> **Jake:** */me's jaw tightens. He sets the glass down carefully — too carefully.*
>
> "I said walk away."
>
> **Marcus:** */me steps closer, leaning against the bar next to Jake*
>
> "Make me."
>
> **Jake:** */me stands up from the stool, turning to face Marcus. He's close — too close. His fists are clenched at his sides but he hasn't swung.*
>
> "You really want to do this here?"
>
> **Marcus:** */me shoves Jake in the chest with both hands, sending him back a step*
>
> **Bartender:** */me reaches under the bar, hand hovering near the phone*
>
> "Hey! Take it outside or I'm calling the cops. I mean it."
>
> **Jake:** */me catches his balance, looks at the bartender, then back at Marcus. He grabs his jacket from the stool and walks toward the door, shoulder-checking Marcus as he passes.*
>
> "Outside. Now."

**Why this works:** The fight does not start with a punch. It builds through dialogue, body language, history, and escalation. The bartender provides a natural redirect. The scene uses /me to show internal states (the careful glass placement, the clenched fists) and /do to set the environment. The confrontation moves from verbal to physical gradually.

## Scenario 4 — Medical Emergency Scene

**Context:** A civilian suffers a heart attack in a public area. EMS responds, treats the patient, and transports them to the hospital.

**Characters:** Patient (Richard), Bystander, EMS Paramedic (Unit 7)

---

> **Richard:** */me clutches his chest suddenly, stumbling against a park bench. His face goes pale and a thin sheen of sweat appears on his forehead. He drops to one knee.*
>
> "Something's — something's wrong—"
>
> **Bystander:** */me looks over and sees the man collapsing. She rushes over, kneeling beside him.*
>
> "Sir? Sir, can you hear me? What's happening?"
>
> */me pulls out her phone and dials 911*
>
> "/911 — A man just collapsed in Legion Square. He's clutching his chest and he's sweating badly. I think he's having a heart attack. Please send an ambulance."
>
> **Richard:** */me's breathing becomes rapid and shallow. He grabs the bystander's arm, grip weak.*
>
> "Can't... can't breathe. My arm — left arm is numb."
>
> **Bystander:** "Okay, just lie back. I'm going to keep you talking. What's your name?"
>
> **Richard:** "Richard... Richard Hale..."
>
> **EMS (radio):** "Dispatch, Unit 7, 10-76 to Legion Square, Code 3. Possible cardiac event."
>
> **EMS:** */me arrives on scene, grabs the trauma bag, and approaches the patient quickly but calmly*
>
> "I'm a paramedic, I've got him. Sir, can you tell me your name?"
>
> **Richard:** */me manages a weak nod*
>
> "Richard..."
>
> **EMS:** */me opens the trauma bag, pulling out a blood pressure cuff and stethoscope. She wraps the cuff around his right arm while checking his pulse with two fingers on his left wrist.*
>
> "Richard, I'm going to take care of you. Have you had heart problems before? Any medications?"
>
> */do His pulse is rapid and irregular. Blood pressure reads 180 over 110. Skin is clammy and cold to the touch.*
>
> **Richard:** */me shakes his head weakly*
>
> "No... never. Just — chest pressure. Like someone sitting on me."
>
> **EMS:** "Okay, Richard. I'm going to give you some aspirin and start an IV. We're going to get you to Pillbox Hill. You're going to be okay."
>
> */me administers 325mg aspirin orally, establishes IV access in the right antecubital, and prepares nitroglycerin sublingual*
>
> "Can I get help loading him? We need to transport, priority."

**Why this works:** The medical scenario is detailed and realistic. The patient describes symptoms authentically. The bystander acts as a first responder — calling 911, keeping the patient calm. The EMS paramedic follows real medical protocol. The use of /do to describe clinical findings gives the scene medical credibility.

## Scenario 5 — Court Trial

**Context:** A defendant is on trial for armed robbery. The prosecution presents evidence, the defense cross-examines, and the judge presides.

**Characters:** Judge, Prosecutor (ADA Chen), Defense Attorney (Public Defender Ortiz), Defendant (Marcus Webb), Witness (Officer Martinez)

---

> **Judge:** "This court is now in session. The People versus Marcus Webb, case number 2024-CR-0847. The charge is armed robbery under Penal Code section 211. ADA Chen, your opening statement."
>
> **ADA Chen:** */me stands, buttoning his jacket, and approaches the jury box*
>
> "Ladies and gentlemen, the evidence will show that on the evening of March 14th, the defendant, Marcus Webb, entered the 24/7 convenience store on Strawberry Avenue armed with a handgun. He pointed that weapon at the store clerk, demanded cash from the register, and fled the scene with approximately eight hundred dollars. He was arrested forty minutes later during a traffic stop. The arresting officer will testify to recovering the weapon and the stolen cash from the defendant's vehicle. This is not a complicated case. The evidence is clear."
>
> **PD Ortiz:** */me stands slowly*
>
> "Your Honor, members of the jury — my client maintains his innocence. The prosecution's case rests on a single eyewitness identification made under extreme stress, and physical evidence recovered during a traffic stop that my client will argue was conducted without probable cause. I ask you to listen carefully to the testimony and consider whether the State has truly met its burden."
>
> **Judge:** "The prosecution may call its first witness."
>
> **ADA Chen:** "The People call Officer Daniel Martinez."
>
> **Officer Martinez:** */me walks to the witness stand, raises his right hand*
>
> "I do."
>
> */me sits down, adjusting the microphone*
>
> **ADA Chen:** "Officer Martinez, can you describe the events of the evening of March 14th, beginning with your initial call?"
>
> **Martinez:** "At approximately 2147 hours, I received a dispatch call for a 211 at the 24/7 on Strawberry Avenue. I responded Code 3. Upon arrival, the store clerk described a male suspect — black ski mask, dark hoodie, approximately six feet tall — who had brandished a semi-automatic handgun and demanded cash. I obtained a description of the suspect's vehicle — a blue four-door sedan, partial plate starting with 'L-T.' At approximately 2230 hours, I observed a vehicle matching that description heading eastbound on Elgin Avenue and initiated a traffic stop."
>
> **ADA Chen:** "And what did you find during that stop?"
>
> **Martinez:** "After detaining the driver, I observed in plain view a black ski mask on the passenger seat. A subsequent search of the vehicle, conducted with the driver's consent, recovered a 9mm semi-automatic handgun under the driver's seat and $783 in cash in a deposit bag in the trunk."
>
> **ADA Chen:** "No further questions."
>
> **PD Ortiz:** "Officer Martinez, you said the search was conducted with my client's consent. Did you inform my client that he had the right to refuse that search?"
>
> **Martinez:** */me pauses*
>
> "I — I asked him if I could search the vehicle and he said yes."
>
> **PD Ortiz:** "But did you specifically inform him of his right to refuse?"
>
> **Martinez:** "Not in those exact words, no."
>
> **PD Ortiz:** "No further questions, Your Honor."

**Why this works:** The trial follows real courtroom procedure — opening statements, direct examination, cross-examination. Each character plays their role: the prosecutor presents evidence confidently, the defense finds procedural weaknesses, the officer testifies factually but gets caught on a detail. The defendant is present but does not need to speak in this excerpt — their fate is being decided by others, which creates tension.

## Scenario 6 — Hostage Negotiation

**Context:** A desperate individual has taken a hostage inside a convenience store after a failed robbery. The situation has devolved into a standoff with police outside.

**Characters:** Hostage Taker (Danny), Hostage (store clerk), Negotiator (Sergeant Kim), Tactical Commander

---

> **Danny:** */me paces behind the counter, sweat dripping down his face. The pistol shakes in his hand as he looks at the front windows — blue and red lights flashing through the glass.*
>
> "This wasn't supposed to happen. This wasn't supposed to—"
>
> **Hostage:** */me is sitting on the floor behind the counter, hugging her knees to her chest. She flinches every time Danny moves.*
>
> "Please... just let me go. I won't say anything. I swear."
>
> **Danny:** "Shut up! Just — shut up. I need to think."
>
> */do The store phone begins ringing.*
>
> **Danny:** */me stares at the phone for three rings before picking it up*
>
> "What."
>
> **Sgt. Kim:** "Hey. My name is Sergeant Kim. I'm with the LSPD. I'm outside, and I just want to talk. Are you okay in there?"
>
> **Danny:** "Do I sound okay? I've got half the police department outside—"
>
> **Sgt. Kim:** "I know this is stressful. I know this isn't what you planned. But right now, the most important thing is that nobody gets hurt. So let's talk. What's your name?"
>
> **Danny:** */me leans against the wall, sliding down to a sitting position. He keeps the gun pointed loosely in the hostage's direction.*
>
> "Danny."
>
> **Sgt. Kim:** "Danny. Okay. Danny, I want to help you get out of this. But I need to know the person in there with you is safe. Can you put her on the phone?"
>
> **Danny:** "She's fine. She's sitting right here."
>
> **Sgt. Kim:** "Good. That's good. Danny, what do you need? What can I do to make this better?"
>
> **Danny:** */me closes his eyes, pressing his free hand against his forehead*
>
> "I just... I needed the money. My daughter — the medical bills. I didn't plan on hurting anyone."
>
> **Sgt. Kim:** "I hear you. I believe you. And the fact that nobody's hurt right now? That tells me a lot about who you are. You're not a bad person, Danny. You're someone in a bad situation. Let's fix this together."

**Why this works:** The negotiation scene demonstrates de-escalation techniques. Sergeant Kim uses the suspect's name, validates his emotions, asks open-ended questions, and builds rapport. Danny is portrayed as a desperate person rather than a one-dimensional villain, which creates empathy and narrative depth. The hostage's silent fear adds weight to every moment.

## Scenario 7 — Undercover Cop Reveal

**Context:** An undercover officer who has been embedded in a criminal organization for weeks is exposed during a tense meeting.

**Characters:** Undercover Officer (Alex, known to the gang as "Rico"), Gang Leader (Dante), Gang Member (Vince)

---

> **Dante:** */me sits at the head of the table in the back room of the warehouse. He places a phone on the table, screen facing up. A photo is displayed.*
>
> "You know what this is, Rico?"
>
> **Alex (Rico):** */me glances at the phone, keeping his expression neutral. His pulse spikes but his face betrays nothing.*
>
> "A phone?"
>
> **Dante:** "That's a photo of a man walking into Mission Row PD. Tuesday. 3:47 AM. You want to take a closer look?"
>
> */me slides the phone across the table*
>
> **Alex:** */me looks at the photo. It is unmistakably him. His stomach drops but he leans back in the chair, maintaining composure.*
>
> "That could be anyone. Half the city looks like me in a hoodie."
>
> **Vince:** */me steps forward from the corner of the room, pulling a gun from his waistband and racking the slide*
>
> "It's him, D. I followed him. Twice."
>
> **Dante:** */me stands slowly, never raising his voice*
>
> "I gave you a seat at this table. I vouched for you. And you've been wearing a wire this whole time?"
>
> **Alex:** */me's hand drifts toward his waistband, but Vince's gun is already trained on him. He stops. Fear RP — he's outnumbered, outgunned, and exposed.*
>
> */me raises both hands slowly*
>
> "Dante — listen to me—"
>
> **Dante:** "No. You listen. You have ten seconds to give me one reason not to put you in the ground."
>
> */me the room goes silent except for the hum of the fluorescent lights overhead*

**Why this works:** The undercover reveal is built on tension, not violence. Dante confronts Alex with evidence, giving him a chance to deny it. Alex tries to maintain cover but the evidence is damning. Vince enforces the power dynamic with a drawn weapon. Alex correctly plays Fear RP — outnumbered and exposed, he stops reaching for his weapon. The scene ends on a cliffhanger, which is ideal for long-running arcs.

## Key Takeaways From All Scenarios

| Principle | How It Appears |
|---|---|
| **Pacing** | Every scene builds gradually — no instant escalation |
| **Detailed /me** | Physical actions include subtlety — trembling hands, jaw tightening, deliberate movements |
| **Environmental /do** | Scenes are set with lighting, sounds, objects, and atmosphere |
| **Room to respond** | No player writes three paragraphs without letting others react |
| **Fear RP** | Characters comply when outgunned and show genuine fear |
| **Procedure** | Police follow protocol, EMS uses medical terminology, lawyers use legal structure |
| **Character voice** | Each character speaks differently — formal, panicked, controlled, emotional |
| **Consequences matter** | Scenes lead to follow-up — investigations, trials, grief, reputation changes |
