# Character Creation Guide

Your character is the most important thing you bring into a roleplay server. A well-built character makes every interaction richer — for you and for everyone around you. This guide walks you through building a character that feels real, consistent, and interesting to play.

---

## Why Character Creation Matters

A character without depth is just a username with a skin. A character with depth has:
- Reasons for doing what they do
- Things they are afraid of
- People they care about
- A past that shaped them
- A future they are working toward

These details do not need to be broadcast to other players. They just need to exist in your head so your character reacts consistently and believably to the world around them.

---

## Step 1 — The Basics

Start with the fundamentals:

- **Full name** — First and last. Avoid joke names or names that break immersion.
- **Age** — Be realistic about what your character's age means for their experience and physical ability.
- **Nationality / Background** — Where are they from? What culture shaped them?
- **Occupation** — What do they do for a living in the game world?
- **Appearance** — What does your character look like? Does their appearance reflect their personality or background?

---

## Step 2 — The Backstory

You do not need a novel. You need three things:

**Where did they come from?**
What was their life like before arriving in the city? Family, hometown, education, key events. Keep it brief and grounded. Avoid overly dramatic tragic backstories that exist only to justify antisocial behavior.

**What brought them here?**
Why is your character in this city? Looking for work? Running from something? Following someone? This gives you a natural starting motivation.

**What do they want?**
What is your character's goal? It can be simple — stability, respect, money, belonging. Goals drive behavior and make your character active rather than reactive.

---

## Step 3 — Personality

Give your character at least three defining traits — both positive and negative. A character with only strengths is not interesting. Flaws create drama, conflict, and growth.

**Examples:**

| Positive Traits | Negative Traits |
|---|---|
| Loyal | Stubborn |
| Calm under pressure | Emotionally distant |
| Resourceful | Dishonest when cornered |
| Protective of others | Reckless |
| Hardworking | Holds grudges |

Choose traits that will naturally create conflict and interesting scenes — not traits that make your character perfect.

---

## Step 4 — Relationships

Consider who your character knows or wants to know:
- Do they have family in the city?
- Are they looking for community (a gang, a department, a crew)?
- Do they trust people easily or not at all?
- Do they have enemies from their past?

Relationships are where the best roleplay happens. Build your character to be someone who can form meaningful connections.

---

## Step 5 — Limits and Vulnerabilities

Every believable character has things they cannot do or are afraid of:

- Physical limits (injuries, disabilities, age-related limitations)
- Emotional triggers (topics that visibly affect them)
- Moral lines they will not cross
- Situations that make them uncomfortable or irrational

These are not weaknesses to be ashamed of. They are the most interesting parts of your character.

---

## Step 6 — The Voice

How does your character speak?

- Formal or casual?
- Do they use slang? From where?
- Are they quiet and reserved, or loud and expressive?
- Do they have a sense of humor? What kind?
- How do they speak to authority vs. peers vs. strangers?

You do not need to be a voice actor. Just be consistent.

---

## Common Character Creation Mistakes

**The power fantasy:**
> Ex-Special Forces, master hacker, world-class driver, never loses a fight. No flaws, no vulnerabilities. Boring to interact with and exhausting to play long-term.

**The tragedy dump:**
> Every single backstory element is traumatic and used to justify refusing consequences. Trauma can be part of a character — it should not be the entire character.

**The blank slate:**
> "I'll just figure it out as I go." Without any foundation, your character will be inconsistent and forgettable. Spend 10 minutes before you log in.

**Copying a fictional character:**
> Playing exactly as Walter White, John Wick, or any other known character. Draw inspiration, but build your own person.

Use this as a starting point:

```
Age:
Nationality:

Speech style:

Relationships / Connections:
```

---

## Final Thought

Your character will evolve. The version you create today will not be the version you play six months from now — and that is exactly how it should be. Leave room for growth, change, and surprise. The best characters are the ones that develop naturally through the stories they live through.

Build someone worth playing. Then go live their life.
