# Beginner's Guide to GTA V Roleplay

Welcome. If you are reading this, you are probably about to join a GTA V roleplay server for the first time — or you have joined one and felt completely lost. Either way, this guide will get you up to speed on everything you need to know before you start playing.

---

## What Is a GTA V RP Server?

A GTA V RP server is a privately hosted multiplayer server (on platforms like FiveM, RageMP, or alt:V) where players agree to roleplay — meaning they play as fictional characters in a living world, rather than just playing the game normally.

These servers have:
- Custom rules and laws
- Player-run economies, police departments, criminal organizations, businesses
- Whitelisted roles that require applications (police, EMS, etc.)
- Admin teams that enforce the rules
- Their own lore, map modifications, and custom scripts

Every server is different. Always read the specific server's rules before playing.

---

## Before You Join

**1. Read the server rules**
Every server has a rulebook. Read it fully. Ignorance of the rules is never an accepted excuse.

**2. Create a character**
Do not log in without at least a basic idea of who your character is. See the [Character Creation Guide](character-creation.md) for help.

**3. Understand IC and OOC**
Know the difference between speaking as your character and speaking as yourself. See [IC vs OOC](ic-vs-ooc.md).

**4. Learn what metagaming and powergaming are**
These are the two most common ways new players break the rules without realizing it. See [Metagaming & Powergaming](metagaming-and-powergaming.md).

## Your First Hours

**Start as a civilian**
Do not apply for police, EMS, or any whitelisted role immediately. Spend your first hours simply existing in the world as a regular civilian. Walk around, talk to people, observe how experienced players behave.

**Listen more than you talk**
Watch how veteran players handle situations. How do they speak? How do they react to conflict? How do they use emotes and commands? Learn by observation before trying to lead scenes.

**Ask questions OOC — not IC**
If you are confused about a rule or mechanic, use OOC chat or Discord to ask. Do not break character in the middle of a scene to ask how something works.

**Do not be afraid to make mistakes**
Every experienced roleplayer was a beginner once. Most communities are welcoming to new players who are genuinely trying. The key is to try in good faith and learn from corrections.

## Essential Commands

Most GTA V RP servers share common commands, though exact syntax may vary:

| Command | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `/me [action]` | Describe a physical action your character takes |
| `/do [description]` | Describe something observable in the environment |
| `/say` or just speaking | IC speech to nearby players |
| `/ooc [message]` | Out-of-character message to nearby players |
| `/911 [message]` | Call emergency services IC |
| `/twitter` or `/phone` | Send IC social media or text messages |

**Examples:**

> */me reaches into his jacket pocket slowly*

> */do The door appears to be locked from the inside.*

> *(( Hey, quick OOC — is this area a PvP zone? ))*

## Understanding Roleplay Etiquette

**Value your character's life**
Your character should fear death. Do not run into gunfire recklessly or treat death as an inconvenience. This is called "Fear RP" — your character should behave as a real person would under threat.

**Follow the New Life Rule (NLR)**
When your character dies, they lose memory of the events surrounding their death. You cannot return to the same area or seek revenge based on what happened before you died. Check your server's specific NLR rules.

**Do not combat log**
Never disconnect from the server during an active RP scene — especially during arrests, fights, or confrontations. This is one of the most punished offenses on any server.

**Comply first, complain later**
If you believe a rule was broken during a scene, finish the scene first. Then file a report or speak to an admin afterward. Stopping a scene mid-way to argue is disruptive to everyone involved.

**Roleplay the injury**
If your character is shot, injured, or in a crash — act like it. Limp, seek medical help, stay down. Do not immediately stand up and run as if nothing happened.

## Common Beginner Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It's a Problem |
|---|---|
| Using real name / gamer tag as character name | Breaks immersion immediately |
| Talking about game mechanics IC | "My ping is bad" is not something your character would say |
| Going straight to the action (robbery, shooting) | No story context, poor RP, usually results in a ban |
| Ignoring /me and /do commands | These are the backbone of visual storytelling in RP |
| Treating it like regular GTA Online | Completely different experience and expectations |
| Not valuing your character's life | Makes conflict meaningless |

## Types of Roleplay You Will Encounter

**Passive RP** — Everyday civilian life. Conversations, jobs, shopping, driving. The foundation of a living world.

**Action RP** — Police chases, robberies, confrontations, combat. High intensity but should always have story context.

**Emotional RP** — Serious character moments. Loss, trauma, relationships, moral dilemmas. Requires sensitivity and commitment.

**Legal RP** — Court cases, business deals, government proceedings. Highly structured and rewarding.

**Criminal RP** — Gang life, drug operations, heists. Some of the most complex and story-rich RP available.

---

## Getting Involved

**Talk to people**
Walk into a diner, a mechanic shop, a bar. Start a conversation. The best RP happens organically between characters who have no pre-planned agenda.

**Find a community**
Most servers have Discord servers where you can meet other players OOC, find groups to join, and get help from experienced members.

**Apply for a role when you are ready**
Once you understand the world and have established your character, apply for whitelisted roles like police or EMS if that interests you. These roles come with training and deeper systems.

---

## Final Words

GTA V roleplay at its best is like being part of an ongoing, improvised TV series. The moments you will remember are not the ones where you won a fight or escaped the police. They are the conversations, the alliances, the betrayals, the grief, the unexpected friendships that formed between characters.

Be patient. Be consistent. Be present in every scene.

The story is already happening — go be part of it.
