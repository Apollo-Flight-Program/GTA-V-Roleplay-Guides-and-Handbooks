# Tow Truck Driver Guide

Tow truck operators are the unsung heroes of civilian roleplay. You are the first responder for every breakdown, every accident, and every abandoned vehicle clogging the streets of Los Santos. While police and EMS handle the people, you handle the metal. This guide covers how to build a tow truck character with depth, professionalism, and plenty of story potential.

---

## The Role of a Tow Truck Operator

Your job sits at the intersection of multiple factions. You work alongside police at accident scenes. You coordinate with mechanic shops for repairs. You deal with civilians at their most frustrated — broken down on the Great Ocean Highway at midnight with no cell signal and a dying battery.

Tow operators are consistently present at high-activity scenes without being the center of attention. That peripheral position gives you a unique view of the city's stories.

## Choosing Your Setup

| Vehicle | Best For | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Slamvan (tow variant) | Light-duty towing | Good for sedans and small vehicles |
| Flatbed | Medium-duty, versatile | Handles most civilian vehicles cleanly |
| Large tow truck | Heavy-duty | Trucks, SUVs, commercial vehicles |
| Towtruck (standard model) | General purpose | The classic hook-and-chain look |

Your vehicle choice communicates your operation's size. A solo operator with a beaten-up Slamvan tells a different story than a fleet driver working for a professional towing company.

## Dispatch and Call Handling

Whether you work solo or with a company, calls come in and you need to handle them efficiently.

**Taking a call:**
> *"Tow 3, copy. Vehicle breakdown at the intersection of Strawberry Avenue and Capital Boulevard. En route, ETA four minutes."*

**Arriving on scene:**
1. Park safely — hazard lights on, off to the side of the road
2. Approach the customer and introduce yourself
3. Assess the situation — what happened, where does the vehicle need to go
4. Provide a cost estimate before hooking up

**Completing the tow:**
> *"Tow 3, vehicle delivered to Hayes Auto in La Mesa. Back in service."*

Keep dispatch communication brief and professional. If working for a company, follow their call sign and frequency protocols.

## Accident Scene Protocol

Accident scenes are where tow operators interact with police and EMS directly. Know your place and your process.

**Step 1 — Wait for clearance**
Do not roll up and start hooking vehicles while police are still investigating or EMS is treating patients. Wait until law enforcement gives you the go-ahead.

**Step 2 — Assess the vehicles**
Walk around each vehicle. Check for leaking fluids, structural damage, and tow-ability. Some vehicles may not be safe to tow normally and need a flatbed.

**Step 3 — Coordinate with police**
Officers may need to photograph or document the vehicles before you move them. Ask if they need anything before you hook up. If a vehicle is evidence, it goes to the impound lot — not a mechanic shop.

**Step 4 — Clear the scene**
Once vehicles are loaded, clear the road as quickly as safely possible. Traffic is backing up and people are getting impatient. That is part of the RP.

**Step 5 — Deliver the vehicle**
Take it where it needs to go — the owner's preferred mechanic, the impound lot, or a holding yard.

## Impound Procedures

Impounding vehicles is a regular part of the job, usually at police request.

| Reason for Impound | Who Authorizes | Typical Hold Time |
|---|---|---|
| Illegally parked | Police or parking enforcement | Until fine is paid |
| Abandoned vehicle | Police or city services | 48–72 hours before processing |
| Evidence in a crime | Police or detectives | Until investigation concludes |
| Unregistered / no plates | Police during traffic stop | Until registration is provided |
| Owner arrested | Police | Until owner is released or designee claims |
| Unsafe to drive | Police or mechanic inspection | Until repairs are verified |

When impounding, log the vehicle — make, model, color, plate number, condition, and reason for impound. This paperwork is RP gold. It creates records, accountability, and potential disputes when owners come to retrieve their cars.

## Police Cooperation

Your relationship with law enforcement shapes your daily experience.

**Building a professional relationship:**
- Be reliable when dispatched to scenes
- Follow police instructions without argument at active scenes
- Provide honest documentation about vehicle conditions
- Do not touch or move anything police have not cleared

**Boundaries to maintain:**
- You are not law enforcement — do not interrogate vehicle owners
- Do not search vehicles unless specifically instructed by an officer
- If you find something suspicious in a vehicle (weapons, drugs, large amounts of cash), report it — do not pocket it
- Maintain confidentiality about what you see at crime scenes

A tow operator who is trusted by the PD gets more calls, more work, and more interesting scenes. A tow operator who is unreliable or shady gets cut from the rotation.

## Pricing

Have clear pricing and communicate it before starting work.

| Service | Suggested Price |
|---|---|
| Standard tow (within Los Santos) | $200 – $500 |
| Long-distance tow (to Blaine County) | $500 – $1,000 |
| Long-distance tow (to Paleto Bay) | $800 – $1,500 |
| Accident recovery | $300 – $700 |
| Ditch / off-road recovery | $400 – $800 |
| Impound tow (billed to PD or city) | Fixed contract rate |
| After-hours / emergency call | +50% surcharge |
| Winch-out (stuck vehicle, no tow needed) | $150 – $300 |

Always give the price upfront. Surprising a customer with the bill after the tow is a fast way to create conflict — which can be great RP, but only if your character is intentionally that kind of operator.

## Handling Difficult Customers

Tow truck drivers deal with people at their worst. Their car is broken, they are stranded, and they are paying for something they did not want.

**The angry customer:**
They blame you for the price, the wait time, or the situation. Stay professional. Explain the charges calmly. If they escalate, give them the company's complaint number and move on. Do not match their energy.

**The sob story:**
They cannot afford the tow. They just lost their job. Their kid is in the car. Your character decides — do you give a discount? Do you offer a payment plan? Do you stand firm? Either response is valid RP.

**The intimidator:**
Someone threatens you to avoid paying. Value your life. If they become physically threatening, leave the scene and call police. You are a tow truck driver, not a bouncer.

**The runner:**
They say they will "be right back with the money" and disappear. Now you have their car. Keep it. Charge storage fees. When they come back a week later, the bill has doubled.

## Equipment and Safety

Roleplay your equipment. It adds realism and grounds the character.

**Essential gear:**
- Tow straps and chains
- Reflective safety vest
- Road flares or cones for scene safety
- Work gloves
- Flashlight for night calls
- Basic tool kit for minor roadside fixes
- Clipboard and pen for documentation

**Safety practices:**
- Always set up cones or flares when working roadside, especially on highways
- Wear your vest — `/me puts on reflective safety vest`
- Check that the vehicle is secure before driving
- Watch for traffic when working near active lanes

---

## Legal Obligations

Tow operators work within a legal framework. Know the rules your character follows.

- You cannot tow a vehicle without authorization from the owner, police, or property owner
- Impounded vehicles must be properly documented and stored
- Owners have the right to retrieve their vehicles during business hours upon paying fees
- Predatory towing (towing vehicles without proper cause to charge storage) is illegal on most servers
- You must carry proper licensing and insurance for your operation

Breaking these rules can result in lawsuits, loss of your towing license, or criminal charges — all of which are excellent RP consequences.

## Priority Calls

Not every call is equal. Learn to prioritize.

| Priority | Situation | Response |
|---|---|---|
| High | Accident scene with police/EMS on site | Immediate response, coordinate with emergency services |
| High | Vehicle blocking emergency access | Drop what you are doing, clear the obstruction |
| Medium | Breakdown on highway | Respond promptly, road safety concern |
| Medium | Police-requested impound | Respond within reasonable timeframe |
| Low | Breakdown in parking lot | Safe location, can wait |
| Low | Vehicle relocation request | Schedule at convenience |

When multiple calls come in, communicate with dispatch about which you are taking and why. Prioritization is a professional skill that adds depth to the role.

## Running a Tow Company

If you want to expand beyond solo operation:

- Register your business with the city
- Hire additional drivers and assign zones (Los Santos, Blaine County, highway patrol)
- Establish contracts with mechanic shops for referral business
- Negotiate with the PD for an exclusive impound contract
- Maintain a fleet — repair costs, fuel, insurance for each truck
- Handle scheduling, payroll, and customer complaints
- Compete with rival tow companies for territory and contracts

A tow company owner deals with business management RP on top of the driving — finances, employee problems, contract negotiations, and the constant pressure of keeping the trucks running.

## Common RP Scenarios

**The highway breakdown:**
> A call comes in for a breakdown on the Great Ocean Highway near Chumash. You arrive to find a family stranded with a flat tire. No spare. You load the car, drive them to the nearest shop, and make small talk about their road trip gone wrong.

**The suspicious impound:**
> Police ask you to tow a Vapid Dominator from a warehouse in La Puerta. The car has a modified trunk, tinted windows, and smells like chemicals. You do your job, deliver it to impound, and do not ask questions — but you notice things.

**The turf dispute:**
> Another tow operator shows up at the same accident scene. They claim they were called first. You disagree. The argument happens in front of the police officers on scene. Now the cops have to mediate a tow truck turf war while managing an accident.

**The repeat customer:**
> The same person calls you for the third time this month. Their Declasse Tornado breaks down constantly and they refuse to buy a new car. You know the vehicle's problems by heart. You have opinions about this car. You share them.

---

## Final Thoughts

The tow truck driver sees everything — accident scenes, crime scenes, domestic disputes, stranded travelers, and the occasional car left running in a field with no explanation. You are always on the edge of someone else's story, close enough to witness it but far enough to maintain your own.

Hook it up. Haul it out. Keep the city moving.
