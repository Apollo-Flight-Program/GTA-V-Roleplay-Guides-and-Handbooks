# Nightclub Guide — Entertainment Venue Roleplay

A nightclub is controlled chaos set to a beat. It is the loudest, flashiest, most high-energy business you can run in Los Santos — and one of the most demanding. You are not just selling drinks. You are selling an experience, a feeling, a place where people come to let go. This guide covers everything from booking DJs to breaking up fights to keeping the city from shutting you down.

---

## Why Nightclub RP Matters

Nightclubs are event generators. They create concentrated social interaction — dozens of characters in one space, drinking, dancing, talking, and making decisions they would not make in daylight. More storylines start on a nightclub dance floor than almost anywhere else on a server.

A nightclub also connects multiple RP ecosystems. You need security staff (ties to bouncer and protection RP), DJs and performers (entertainment RP), bartenders (food service RP), promoters (social networking RP), and the inevitable police response when things go sideways (law enforcement RP). One club, five factions, one Friday night.

## Choosing Your Venue

The type of club you run sets the tone for everything else.

| Venue Type | Vibe | Location Fit | Clientele |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mega club | High-energy, bass-heavy, massive crowd | Downtown, Del Perro | Everyone, party crowd |
| Rooftop lounge | Upscale, curated, skyline views | Pillbox Hill, Rockford Hills | Professionals, VIPs, high spenders |
| Underground rave | Illegal, intimate, word-of-mouth only | Warehouses in La Mesa, El Burro | Subculture, thrill-seekers |
| Beach club | Open-air, daytime to sunset, tropical | Vespucci Beach, Chumash | Tourists, casual crowd |
| Hip-hop club | Urban, performance-focused, high energy | Strawberry, Davis, Downtown | Music fans, local scene |
| Country / biker bar | Live music, pool tables, rough edge | Sandy Shores, Route 68 | Bikers, desert locals, country types |

The Galaxy Nightclub in Downtown, Bahama Mamas in Del Perro, and the Vanilla Unicorn in Strawberry are all iconic GTA V locations that servers frequently repurpose for club RP. If your server has custom interiors, work with the owners to design a space that fits your concept.

## Club Management

Running a nightclub is a nightly production. Each evening is a show.

### Pre-Opening Routine

| Time | Task |
|---|---|
| 2 hours before | Staff meeting — review the night's plan, special events, VIP reservations |
| 1.5 hours before | Sound check with DJ — test equipment, set levels, finalize playlist direction |
| 1 hour before | Bar setup — stock ice, cut garnishes, prep signature cocktails, restock glasses |
| 45 minutes before | Security briefing — review entry protocols, known problem patrons, emergency exits |
| 30 minutes before | Final walkthrough — clean floors, check restrooms, test lighting |
| Opening | Doors open, music starts, energy begins building |

### During Operations

- Monitor crowd density — do not exceed capacity limits
- Rotate security positions every 90 minutes to keep staff alert
- Check in with bar staff hourly on stock levels
- Manage the DJ — ensure the energy level matches the crowd
- Handle VIP requests personally or through a dedicated manager
- Watch for escalating situations before they become incidents

### Post-Closing

- Clear the venue completely — no stragglers
- Secure all entrances and exits
- Cash out registers and count revenue
- Debrief with staff — review incidents, highlights, and issues
- Schedule cleaning crew or handle it with staff
- Log the night's earnings, expenses, and notable events

## DJ Operations

The DJ is the engine of your nightclub. They control the energy, the mood, and the flow of the entire evening.

### Hiring DJs

Look for players who:
- Are willing to commit to scheduled sets (2–4 hours)
- Understand pacing — building energy over time, not peaking immediately
- Can read the room and adjust to the crowd's vibe
- Interact with the audience through emotes and occasional call-outs
- Have their own style or brand that attracts a following

### DJ Expectations

| Responsibility | Details |
|---|---|
| Set preparation | Plan a setlist or at least a direction before the night |
| Sound management | Keep volume at appropriate levels — loud enough to feel, not enough to drown conversation |
| Crowd engagement | Acknowledge the crowd, take requests, hype key moments |
| Transitions | Mix smoothly between songs — dead air kills energy |
| Collaboration | Work with the owner on theme nights, special events, and branded sets |

### Paying DJs

| Payment Model | Rate | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Flat rate per night | $1,000 – $5,000 | Regular house DJs |
| Percentage of door | 10–20% of cover charge revenue | Guest DJs, big names |
| Hourly rate | $300 – $800/hour | Fill-in or short sets |
| Residency deal | Guaranteed weekly pay + bonuses | Committed long-term DJs |

A resident DJ becomes part of your brand. They draw their own crowd, which becomes your crowd.

## VIP Sections

VIP is where the real money lives.

### VIP Offerings

| Tier | Includes | Price |
|---|---|---|
| Standard VIP | Reserved seating, priority entry, dedicated server | $500 – $1,000 |
| Premium VIP | Private booth, bottle service, personal host | $1,000 – $3,000 |
| Ultra VIP | Private room, open bar, dedicated security, DJ requests honored | $3,000 – $10,000 |

### Bottle Service

Bottle service is the nightclub's highest-margin offering:

> `/me carries a bottle of champagne to the VIP booth on a tray with sparklers, setting it down in front of the group.`
> "Compliments of the house for our VIP guests tonight. Your server Alex will take care of everything. Enjoy your evening."

**Bottle pricing (markup is expected):**

| Bottle | Retail Value | Club Price |
|---|---|---|
| Pißwasser bucket (6 bottles) | $24 | $60 |
| House vodka | $30 | $150 |
| Premium whiskey | $60 | $250 |
| Champagne | $80 | $400 |
| Top-shelf tequila | $100 | $500 |

The markup pays for the experience — the seating, the service, the sparklers, the status. Customers are not buying alcohol; they are buying a feeling.

### Managing VIP Expectations

VIP customers expect attention, priority, and exclusivity. Assign a dedicated host or server to each VIP section. Check in regularly. Ensure their drinks never go empty and their requests are handled immediately. When a VIP is unhappy, the club owner deals with it personally.

## Security and Bouncer Staff

Security is the most critical role in a nightclub. Without them, one bad night ends your business.

### Door Operations

**Entry protocol:**
1. Check ID — verify age (if your server enforces it)
2. Assess dress code compliance
3. Check the ban list — is this person barred from entry?
4. Collect cover charge (if applicable)
5. Stamp hand or provide wristband for re-entry
6. Direct to coat check, bar, or dance floor

**When to deny entry:**
- Visibly intoxicated before entering
- On the ban list for prior incidents
- Dress code violation they refuse to correct
- Carrying visible weapons (policy-dependent)
- Known troublemakers flagged by staff

> Bouncer: "Hey man, I appreciate you coming out tonight, but I can't let you in with those. House rules — no open carry inside the venue. You're welcome to secure them in your vehicle and come back."

### Inside Security

Position security staff at key points:
- **Main entrance** — controls flow in and out
- **VIP section** — protects high-value guests
- **Dance floor** — monitors for fights, harassment, and overcrowding
- **Bar area** — supports bartenders with problem customers
- **Back exit / staff area** — prevents unauthorized access

### Handling Fights

Fights will happen. How you handle them defines your club.

**Escalation protocol:**
1. **Spot it early** — watch for aggressive body language, raised voices, chest puffing
2. **Verbal de-escalation** — security approaches calmly: "Hey, let's take a breath. What's going on?"
3. **Separate** — physically move the parties away from each other
4. **Remove the aggressor** — escort them to the exit firmly but without excessive force
5. **Call police** — if weapons are involved or the situation is uncontrollable
6. **Document** — log the incident, the individuals involved, and how it was resolved

> `/me steps between the two men, placing a hand on each chest.`
> "Alright, that's enough. Both of you — outside. Now. You can sort this out in the parking lot, away from my club."

After a fight, check on bystanders and staff. A fight that is handled quickly and professionally actually builds your reputation — people feel safe knowing your team can control the room.

## Drug Presence Management

Drugs are a reality in nightclub RP. How your character handles it depends on their morality, their business sense, and the server's rules.

**Options for management:**
- **Zero tolerance** — security searches patrons, kicks out anyone caught, cooperates with police. Clean reputation, but harder to enforce and may alienate part of the crowd.
- **Look the other way** — do not actively search, but do not participate. Plausible deniability. Risky if police investigate.
- **Controlled tolerance** — allow discrete use but intervene if it becomes visible or dangerous. The "don't ask, don't tell" approach.
- **Active involvement** — the club is a front or distribution point. High profit, high risk, and it fundamentally changes your character arc from business owner to criminal.

Regardless of your approach, if someone overdoses in your club, the response is immediate: call EMS, clear the area, cooperate with medical personnel. A death in your venue can shut you down permanently — IC and OOC.

## Event Nights

Themed events fill your venue on nights that would otherwise be slow.

| Event | Description | Draw |
|---|---|---|
| Ladies' Night | Discounted or free entry for women, drink specials | Attracts a mixed crowd, high energy |
| Throwback Thursday | Classic music from a specific era | Nostalgia, sing-alongs, relaxed vibe |
| Live Performance Night | Guest rapper, singer, or band performs live | Music fans, new audience |
| Foam / Glow Party | Themed decorations, glow sticks, unique atmosphere | Novelty seekers, photo-ops |
| Industry Night | Discounts for other service industry workers (bartenders, cooks, mechanics) | Builds cross-business relationships |
| Fight Night | Organized boxing or MMA matches on-site | Betting crowd, adrenaline junkies |
| VIP Takeover | Entire club reserved for a private party | Massive single-night revenue |
| Open Decks | Amateur DJs take 30-minute sets | Community engagement, talent discovery |

Promote events at least a week in advance through IC channels. Partner with other businesses to cross-promote — a restaurant owner promotes your event, you promote their brunch. Everyone wins.

## Dress Codes

A dress code sets expectations and curates your crowd.

| Dress Code | What It Means | Club Type |
|---|---|---|
| Casual | Anything goes within reason | Beach clubs, dive bars |
| Smart casual | No sportswear, no flip-flops, look presentable | Mid-range clubs |
| Upscale | Collared shirts, dresses, dress shoes, no hats | Rooftop lounges, premium venues |
| Black tie | Formal wear only — suits, gowns | Exclusive events, galas |
| Themed | Specific attire tied to the event (all white, neon, etc.) | Theme nights |

Enforce the dress code at the door, not after someone is inside. Be polite but firm — and make sure your security staff knows the rules before the night starts.

## Noise Complaints and Licensing

Your neighbors will complain. Plan for it.

**Preventing issues:**
- Know your operating hours and stick to them
- Soundproof where possible (roleplay this with the venue design)
- Keep outdoor noise to a minimum — no speakers blasting into the street
- End outdoor events earlier than indoor ones

**When complaints come in:**
- A neighbor calls the police about noise — officers arrive and ask you to turn it down
- The city issues a formal noise violation with a fine
- Repeated violations lead to a hearing at City Hall where your operating license is reviewed

**Liquor license for clubs:**
Nightclub liquor licenses often have stricter requirements than restaurants:
- Specified hours during which alcohol can be served
- Required security-to-patron ratio
- Liability for incidents involving intoxicated patrons
- Higher licensing fees and more frequent inspections
- Separate permits for outdoor drinking areas

Treat your liquor license like your lifeline. Losing it does not just stop the drinks — it empties your club.

## Promoter Roleplay

Promoters are the connective tissue between your club and the city's social scene.

**What a promoter does:**
- Spread the word about upcoming events
- Bring groups of people to the club in exchange for a cut of the door
- Build guest lists and manage VIP reservations
- Network relentlessly — they know everyone and everyone knows them
- Create buzz and hype around your brand

**Paying promoters:**

| Model | Rate |
|---|---|
| Per head | $10 – $25 per person they bring |
| Percentage of door revenue | 15–25% of cover charge from their list |
| Flat fee per event | $500 – $2,000 depending on the event size |
| Commission on VIP bookings | 10–15% of VIP spend they arrange |

A good promoter fills your club on a Wednesday. A great promoter makes people feel like they are missing out if they are not there.

## Common RP Scenarios

**The grand opening:**
> You have been building toward this night for weeks. The interior is finished, the DJ is set, the staff is briefed, and half the city knows about it. The doors open at 10 PM. By midnight, the line is around the block. The bass is shaking the walls. Someone buys a $3,000 bottle in VIP. Your bartenders are drowning. Security breaks up one scuffle and handles it clean. At 3 AM, you lock the doors, count the money, and realize you just had the best night of your character's life.

**The police raid:**
> LSPD shows up with a warrant based on reports of drug activity. They clear the dance floor, search the back rooms, and question your staff. Whether they find anything depends on how you have been running the operation. If you are clean, it is an inconvenience. If you are not, this night changes everything.

**The VIP incident:**
> A high-profile character gets drunk in VIP and starts harassing other guests. Your host tries to manage the situation, but the VIP is spending $5,000 and feels untouchable. Do you protect the big spender or protect your other customers? The wrong call costs you either money or reputation.

**The rival club:**
> A new venue opens three blocks away with a bigger sound system, a celebrity DJ, and a lower cover charge. Your regulars start drifting. Do you escalate — bigger events, more spending, price wars? Or do you double down on what makes your club different — the vibe, the staff, the loyalty?

---

## Final Thoughts

A nightclub is a living thing. It breathes with the bass, moves with the crowd, and dies the moment the energy stops. Running one is exhausting, expensive, and chaotic — and it is some of the most exhilarating RP you will ever do.

Control the door. Trust your team. Keep the music loud and the drinks cold. The rest will sort itself out on the dance floor.
