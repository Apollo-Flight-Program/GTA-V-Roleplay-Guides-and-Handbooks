# Hospital Roleplay Guide — Inside the Walls of Pillbox Medical

The hospital is where the story continues after the sirens stop. A paramedic drops off a gunshot victim. A police officer brings in a suspect needing medical clearance. A civilian walks into the ER with chest pain. What happens next — the triage, the treatment, the surgery, the recovery — is some of the richest, most emotionally layered RP available on any GTA V server.

This guide covers hospital operations from top to bottom — emergency room flow, surgery RP, patient management, doctor-patient dynamics, and the administrative side that keeps a medical facility running.

---

## Hospital Locations in Los Santos

| Facility | Location | Typical Use |
|---|---|---|
| Pillbox Medical Center | Pillbox Hill, downtown LS | Primary trauma center, most RP happens here |
| Mount Zonah Medical Center | Davis / South LS | Secondary hospital, overflow during mass events |
| Sandy Shores Medical Center | Sandy Shores, Blaine County | Rural clinic, limited capabilities |
| Paleto Bay Medical | Paleto Bay | Small-town clinic, stabilize-and-transfer |

Most servers designate Pillbox Medical Center as the main hub for hospital RP. It has an ER, operating rooms, ICU, patient rooms, a lobby, and a rooftop helipad.

## Hospital Staff Structure

| Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| Medical Director / Chief of Medicine | Runs the hospital, sets policy, handles administration |
| Attending Physician / Surgeon | Senior doctor, performs surgeries, oversees cases |
| Resident / Junior Doctor | Learning physician, handles routine cases under supervision |
| Emergency Room Physician | First doctor patients see in the ER, triages and stabilizes |
| Nurse | Patient care, medication administration, monitoring, vitals |
| Surgeon | Performs surgical procedures — trauma, orthopedic, general |
| Psychiatrist | Mental health evaluations, 5150 holds, therapy RP |
| Pharmacist | Manages and dispenses medications |
| Hospital Security | Controls access, manages disruptive individuals, protects staff |
| Administrative Staff | Billing, records, front desk operations |

**Not every server will have all these roles filled.** Often, one or two doctors handle everything. Adapt the guide to your server's staffing reality.

## Emergency Room Operations

The ER is the front door of the hospital. Everything comes through here first.

**ER workflow:**

1. **Patient arrival** — Walk-in, ambulance delivery, or police transport
2. **Triage assessment** — Nurse or ER doctor evaluates severity
3. **Bed assignment** — Patient is placed in an ER bay based on priority
4. **Evaluation** — Doctor performs examination, orders tests
5. **Treatment** — Medications, procedures, stabilization
6. **Disposition** — Admit to the hospital, discharge, transfer, or pronounce

**Triage at arrival:**

When EMS brings a patient, the ER doctor receives the handoff report and makes a triage decision.

| Triage Level | Color | Severity | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — Resuscitation | Red | Immediately life-threatening | Cardiac arrest, massive hemorrhage, airway failure |
| 2 — Emergent | Orange | Could become life-threatening | Chest pain, severe abdominal injury, altered consciousness |
| 3 — Urgent | Yellow | Serious but stable | Fractures, deep lacerations, moderate pain |
| 4 — Less Urgent | Green | Minor | Sprains, small cuts, mild illness |
| 5 — Non-Urgent | Blue | Can wait | Prescription refills, minor complaints, chronic issues |

> **Paramedic:** "Doc, 24-year-old male, single GSW to the right upper abdomen. BP dropping — 90 over 60. Two large-bore IVs running wide open. He's been in and out of consciousness."
> **ER Doctor:** `/me takes the stretcher rail and directs the team to Trauma Bay 1.`
> "Trauma Bay 1, now! I need a chest X-ray, type and crossmatch for four units, and get surgical on the line. Let's move."

## Surgery Roleplay

Surgery is theater — literally. It is dramatic, high-stakes RP that can last anywhere from five to thirty minutes depending on the players involved.

**Pre-surgery:**
- Patient is prepped — gowned, IV access confirmed, consent obtained (if conscious)
- Surgeon reviews the case and briefs the team
- Anesthesia is administered

> `/me reviews the imaging on the lightboard, pointing to the shadow near the patient's liver.`
> "The round is lodged near the hepatic flexure. We're going to do an exploratory laparotomy. I need the abdomen prepped and draped. Let's get anesthesia started."

**During surgery:**

| Phase | RP Actions |
|---|---|
| Incision | `/me makes a midline incision through the abdominal wall, carefully cutting through each layer of tissue.` |
| Exploration | `/me inserts a retractor and begins visually inspecting the abdominal cavity for the source of bleeding.` |
| Repair | `/me locates the perforated bowel segment and begins suturing the laceration with absorbable suture.` |
| Closure | `/me irrigates the cavity with warm saline, checks for any remaining bleeders, then begins closing the fascia layer by layer.` |
| Completion | `/me places the final skin staples and applies a sterile dressing. Steps back from the table.` "Good work, everyone. Let's get her to recovery." |

**Post-surgery:**
- Patient is moved to recovery or ICU
- Surgeon briefs family or friends waiting outside
- Surgical report is documented

> `/me removes the surgical gloves and mask, pushes through the double doors into the waiting area. Sees a young man stand up anxiously from the plastic chair.`
> "Are you family? She's out of surgery. We repaired the damage and she's stable. She's going to be in the ICU for monitoring, but I expect her to recover. You can see her in about an hour."

## Patient Admission and ICU

**Admission process:**
When a patient needs ongoing care beyond the ER visit, they are admitted to the hospital.

- Assign a room or ICU bed
- Create a patient chart with diagnosis, treatment plan, and medication orders
- Assign a nurse for monitoring
- Set visiting hours and notify family

**ICU monitoring RP:**
The ICU is for critically ill patients who need constant observation.

> `/me checks the monitors — heart rate steady at 72, blood pressure 118 over 74, oxygen saturation 98%. Adjusts the IV drip rate and makes a note on the chart.`
> "Looking better. If she stays stable through the night, we can move her to a regular room in the morning."

**ICU scenarios that generate great RP:**
- Patient waking up from a coma and not knowing where they are
- Family members keeping vigil at the bedside
- A patient's condition suddenly deteriorating — code blue
- Difficult conversations about prognosis and end-of-life decisions

## Prescription Management

Doctors prescribe medications. This creates RP opportunities and responsibilities.

| Medication Type | Common Uses in RP | Considerations |
|---|---|---|
| Painkillers | Post-surgery, trauma, chronic pain | Addiction potential — track repeat requests |
| Antibiotics | Infection prevention after injuries | Standard post-treatment prescription |
| Psychiatric medications | Anxiety, depression, PTSD, psychosis | Requires psychiatric evaluation |
| Controlled substances | Severe pain, sedation | Strict documentation, potential for abuse RP |
| OTC recommendations | Minor ailments | Suggest over-the-counter options for minor issues |

**Prescription abuse RP:**
A patient who keeps coming back for painkillers creates story. A doctor who notices the pattern has to decide — confront the patient, flag the chart, report to authorities, or look the other way? A pharmacist who fills a suspicious prescription faces their own moral choice.

> "Mr. Davis, this is the third time in two weeks you've come in asking for Oxycodone. Your chart shows your original injury should be well past the acute pain phase. I'm going to refer you to our pain management specialist. I can prescribe ibuprofen in the meantime."

## Hospital Security

Hospitals are not always peaceful. Gang members come looking for rivals. Distraught family members lose control. Patients try to leave against medical advice.

**Security responsibilities:**
- Control access to treatment areas — not everyone gets past the waiting room
- Manage crowds during mass casualty events
- Remove disruptive individuals who threaten staff or patients
- Coordinate with LSPD when situations escalate beyond hospital security's capability
- Protect patient privacy — deny information to unauthorized people

> `/me steps in front of the man trying to push past the ER doors.`
> "Sir, I understand you're worried about your friend. But I can't let you back there right now. The doctors are working on him. Have a seat and someone will update you as soon as they can."

---

## Visiting Hours and Patient Privacy

**Visiting rules create natural RP friction:**
- Set visiting hours (e.g., 10:00–20:00)
- Limit visitors per patient at a time
- Deny access to unauthorized individuals — this creates conflict when a gang wants to see their injured member or police want to question a patient

**Doctor-patient confidentiality:**
- Do not share a patient's medical information with anyone without patient consent
- Police requesting medical records need a warrant or court order
- Family members can be updated on general condition if the patient consents
- This creates excellent legal and ethical RP when police pressure doctors for information

> **Detective:** "Doctor, I need to know what the victim told you about the shooting."
> **Doctor:** "Detective, anything my patient said to me during treatment is confidential. If you want access to medical records, get a warrant. I'll cooperate fully once you have one."

## Specialist Referrals

Not every doctor handles every case. Referrals add realism and generate RP for specialized characters.

| Specialist | Referral Reasons |
|---|---|
| Orthopedic surgeon | Complex fractures, joint injuries |
| Neurosurgeon | Head trauma, spinal cord injuries |
| Psychiatrist | Mental health crises, 5150 evaluations, PTSD |
| Cardiologist | Heart conditions, chest pain of cardiac origin |
| Plastic surgeon | Severe burns, facial reconstruction |
| Physical therapist | Rehabilitation after major injuries |

> "The fracture in your leg is going to need surgical fixation — plates and screws. I'm referring you to Dr. Chen in orthopedics. She'll see you tomorrow morning. In the meantime, we'll keep you comfortable and monitor for any complications."

## Discharge Procedures

Sending a patient home is the final chapter of their hospital stay.

**Discharge checklist:**
- Confirm the patient is medically stable
- Provide discharge instructions (wound care, activity restrictions, follow-up appointments)
- Prescribe any necessary medications
- Ensure the patient has transportation home
- Document the discharge in the patient's chart

> `/me hands the patient a printed sheet of discharge instructions.`
> "Keep the wound clean and dry for 48 hours. Change the dressing once a day. If you notice redness, swelling, or fever — come back immediately. I've prescribed a five-day course of antibiotics. Take them all, even if you feel fine. Follow up with your primary care doctor in one week. Any questions?"

## Medical Records

Maintaining patient records adds depth and continuity to hospital RP.

**What records should contain:**
- Patient name, DOB, and contact information
- Allergies and current medications
- Visit history — every ER visit, admission, and discharge
- Surgical history
- Vaccination records (if applicable to your server)
- Mental health notes (restricted access)
- Next of kin / emergency contact

Records allow doctors to reference previous visits: "I see you were here last month with a similar complaint. Let's run some additional tests this time."

## Common Hospital RP Scenarios

**The waiting room drama:**
> Two rival gang members are both in the ER — one as a patient, one as a visitor for a different patient. They spot each other. Security has to intervene before the waiting room becomes another crime scene.

**The ethical dilemma:**
> A known criminal is brought in with life-threatening injuries. A nurse recognizes him as the person who robbed their character last week. They still have to provide care. Professionalism versus personal feelings.

**The grieving family:**
> A doctor has to tell a mother that her son did not survive surgery. The conversation, the reaction, the aftermath — this is RP that stays with players for months.

**The escaped patient:**
> A suspect in police custody at the hospital rips out their IV and runs. Hospital security, the escorting officer, and medical staff all have to react.

---

## Final Thoughts

Hospital RP is not fast-paced. It is deliberate, emotional, and character-driven. The best hospital scenes happen when everyone — doctors, nurses, patients, visitors, security — commits to the moment. A surgery scene where the entire room is engaged creates RP that players remember. A quiet conversation between a doctor and a dying patient can be the most powerful scene on the server.

The hospital is where consequences live. Every gunshot, every crash, every overdose ends here. Make it matter.

Keep the lights on. Keep the doors open. The city depends on it.
