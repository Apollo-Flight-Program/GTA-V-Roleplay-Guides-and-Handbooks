## Part I: Personnel Philosophy

### 1.1 Who SACTF Personnel Are

The effectiveness of the San Andreas Counter-Terrorism Task Force rests entirely on the quality of its personnel. Technical capabilities, legal authorities, organizational structures — none of these matter if the people who operate them lack the judgment, integrity, resilience, and skill that counterterrorism demands.

SACTF does not look for personnel who are aggressive or fearless. It looks for personnel who are disciplined and clear-headed. The ability to act calmly under extreme pressure — to maintain analytical objectivity when the stakes are high, to follow protocols when shortcuts feel faster, to tell command things they do not want to hear — is more valuable than any individual tactical skill.

The Task Force recruits from contributing agencies. This means SACTF personnel have already demonstrated the baseline competencies of law enforcement service. The selection process does not reassess those competencies. It selects, from among those who already have them, the individuals who have the additional qualities that SACTF work specifically requires.

### 1.2 Core Character Requirements

**Integrity under pressure:** SACTF operations will, at some point, present every officer with situations where the ethical path is harder than the alternative. Fabricating a detail in an affidavit that you believe is substantially accurate anyway. Looking the other way on a partner's procedural shortcut. Providing testimony that omits inconvenient facts. These are not hypothetical challenges — they are the predictable consequences of high-stakes investigations. SACTF does not tolerate any of them.

**Analytical discipline:** The tendency to reach a conclusion and stop collecting is one of the most dangerous cognitive failures in intelligence work. SACTF personnel must demonstrate the disciplined curiosity to keep asking questions even after a satisfying explanation has emerged, and the intellectual courage to revise their assessments when the evidence demands it.

**Operational discretion:** Counterterrorism operations are frequently destroyed not by the sophistication of the target, but by the carelessness of investigators. SACTF personnel do not discuss operations in public. They do not seek recognition for classified work. They understand that the objective is the outcome, not the credit.

**Psychological resilience:** Long investigations, surveillance shifts, deep cover operations, and critical incident responses are psychologically demanding. SACTF personnel must be capable of sustained performance under conditions of tedium, uncertainty, personal risk, and moral complexity. The selection process specifically evaluates for this resilience.

## Part II: Eligibility Requirements

### 2.1 Base Eligibility

Personnel being considered for SACTF assignment must meet all of the following base eligibility requirements:

**Agency Employment:** Must be currently employed by a SACTF contributing agency (LSPD, BCSO, SASP, FIB).

**Years of Service:** Minimum three years of active law enforcement service with the contributing agency.

**Performance Record:**
- No sustained findings of excessive force within the past five years
- No sustained findings of dishonesty or integrity violations (waiver not available)
- No current Performance Improvement Plan
- No criminal charges or convictions
- Performance ratings of satisfactory or above for all evaluation periods in the past three years

**Educational Background:** High school diploma or equivalent required; college education preferred but not required for all positions. Specific positions (Legal Division, Intelligence Analysis) have higher educational requirements.

**Background:** Must be eligible for and receive clearance as required for position-specific classification access. Prior background investigations conducted by contributing agencies are reviewed but do not substitute for SACTF-specific clearance investigation.

### 2.2 Position-Specific Requirements

**Tactical Operations Division:**
- Minimum two years in an assignment requiring regular physical law enforcement activity (patrol, special operations, SWAT, narcotics, fugitive apprehension, or equivalent)
- No disqualifying physical condition that limits full participation in tactical operations
- Must pass initial tactical fitness assessment (see Part V)
- SWAT or tactical team experience highly preferred but not required

**Investigations Division:**
- Minimum two years in a detective, investigative, or intelligence assignment
- Demonstrated investigative case work — case closure records reviewed during selection
- Report-writing proficiency evaluated during selection process
- Experience with long-form investigation preferred; prior counterterrorism or organized crime investigation experience is a significant advantage

**Intelligence Division — Analysis:**
- College degree strongly preferred; analytical experience may substitute
- Demonstrated analytical writing ability required (writing sample required during selection)
- Familiarity with analytical tools and methods preferred
- Prior intelligence analysis experience (military, law enforcement, or civilian) is a significant advantage

**Undercover Operations Program:**
- Minimum three years of active law enforcement experience
- Prior undercover experience strongly preferred
- Psychological evaluation with specific focus on identity stability under stress
- No current or recent personal or financial circumstances that could be exploited by target subjects
- Detailed discussion of UOP-specific requirements is in restricted UOP documentation

### 2.3 Medical Standards

All personnel must meet general medical fitness standards for law enforcement service. Tactical Operations Division personnel are additionally subject to the tactical fitness standard. A medical examination by an approved physician is required within 90 days of selection.

## Part III: Selection Process

### 3.1 Nomination

Personnel are nominated for SACTF consideration through their home agency. The nomination process:

1. **Supervisor recommendation:** The applicant's direct supervisor submits a recommendation to the agency's SACTF Liaison Officer, identifying the position being sought and providing a detailed assessment of the applicant's qualifications.

2. **Agency review:** The home agency's senior leadership reviews the nomination and confirms agency support for the assignment.

3. **Nomination packet submission:** The SACTF Liaison Officer forwards the complete nomination packet — including service records, performance evaluations, any prior discipline records, and the supervisor recommendation — to the SACTF Administrative Division.

### 3.2 Initial Review

The SACTF Administrative Division conducts an initial administrative review of all nomination packets:
- Confirm base eligibility requirements are met
- Confirm completeness of submission
- Flag any items requiring follow-up

Applicants who do not meet base eligibility are returned to the nominating agency with specific explanation. The agency may resubmit when the disqualifying issue is resolved.

### 3.3 Background Investigation

All applicants undergo an SACTF-specific background investigation. The investigation covers:

- **Personal history:** Comprehensive review of personal background — residential history, employment history, personal and professional relationships
- **Financial review:** Assessment of financial status, history of debt or financial stress that could create vulnerability to corruption or manipulation
- **Association review:** Review of personal and professional associations for any connections to criminal organizations, extremist groups, or other entities of investigative interest
- **Social media and public record review:** Review of publicly available social media content and public records
- **Reference interviews:** Interviews with a minimum of five references, selected by the SACTF (not the applicant), who have direct knowledge of the applicant's professional conduct

Background investigations are conducted by a designated team within the Administrative Division, with FIB support for federal clearance requirements.

### 3.4 Interviews

Applicants who pass the initial review and background investigation proceed to the interview phase:

**Panel Interview:** A structured interview before a three-person panel. The panel includes a senior SACTF officer, a representative of the applicant's prospective division, and an Administrative Division representative. Structured questions assess:
- Investigative judgment scenarios
- Ethical decision-making scenarios
- Knowledge of relevant law and procedure
- Communication ability
- Motivation for SACTF assignment

**Division-Specific Interview:** For tactical positions, this includes a practical skills assessment. For investigative positions, this includes a case review exercise. For analytical positions, this includes an analytical writing exercise.

**Psychological Evaluation:** All applicants receive a psychological evaluation by an approved psychologist. The evaluation assesses:
- Cognitive function under stress
- Decision-making pattern
- Integrity indicators
- Psychological resilience and stability
- Suitability for the specific assignment type (the psychological evaluation for UOP candidates has additional specific components)

### 3.5 Selection Board

Completed packages for all applicants who clear the background and interview phases are reviewed by the SACTF Selection Board. The Board consists of:
- Deputy Director — Operations or Deputy Director — Intelligence (depending on position)
- Relevant Division Commander
- Representative of the applicable contributing agency

The Board reviews all materials and makes a selection recommendation. Final selection decisions for standard positions are made by the Deputy Directors. UOP selections require Director approval.

### 3.6 Notification and Assignment

**Selected applicants:** Notified by the Administrative Division. Assignment start date is coordinated between SACTF and the contributing agency. Home agency is given reasonable time to manage the backfill before the applicant transfers.

**Non-selected applicants:** Notified with a general statement that the selection process was highly competitive and that specific feedback is not provided. Non-selection does not reflect a finding of misconduct or unsuitability — it reflects the realities of a competitive process with limited positions.

**Reapplication:** Applicants who are not selected may reapply in subsequent selection cycles. The minimum waiting period before reapplication is six months.

## Part IV: Onboarding

### 4.1 Orientation Program

All new SACTF personnel complete a structured orientation program during their first two weeks:

**Day 1–2: Administrative and Security**
- Security clearance processing and access establishment
- Facilities orientation
- Communications systems access and training
- Equipment issuance
- Review and acknowledgment of all policy documents

**Day 3–5: Mission and Legal Framework**
- Task Force mission, history, and organizational structure
- Legal framework for SACTF operations
- Relevant legal authorities — warrant types, surveillance law, evidence standards
- Constitutional limitations
- Oversight requirements and reporting obligations

**Day 6–10: Division Orientation**
- Specific orientation to assigned division under the supervision of a designated mentor
- Introduction to current cases and operational status (appropriate to clearance level)
- Division-specific procedures, reporting requirements, and tools
- Relationship introductions — partner agency points of contact, inter-division liaisons

### 4.2 Probationary Period

All new personnel serve a 90-day probationary period. During the probationary period:
- Performance is assessed weekly by the direct supervisor
- Probationary personnel may not serve as case leads or mission commanders
- Any performance concern during the probationary period is immediately escalated to the Division Commander
- Probationary personnel who are not performing adequately may be returned to their home agency without the full separation process applicable to non-probationary personnel

## Part V: Physical Fitness Standards

### 5.1 General Fitness Standard (All Personnel)

All SACTF personnel are required to meet and maintain the following general fitness standard:

| Event | Minimum Standard |
|---|---|
| 1.5-mile run | Completed within applicable age/gender standard |
| Push-ups | Minimum 30 (age/gender adjusted) |
| Sit-ups | Minimum 35 (age/gender adjusted) |

Fitness testing is conducted semi-annually. Personnel who fail a fitness test enter a 60-day remediation program. Personnel who fail two consecutive tests are subject to return-to-home-agency action.

### 5.2 Tactical Fitness Standard (TOD Personnel)

Tactical Operations Division personnel are subject to the enhanced Tactical Fitness Standard, tested quarterly:

| Event | Minimum Standard |
|---|---|
| 1.5-mile run | Under 12:00 minutes (all ages/genders) |
| 300-meter sprint | Under 52 seconds |
| Push-ups (2 minutes) | Minimum 42 |
| Pull-ups | Minimum 6 |
| Tactical obstacle course | Pass/fail (timed with minimum standard) |
| Weighted carry — 50 lbs, 100 meters | Under 45 seconds |

TOD personnel who fail the tactical standard enter a 30-day remediation program. Personnel unable to meet the tactical standard within the remediation period are reassigned to non-tactical positions or returned to the home agency.

### 5.3 Weapons Qualification

**All personnel:** Minimum annual qualification with service pistol. Minimum passing score per the standard qualification course.

**Tactical personnel:** Quarterly qualification with service pistol. Annual qualification with long gun (rifle). Annual qualification with additional weapons assigned to the operator. All tactical personnel must pass the SACTF tactical qualification course, which includes stress inoculation elements (elevated heart rate, low light, and moving target components).

## Part VI: Separation from SACTF

### 6.1 Voluntary Separation

Personnel returning to their home agency at the end of an assignment, or separating voluntarily for any reason, complete a structured out-processing that includes:

- Return of all SACTF equipment and credentials
- System access revocation
- Clearance adjustment (classified access reverts to home agency levels)
- Debrief by the Administrative Division and Intelligence Division (for personnel with access to sensitive operational information)
- Completion of all outstanding documentation and case obligations
- Separation review with direct supervisor

Voluntarily separating personnel who have access to sensitive information are subject to post-separation non-disclosure obligations.

### 6.2 Involuntary Separation

Personnel may be involuntarily separated from SACTF for:
- Sustained misconduct findings
- Sustained integrity violations
- Medical or fitness disqualification
- Compromise of operational security
- Conduct outside SACTF that reflects adversely on the Task Force

Involuntary separations are decided by the Director for senior personnel and the relevant Deputy Director for all others. All involuntary separations are documented. The home agency is formally notified of the reason for separation; the level of detail shared is calibrated to the severity of the conduct and the security classification of relevant information.
