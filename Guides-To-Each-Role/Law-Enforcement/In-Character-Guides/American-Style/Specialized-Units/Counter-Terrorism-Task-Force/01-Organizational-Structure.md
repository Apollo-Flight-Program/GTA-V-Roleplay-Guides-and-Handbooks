## Part I: Command Structure

### 1.1 Executive Leadership

**Director, SACTF**  
The Director is the senior executive of the Task Force and is accountable to the Governor's Office of Public Safety and Security. The Director:

- Sets strategic priorities and long-range operational objectives
- Manages relationships with agency heads of all contributing organizations
- Represents the Task Force before legislative and executive bodies
- Authorizes the most sensitive categories of operation (undercover placements, long-duration surveillance, and all WMD-related operations)
- Resolves jurisdictional and inter-agency disputes that cannot be resolved at lower levels
- Signs all personnel decisions at the senior agent and supervisory grade level

The Director maintains a direct staff including a Chief of Staff, a General Counsel, a Public Affairs Officer (for authorized external communication only), and an Inspector General function.

**Deputy Director — Operations**  
The Deputy Director for Operations is the senior operational executive. All operational divisions report through this position. The Deputy Director:

- Oversees the daily operational tempo of the Task Force
- Coordinates cross-division operations involving multiple units
- Serves as Director in the Director's absence
- Reviews and approves all operational plans above the routine threshold
- Chairs the daily intelligence briefing and operational coordination session

**Deputy Director — Intelligence**  
The Deputy Director for Intelligence is the senior intelligence executive. The Intelligence Division and the Intelligence Analysis Cell report through this position. The Deputy Director:

- Sets intelligence collection priorities across all platforms
- Oversees production and quality control of all analytical products
- Manages intelligence-sharing relationships with partner agencies
- Ensures compliance with all applicable intelligence oversight requirements

### 1.2 Division Structure

The SACTF is organized into five operational and three support divisions:

```
DIRECTOR

│   ├── Tactical Operations Division (TOD)
```

## Part II: Operational Divisions

### 2.1 Tactical Operations Division (TOD)

**Mission:** The TOD provides the Task Force's direct action capability — the capacity to conduct high-risk warrant service, hostage rescue, and tactical counterterrorism operations.

**Composition:**

*Headquarters Element*
- Division Commander (Captain-equivalent)
- Executive Officer
- Operations Sergeant Major
- Intelligence Liaison Officer
- Medical Officer (Tactical Paramedic)
- Communications Sergeant

*Alpha Team (Primary Assault)*  
Six personnel: Team Leader (Sergeant), Assistant Team Leader (Senior Agent), and four Operators. Specializations across the team include: breachers, snipers, K-9 handler, communications, and medical.

*Bravo Team (Secondary Assault/Perimeter)*  
Six personnel: identical structure to Alpha Team. Bravo Team typically handles secondary entry points, perimeter establishment, and containment during Alpha operations.

*Charlie Team (Sniper/Reconnaissance)*  
Four personnel: two sniper/observer pairs. Primary function is long-range observation, area denial, and precision fire support to assault elements.

*Crisis Negotiation Unit (CNU)*  
Three personnel: Lead Negotiator, Secondary Negotiator (trained in behavioral analysis), and Intelligence Support Analyst. The CNU operates under TOD command for crisis events but works closely with the Intelligence Division for subject assessments.

**Operational Authority:** The TOD is employed only on authorized operations with approved operational plans. No tactical operation is conducted without documented intelligence, risk assessment, command approval, and a written operational plan signed by the Operations Deputy Director or Director.

**Readiness Standard:** TOD maintains 24/7 on-call readiness with a response time standard of 30 minutes from notification to deployment-ready for designated standby personnel.

### 2.2 Investigations Division

**Mission:** The Investigations Division conducts the long-form intelligence-driven investigations that form the backbone of SACTF's threat response. Investigations Division cases are the primary driver of prosecution outcomes.

**Composition:**

*Division Commander*  
Senior Special Agent in Charge (equivalent to Lieutenant). Manages all active investigation cases, coordinates with the Legal Division on prosecutive strategy, and supervises squad supervisors.

*Domestic Extremism Squad*  
Supervisor plus six Special Agents. Responsible for all investigations involving domestic extremist organizations and individuals.

*Transnational Threats Squad*  
Supervisor plus six Special Agents. Responsible for investigations involving groups with international dimensions, including transnational criminal organizations with terroristic capabilities.

*WMD/Critical Infrastructure Squad*  
Supervisor plus four Special Agents plus two Technical Specialists. Responsible for investigations involving weapons of mass destruction, critical infrastructure attacks, and cyber-enabled terrorism.

*Financial Investigations Unit*  
Supervisor plus three Special Agents with financial crimes specialization. Works across all squads to trace terrorism financing, identify money-laundering networks, and develop financial evidence for prosecution.

**Case Management:** Each active investigation is assigned a lead agent who owns the case file and coordinates collection, analysis, and operational activity. Case files are maintained in the Task Force case management system and are accessible to authorized personnel across the division.

### 2.3 Undercover Operations Program (UOP)

**Mission:** The UOP manages the Task Force's human penetration capability — the placement of undercover personnel inside or in close contact with targeted organizations.

**Composition:** The UOP does not publish its organizational details in general distribution documents. Its structure, personnel count, and operational methods are documented in restricted UOP-series documents.

**General Principles:**
- All UOP operations require Director-level authorization
- UOP personnel maintain strict cover and do not interact with Task Force personnel in any manner that could compromise their cover
- A dedicated handler-supervisor relationship governs each undercover placement
- UOP operations have defined entry criteria, operational parameters, and exit procedures
- Personnel welfare, safety, and psychological health are primary management responsibilities

### 2.4 Critical Incident Response Team (CIRT)

**Mission:** CIRT provides the Task Force's mass-casualty incident response capability — the specialized team that responds when an attack has occurred or is in progress, requiring immediate multi-agency coordination at scale.

**Composition:**

*CIRT Commander*  
Exercises command over all responding Task Force elements during declared Critical Incidents. Has authority to request and direct support from any SACTF division and to invoke mutual aid agreements.

*Operational Coordination Element*  
Three senior personnel who establish and staff the forward command post, manage communications with all responding units, track situation status, and maintain the operational picture.

*HAZMAT Integration Team*  
Two agents trained in HAZMAT operations coordination, working as liaison to LSFD HAZMAT and specializing in the terrorism-specific dimensions of chemical, biological, and radiological incidents.

*Evidence Response Team (ERT)*  
Four agents with specialized training in terrorism crime scene processing, post-blast analysis, and evidence collection under compromised conditions.

*Media Liaison Officer*  
One agent designated to coordinate with the Public Affairs Officer during incidents when media management is required. The MLO does not speak to media without explicit Director authorization.

## Part III: Intelligence Division

### 3.1 Intelligence Division Structure

**Mission:** The Intelligence Division collects, processes, analyzes, and disseminates intelligence about terrorism threats within the SACTF area of responsibility.

### 3.2 Intelligence Analysis Cell (IAC)

**Composition:** Six intelligence analysts, two senior analysts, and a Cell Chief.

**Functions:**
- **Current Intelligence:** Daily threat assessment and situational awareness products for Task Force leadership and partner agencies.
- **Target Development:** Building the intelligence picture on specific targets — individuals, organizations, networks — identified as priorities.
- **Strategic Analysis:** Long-range assessments of threat trends, organizational development, and shifting threat environment.
- **Link Analysis:** Mapping relationships, communications, financial connections, and operational associations across target networks.
- **Geospatial Analysis:** Location-based analysis of target activity, including mapping, pattern-of-life analysis, and geographic profiling.

**Products:**
- *Daily Intelligence Summary (DIS):* Brief, current-focus assessment of active threats and significant developments. Produced daily, distributed to Task Force leadership.
- *Target Packages:* Comprehensive intelligence compilation on a specific target, used as the foundation for operational planning.
- *Threat Assessments:* Evaluated analysis of the credibility, capability, and intent of a specific threat. Used to support both operational decisions and resource allocation.
- *Weekly Threat Report:* Extended analysis of the current threat environment, distributed to partner agency leadership.

### 3.3 Surveillance Unit

**Composition:** Two supervisors, eight surveillance agents organized in two four-person surveillance teams, and two dedicated surveillance vehicle drivers.

**Functions:** Physical surveillance of targets — moving surveillance, static observation, area coverage. The Surveillance Unit works closely with the Technical Operations Unit and the IAC.

**Operational Details:** See `06-Surveillance-Operations.md`.

### 3.4 Technical Operations Unit (TOU)

**Composition:** One supervisor, four technical specialists.

**Functions:** Installation and monitoring of court-authorized technical collection — electronic intercept, tracking devices, and related collection. The TOU does not conduct any technical collection without specific court authorization.

**Operational Details:** See `06-Surveillance-Operations.md`.

### 3.5 Fusion Cell

**Mission:** The Fusion Cell integrates intelligence from SACTF organic collection with intelligence from FIB, SASP intelligence functions, and other authorized partners to produce the most complete possible picture of current threats.

**Composition:** SACTF provides two senior analysts to the Fusion Cell. FIB provides two analysts. SASP provides one analyst. Additional agency representatives may be added for specific operations or threat responses.

**Products:** Fusion Cell products are classified above the SACTF standard and are distributed on a need-to-know basis. All Fusion Cell personnel must be cleared for access to federal intelligence products under applicable information-sharing agreements.

## Part IV: Support Divisions

### 4.1 Administrative Division

**Functions:**
- Personnel management for all SACTF-assigned staff
- Budget, procurement, and resource management
- Facilities management for SACTF work spaces
- Records management (excluding operational and intelligence records)
- Fleet management for SACTF vehicles
- Equipment inventory and maintenance coordination

### 4.2 Training Division

**Mission:** The Training Division ensures that all SACTF personnel maintain the qualification, knowledge, and skills required for their assigned roles.

**Functions:**
- Initial Task Force orientation for newly assigned personnel
- Specialized counterterrorism training programs
- Tactical qualification and recertification for TOD personnel
- Intelligence analysis training for IAC personnel
- Coordination of external training — advanced courses, federal programs, partner agency cross-training
- After-action review integration (translating lessons from operations into training program improvements)

**Training Standards:** Detailed training requirements are contained in `03-Training-Program.md`.

### 4.3 Legal Division

**Mission:** The Legal Division ensures that all SACTF operations are conducted within applicable legal authorities, and that evidence developed through SACTF investigations is packaged for successful prosecution.

**Functions:**
- Legal review of all planned operations at or above the review threshold
- Warrant preparation support — drafting, reviewing, and advising on affidavits for judicial authorization
- Prosecution support — coordinating with the District Attorney and federal prosecutive authorities
- Legal guidance to agents on applicable legal standards for investigative activities
- Oversight compliance — ensuring that SACTF operations comply with all applicable civil liberties and oversight requirements

## Part V: Rank Structure and Equivalencies

### 5.1 SACTF Assignment Grades

Personnel assigned to SACTF from contributing agencies retain their home-agency rank. For operational command purposes within the Task Force, the following grade structure applies:

| SACTF Grade | Description | Home Agency Equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| Director | Senior executive | Chief/Commissioner level |
| Deputy Director | Division-level executive | Assistant Chief/Commander |
| Special Agent in Charge (SAC) | Division/Squad commander | Captain/Lieutenant |
| Supervisory Special Agent (SSA) | Team/Unit supervisor | Sergeant/Detective Sergeant |
| Senior Special Agent | Experienced operator/investigator | Senior Detective/Senior Officer |
| Special Agent | Line investigator/operator | Detective/Officer |
| Intelligence Analyst | Non-sworn analytical role | Civilian analyst |
| Technical Specialist | Non-sworn technical role | Civilian specialist |

### 5.2 Command Authority

During SACTF operations, command authority flows through SACTF grades regardless of home-agency rank. A Special Agent from LSPD who holds the rank of Detective takes direction from SACTF Supervisory Special Agents during Task Force operations, even if those SSAs hold a lower home-agency rank.

This structure is established by the contributing agency MOUs and is a non-negotiable feature of the integrated operations model.

### 5.3 Home Agency Coordination

SACTF supervisors who need home-agency resources outside the normal Task Force structure coordinate through the inter-agency liaison process, not through direct command of non-assigned home-agency personnel. The appropriate contacts at each contributing agency are listed in the current SACTF Contacts Directory (restricted distribution).

## Part VI: Duty Assignment and Rotation

### 6.1 Assignment Duration

Personnel assigned to SACTF from contributing agencies serve tours of typically 2–3 years. Shorter or longer assignments may be approved by mutual agreement. Assignment duration is designed to provide operational continuity while preventing the insularity that comes from personnel who never return to their home agencies.

### 6.2 Availability Requirements

**Tactical Operations Division:** TOD personnel are subject to 24/7 on-call availability during designated standby periods. Standby rotation is managed by the TOD Commander.

**Investigations Division:** Investigators maintain regular business-hours availability with on-call provisions for active, time-sensitive operations.

**Intelligence Division:** The IAC maintains continuous monitoring through rotating shift coverage. Individual analysts work standard shifts plus on-call periods during elevated threat conditions.

### 6.3 Readiness and Fitness Standards

All SACTF personnel are required to maintain physical fitness standards appropriate for their assignment. TOD personnel are subject to the Tactical Fitness Standard (detailed in `03-Training-Program.md`). Investigators and analytical personnel are subject to the General Fitness Standard.

Failure to maintain required fitness standards initiates a remediation period. Personnel who cannot meet standards within the remediation period are returned to their home agency.

## Part VII: Communications and Chain of Command

### 7.1 Internal Communications

The SACTF operates a secure internal communications system for all operational communications. This system is compartmented from general agency communications networks.

**Operational Communications:** All communications related to active operations are conducted over the SACTF secure net. Personnel do not use personal phones, home-agency radios, or other non-secure means for operational communications.

**Administrative Communications:** Routine administrative matters may be handled through standard channels.

**Emergency Communications:** Each TOD operator maintains a designated emergency contact protocol for life-safety situations. See `17-Communication-Protocols.md` for full communications procedures.

### 7.2 Chain of Command Escalation

**Routine decisions:** Resolved at team/unit level.  
**Non-routine operational decisions:** Escalated to SSA.  
**Significant operational decisions:** Escalated to SAC.  
**High-sensitivity or high-risk decisions:** Escalated to Deputy Director.  
**Director-reserved decisions:** Undercover placements, WMD operations, operations involving diplomatic dimensions, operations with potential for significant public visibility.

When in doubt about which level a decision requires, escalate. The Task Force does not penalize personnel for appropriate escalation.
