## Part I: Evidence Fundamentals

### 1.1 Why Evidence Integrity Is Non-Negotiable

The purpose of evidence collection is to support the prosecution of terrorists in court. A case that produces a guilty verdict but collapses on appeal because of evidence mishandling has not accomplished its objective. It has let a dangerous individual go free while spending years and resources on an investigation.

Every decision about how evidence is handled — how it is collected, documented, stored, transferred, and presented — has consequences for prosecution. The chain of custody is not red tape. It is the backbone of every terrorism case SACTF brings.

### 1.2 Chain of Custody

The chain of custody is the documented record of who had custody of an item of evidence, from the moment of collection to its presentation in court. It must be unbroken — there must be no gap in the record when evidence was in an unaccounted location or in the possession of an undocumented person.

Any gap in the chain of custody provides defense counsel with grounds to challenge the evidence. Not all challenges succeed, but each gap is a risk that careful handling eliminates.

## Part II: Physical Evidence Collection

### 2.1 At the Scene

**First responder obligations:** The first SACTF agent to arrive at a potential crime scene has three priorities:
1. Officer safety and medical response for injured persons
2. Scene protection — establish a perimeter, prevent unauthorized access, prevent contamination
3. Notification — notify the Evidence Response Team (ERT), supervisor, and case agent

**Do not disturb:** Evidence at the scene is not touched, moved, or disturbed for any reason other than officer safety until the ERT arrives and begins documentation. If an item must be moved for safety, the movement is announced aloud, documented, and photographed before and after.

### 2.2 Documentation Before Collection

Before any item is collected, it is documented in place:
- **Photography:** Item is photographed from multiple angles with scale reference (ruler or evidence scale placard)
- **Location documentation:** The item's exact location is recorded — GPS coordinates, narrative description, diagram if necessary
- **Video walkthrough:** For significant scenes, a video walkthrough is conducted before any collection begins

### 2.3 Collection Procedures

**Personal protective equipment:** Evidence collection personnel wear gloves when handling any evidence. For scenes with potential CBRN hazard, appropriate PPE is worn before beginning collection.

**Handling:** Items are handled minimally and by edges, grips, or surfaces least likely to bear fingerprints or other trace evidence of interest.

**Packaging:** Each item is packaged separately in an appropriate container:
- Paper bags for items bearing biological trace evidence (fabric, items with blood, etc.) — paper breathes, preventing moisture buildup that destroys biological evidence
- Plastic bags for items not bearing biological trace evidence
- Rigid containers for fragile items or items requiring specific protection
- Specialized containers for electronic devices, CBRN-contaminated items

**Sealing:** Packaging is sealed with evidence tape. The sealing agent initials and dates the seal across the tape/container junction.

### 2.4 Evidence Marking

Every item receives an evidence number immediately upon collection:
- The number is written on the evidence tag (attached to the package) and the package itself
- The tag includes: evidence number, case number, date, time of collection, location of collection, description of item, collecting agent identifier
- The evidence number and description are simultaneously entered in the evidence log

### 2.5 Special Categories

**Electronic devices:** Phones, computers, tablets, drives — electronic devices require special handling to prevent evidence destruction through remote wipe capability and to preserve the evidentiary state of the device:
- Immediately place in airplane mode or a Faraday bag (blocks wireless signals) upon collection
- Do not attempt to power on a device that is off
- Do not attempt to access a powered-on device
- Transfer to digital forensics specialists as soon as practicable

**Weapons:** Weapons are rendered safe by a qualified agent before packaging. The render-safe process is documented. Weapons are packaged with barrel facing safe direction.

**Documents:** Documents are handled by edges, bagged individually, and photographed in situ before collection.

**Latent fingerprints:** Items bearing latent fingerprints are packaged before any processing. Fingerprint processing is conducted by forensic specialists in the appropriate environment.

## Part III: Chain of Custody Procedures

### 3.1 Evidence Log

Every evidence collection generates a contemporaneous evidence log. The log is a separate document (not the case file) that records every item collected with its evidence number, collection details, and collecting agent identifier.

The evidence log is the primary chain of custody document. It is maintained by the case agent and is entered into the SACTF evidence management system.

### 3.2 Transfers

Every time an item of evidence changes hands:
- The transferring agent and the receiving agent both sign the chain of custody form for that item
- The date, time, and reason for transfer are recorded
- The condition of the item at transfer is noted
- For transfers between agencies (e.g., SACTF to FIB forensics), a formal evidence transfer receipt is executed

**Never:** Evidence is never passed informally — left on someone's desk, dropped off with a receptionist, transferred without documentation. Every transfer is a documented event.

### 3.3 Evidence Storage

**SACTF evidence room:** A designated, secured, access-controlled facility. Access is logged. Persons who enter the evidence room for any purpose are recorded.

**Storage conditions:** Items are stored in conditions appropriate to their type:
- Biological evidence: refrigerated storage
- Electronic evidence: stable temperature, protected from electromagnetic interference
- Explosive-related materials: according to EOD guidance
- CBRN-related materials: according to HAZMAT handling requirements

**Periodic inventory:** The evidence room is inventoried quarterly. Any discrepancy between the logged contents and the physical contents is immediately reported and investigated.

## Part IV: Electronic Evidence

### 4.1 Digital Forensics

Electronic evidence from terrorism investigations is processed by qualified digital forensic specialists. The processing follows a forensic protocol that:
- Creates an authenticated forensic image of the original device (a complete copy that can be analyzed without disturbing the original)
- Preserves the original device in an unaltered state as the evidence item
- Analyzes the forensic image
- Documents all forensic actions taken

### 4.2 Preserved vs. Live Evidence

**Preserved evidence:** A static record — the device as it was at collection. This is the primary evidence item.

**Operational intelligence:** Data from electronic devices often has immediate operational intelligence value. The investigative team may request rapid extraction of specific data (known contacts, recent communications, location history) for operational purposes. This extraction is conducted by forensic specialists and does not affect the preserved evidence.

### 4.3 Metadata

Electronic files carry metadata — information about when the file was created, modified, and accessed; what device it was created on; in some cases, GPS data embedded in the file. Metadata is evidence. It is preserved and documented as part of forensic processing.

## Part V: Classified Evidence

### 5.1 Handling Intelligence-Derived Evidence

Evidence that is derived from classified intelligence collection — evidence whose existence or content reveals classified collection methods — requires special handling:

**Taint team:** Before classified collection is introduced into a prosecution, a taint team (typically from the Legal Division with prosecutive authority) reviews the evidence to assess what can be used openly versus what requires classified evidence procedures.

**Classified evidence procedures:** Formal procedures govern the use of classified evidence in prosecution. These procedures allow a court to review classified material in camera (privately) to assess relevance without disclosure to defense and public.

**Substitution preparation:** Where classified evidence cannot be disclosed, preparation of an unclassified summary or substitution is required. The substitution must provide the defendant with substantially the same information as the original in usable form.

### 5.2 Parallel Construction Risk

**Parallel construction** refers to the practice of developing a non-classified prosecutive basis for evidence that was initially identified through classified collection, concealing the classified origin. This practice creates serious legal and ethical risks:
- It may constitute a deception of the court
- It may violate the defendant's right to discovery
- It may create reversible error on appeal

SACTF does not engage in parallel construction. If evidence was initially identified through classified collection, the Legal Division and prosecutive authority are informed from the outset, and the classified evidence procedures are followed.

## Part VI: Evidence at Trial

### 6.1 Expert Witnesses

SACTF personnel who collected or processed evidence may be called as expert witnesses to explain their methods and findings. Expert witness testimony requires:
- Demonstrated qualification in the relevant field
- Testimony limited to the witness's actual area of expertise
- Honest, objective testimony that the jury can use to evaluate the evidence fairly

Expert witnesses for SACTF do not advocate for conviction. They explain their findings accurately and let the factfinder assess what those findings mean.

### 6.2 Chain of Custody Testimony

Every link in the chain of custody may need to be established through testimony. Chain of custody testimony must:
- Accurately reflect what the witness did and observed
- Be based on contemporaneous documentation reviewed before testimony
- Not claim memory of specific details that cannot be supported by documentation
