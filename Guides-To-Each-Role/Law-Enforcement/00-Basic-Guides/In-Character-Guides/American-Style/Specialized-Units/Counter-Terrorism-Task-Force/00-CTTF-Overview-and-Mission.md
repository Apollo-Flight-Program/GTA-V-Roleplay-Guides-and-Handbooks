## Foreword from the Director

The San Andreas Counter-Terrorism Task Force exists because no single agency can defeat terrorism alone.

The threat environment facing the State of San Andreas is multi-dimensional. Domestic extremist organizations, transnational criminal networks with terroristic capabilities, organized groups capable of mass-casualty attacks, and cyber-enabled threats to critical infrastructure all demand a response that is more agile, more informed, and more integrated than any single department can provide.

This Task Force is the answer to that demand. It is not a replacement for the LSPD, the BCSO, the San Andreas State Police, or our federal partners. It is the mechanism by which those agencies act as one when the threat demands it.

This document establishes the charter, mission, authority, and operating principles of the San Andreas Counter-Terrorism Task Force. All personnel assigned to or working in conjunction with SACTF are bound by these principles. They are not guidelines. They are the foundation on which everything else in this organization rests.

## Part I: Establishment and History

### 1.1 Origins

The San Andreas Counter-Terrorism Task Force was established in response to a documented escalation in organized terroristic activity within the State of San Andreas. A comprehensive threat assessment conducted jointly by the Los Santos Police Department, the San Andreas State Police, the Blaine County Sheriff's Office, and the Federal Investigation Bureau identified critical gaps in the existing law enforcement response framework:

- **Intelligence fragmentation:** Threat-relevant information was distributed across multiple agencies with no systematic mechanism for fusion or joint analysis.
- **Jurisdictional seams:** Terroristic operations deliberately exploited boundaries between municipal, county, state, and federal jurisdiction to complicate response and delay coordination.
- **Capability gaps:** No single agency maintained the full spectrum of capabilities required to address the complete range of identified threats — from early-stage intelligence collection through tactical interdiction and prosecution.
- **Accountability diffusion:** The absence of a single command authority with responsibility for terrorism response resulted in duplicated effort, missed connections, and unclear accountability when things went wrong.

The SACTF charter was drafted to address each of these gaps. The Task Force does not eliminate the agencies that feed it — it integrates them.

### 1.2 Legal Authority

The SACTF operates under the authority of:

- **San Andreas Revised Statutes, Title 18 (Public Safety and Security)** — establishing the legal basis for the Task Force and its multi-agency composition
- **San Andreas Executive Order on Inter-Agency Intelligence Coordination** — authorizing information sharing protocols across state and local agencies
- **Memoranda of Understanding between SACTF and contributing agencies** — defining personnel assignments, resource sharing, command relationships, and operational authorities
- **Federal partnership agreements** — governing cooperation with FIB counterterrorism programs and access to federal analytical and technical resources

No provision of this charter supersedes the constitutional rights of any person. All SACTF operations must remain within the bounds of applicable law. Personnel who believe an order is unlawful have not only the right but the obligation to refuse it.

### 1.3 Organizational History

The SACTF has operated through several distinct phases since its establishment:

**Phase I — Foundation and Integration:** Early operations focused primarily on establishing inter-agency trust, building the intelligence fusion infrastructure, and developing joint protocols. This phase was characterized by significant cultural adjustment as personnel from agencies with different traditions, priorities, and operational norms learned to work as a unified team.

**Phase II — Capability Development:** The Task Force invested in specialized training, equipment, and analytical capacity that individual member agencies could not have sustained independently. The Tactical Operations Division, the Intelligence Analysis Cell, and the Undercover Operations Program were all formalized during this phase.

**Phase III — Operational Maturity:** The current operational model, characterized by continuous intelligence-driven operations, regular joint exercises, and established inter-agency protocols, represents the Task Force's mature form. Operations in this phase have demonstrated the integration model's effectiveness across the full spectrum of threat types.

## Part II: Mission

### 2.1 Mission Statement

The San Andreas Counter-Terrorism Task Force detects, disrupts, and dismantles terrorism and terroristic threats within the State of San Andreas through the integrated application of intelligence, investigative, and tactical capabilities across all participating agencies.

### 2.2 Core Functions

**Detection:** The SACTF maintains persistent awareness of terrorism-related activity within its area of responsibility through continuous intelligence collection, analysis, and fusion. Detection is not reactive — it is active and ongoing.

**Disruption:** When a threat is identified and assessed, the SACTF acts to disrupt that threat before it can materialize. Disruption may take investigative, legal, or tactical form. It is designed to interrupt the threat without necessarily resolving the underlying network.

**Dismantlement:** The full resolution of a threat — the systematic targeting and removal of all significant nodes in a terroristic organization — is the Task Force's highest objective. Dismantlement is comprehensive, documented, and legally durable.

**Prosecution Support:** Arrested individuals and entities must be successfully prosecuted for the Task Force's work to have lasting effect. The SACTF supports prosecution at every stage — from the evidence-handling protocols that maintain chain of custody to expert testimony in specialized proceedings.

**Prevention:** The Task Force works proactively to prevent the conditions that produce terrorism, including engagement with community partners, support for counter-radicalization programs, and the identification of vulnerable individuals before they engage in violence.

### 2.3 Threat Categories

The SACTF is responsible for response to the following threat categories:

**Domestic Extremism:** Individuals or groups within San Andreas motivated by ideological, political, racial, religious, or social extremism who engage in or plan violence in furtherance of those beliefs.

**Transnational Terrorism:** Organizations with operational presence or support networks within San Andreas that plan or facilitate terrorism domestically or internationally.

**Mass-Casualty Attack Planning:** Any organization or individual in the advanced planning stages of an attack designed to produce significant civilian casualties, regardless of ideological motivation.

**Weapons of Mass Destruction (WMD) Threats:** Any credible threat involving chemical, biological, radiological, or nuclear materials within the state.

**Critical Infrastructure Threats:** Operations targeting power, water, communications, financial, transportation, or government infrastructure in ways that would substantially disrupt public safety or state function.

**Cyber-Enabled Terrorism:** Attacks on digital systems in support of or as an instrument of terroristic activity.

**Terrorism Financing:** Identification and disruption of financial networks that support terroristic organizations or activities.

## Part III: Jurisdiction and Authority

### 3.1 Primary Jurisdiction

The SACTF exercises primary jurisdiction within the State of San Andreas for terrorism-related investigations as defined by applicable statute. Primary jurisdiction means the Task Force coordinates and leads the response, with supporting agencies operating under its command authority for relevant operations.

Primary jurisdiction does not mean exclusive jurisdiction. LSPD, BCSO, SASP, and other agencies retain their independent authorities and obligations. Where another agency's active investigation involves terrorism-related elements, SACTF liaison will seek coordinated response rather than unilateral displacement.

### 3.2 Geographic Scope

**Urban Core:** The Los Santos metropolitan area, including all incorporated and unincorporated areas within LSPD and BCSO jurisdiction.

**Rural and Blaine County:** The broader San Andreas territory outside metropolitan Los Santos, coordinated primarily through BCSO with SASP support.

**Transportation Corridors:** State highways, rail lines, port facilities, and airport operations relevant to terrorism threat.

**Port of Los Santos:** Joint authority with port security and USCG-equivalent maritime operations for port-related threats.

**International Connections:** The SACTF works with federal partners on threats with international dimensions, providing local knowledge and investigative support to federal-led international operations.

### 3.3 Limitations

The SACTF does not exercise general law enforcement authority. Task Force personnel do not replace patrol officers, investigate routine crimes, or conduct operations unrelated to terrorism or terrorism-adjacent threats.

When SACTF operations produce evidence of non-terrorism criminal activity, that evidence is packaged and referred to the appropriate jurisdictional authority. The Task Force does not self-expand its mandate.

## Part IV: Organizational Principles

### 4.1 Intelligence-Led Operations

Every SACTF operation begins with intelligence. Tactical action without adequate intelligence is not counterterrorism — it is reactive enforcement that leaves the underlying threat intact. The Task Force does not conduct tactical operations until it has a sufficient intelligence picture to understand who, what, where, and — critically — what the intended operational outcome is.

### 4.2 Proportionality

SACTF authority is significant. It must be applied proportionally to the threat. The powers available to this Task Force — including surveillance authorities, undercover operations, and tactical capabilities — represent a substantial capacity to affect individuals' lives. That capacity must be matched to genuine threats, not applied broadly as a matter of convenience or preventive overcaution.

### 4.3 Legal Compliance

Compliance with applicable law is non-negotiable. There is no operational objective for which illegal methods are authorized. Evidence obtained through illegal means will not be usable in prosecution — and will, in many cases, produce consequences for personnel and the Task Force more severe than the failure to make an arrest.

### 4.4 Information Sharing

Information sharing is the Task Force's operational foundation. Personnel from partner agencies assigned to SACTF are expected to share relevant information with the Task Force intelligence cell. Personnel who withhold information from the Task Force to protect their home agency's equities are violating the purpose of the integrated model.

### 4.5 Discretion and Operational Security

The effectiveness of SACTF operations depends on discretion. Personnel do not discuss operations outside of authorized communications channels. They do not confirm or deny the details of ongoing investigations to outside parties, including members of the public, press, and — unless explicitly authorized — even other law enforcement personnel.

### 4.6 Accountability

Every SACTF operation is documented. Every decision made during an operation can be reviewed. Personnel are accountable for their individual decisions and actions. The Task Force does not shield personnel who act illegally, improperly, or without authorization. It does fully support personnel who act lawfully, appropriately, and in good faith.

## Part V: Relationship to Member Agencies

### 5.1 Contributing Agencies

The following agencies are full contributing members of the SACTF:

- **Los Santos Police Department (LSPD)** — primary municipal partner; provides detective-level investigators, patrol support, and local intelligence
- **Blaine County Sheriff's Office (BCSO)** — primary rural/county partner; provides field personnel, county intelligence, and local liaison
- **San Andreas State Police (SASP)** — state-level partner; provides highway operations, statewide database access, and aerial support
- **Federal Investigation Bureau (FIB)** — primary federal partner; provides federal prosecutive authority, national intelligence resources, and specialized capabilities

### 5.2 Supporting Agencies

The following agencies provide support to SACTF operations on a case-by-case or standing agreement basis:

- **Los Santos Fire Department (LSFD)** — HAZMAT and mass-casualty incident support
- **Emergency Medical Services** — medical support for tactical operations and mass-casualty events
- **Port of Los Santos Security** — port facility access and maritime liaison
- **Los Santos International Airport Security** — airport facility coordination for aviation-related threats
- **San Andreas Department of Corrections** — detainee handling and facility support for significant detention events

### 5.3 Command Relationships

SACTF personnel on Task Force assignment operate under SACTF command authority for Task Force operations. Their home agency retains administrative authority — personnel matters, pay, benefits — but operational direction during SACTF assignments comes from the Task Force chain of command.

This dual-authority relationship requires that SACTF leadership communicate clearly with home-agency leadership, particularly when Task Force assignments produce conflicts with home-agency responsibilities. Personnel caught between conflicting directives should immediately report the conflict to their SACTF supervisor.

## Part VI: Values and Ethics

### 6.1 Commitment to Constitutional Rights

Terrorism is a threat to the freedom and safety of the public. Counterterrorism operations that violate constitutional rights contribute to that same threat. The SACTF will not defend the public's freedom by taking it away. All operations must respect the constitutional rights of all persons — including those under investigation, including those who are guilty, and especially those who turn out to be innocent.

### 6.2 Community Trust

The public's trust is the Task Force's most important resource. Communities that trust law enforcement are more likely to report suspicious activity, more likely to cooperate with investigations, and more likely to support the legal outcomes of successful prosecutions. Operations that damage community trust — through discrimination, abuse, or overreach — do not just harm the individuals affected. They harm every future investigation that depends on community cooperation.

### 6.3 Integrity

Personnel will not fabricate, alter, or conceal evidence. They will not provide false testimony. They will not conduct unauthorized operations. They will not accept anything of value from subjects of investigation or their associates. These are not optional principles for difficult situations. They are absolute.

### 6.4 Courage

Counterterrorism work is dangerous. It requires personnel to sustain investigations in the face of personal risk, to make decisions under uncertainty, and to act decisively when action is required. SACTF values courage — the sustained, disciplined courage of professional law enforcement — not recklessness.

## Appendix A: Glossary of Key Terms

**Area of Responsibility (AOR):** The geographic and functional territory within which SACTF exercises its mission authority.

**Dismantlement:** The comprehensive targeting and removal of all significant nodes in a terroristic organization, producing a documented outcome demonstrating the organization's loss of capability.

**Disruption:** An operation designed to interrupt a specific threat or operation without necessarily resolving the broader network.

**Intelligence-Led Operations:** An operational philosophy in which investigative and tactical actions are driven by the analysis of collected intelligence rather than reactive response.

**Joint Operations:** Operations conducted by personnel from multiple agencies operating under unified command.

**Task Force:** A multi-agency unit organized around a specific mission, operating under unified command while drawing resources from contributing agencies.

**Terrorism:** The use or threatened use of violence against persons or property, in furtherance of political, ideological, social, or other non-economic objectives, with intent to coerce, intimidate, or terrorize a civilian population or government.
