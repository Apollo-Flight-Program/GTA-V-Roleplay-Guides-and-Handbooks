## Part I: Warrant Service Philosophy

### 1.1 Deliberate vs. Dynamic Approach

The two primary modes of high-risk warrant service — deliberate and dynamic — represent a spectrum, not a binary choice. The selection of approach is driven by the threat assessment and the intelligence picture, not by preference or habit.

**Deliberate entry** is the default when circumstances permit. It involves methodical approach, announcement, opportunity for voluntary compliance, and a controlled entry into the premises. It is less dangerous to officers, less dangerous to non-target occupants, less legally complex, and produces better evidence documentation.

**Dynamic entry** — high-speed, limited-warning entry designed to exploit surprise — is authorized when the intelligence picture indicates that voluntary compliance is not likely, that the threat posed by allowing the subject time to react is significant, or that the nature of the subject's weapons capability makes a deliberate approach unacceptably dangerous.

The decision between these approaches is made during planning, documented in the operational plan, and not changed without command authorization except in response to immediately observed threat indicators.

### 1.2 Pre-Service Planning Checklist

Before serving a high-risk warrant, the following checklist is completed and documented:

**Intelligence:**
- [ ] Warrant target's identity confirmed
- [ ] Target's location confirmed to current intelligence
- [ ] Physical description of target documented
- [ ] Known associates and expected occupants documented
- [ ] Weapons assessment documented
- [ ] Counter-surveillance awareness level of target documented
- [ ] Floor plan or structure layout documented or sketched from available information
- [ ] Occupants-at-risk assessment (children, elderly, disabled, hostages) documented

**Legal:**
- [ ] Warrant reviewed by Legal Division and confirmed valid
- [ ] Warrant issued within valid service timeframe
- [ ] Warrant specifically authorizes nighttime service if service is at night
- [ ] Specific items to be seized documented from warrant
- [ ] Any material not on the warrant: plain view procedures briefed

**Operational:**
- [ ] Entry approach (deliberate or dynamic) documented with justification
- [ ] All roles assigned: breach, point, cover, stack, perimeter
- [ ] Contingency for plus-one (additional subject not expected)
- [ ] Contingency for armed resistance
- [ ] Contingency for fleeing subject
- [ ] EMS staged in advance
- [ ] Evidence Response Team on standby or deployed

## Part II: Service Types

### 2.1 Search Warrant Service

**Objective:** Enter and search the specified location for the items described in the warrant.

**Requirements before entry:**
1. Knock and announce: *"POLICE, SEARCH WARRANT"* in a voice clearly audible to occupants
2. Wait a reasonable time for compliance (courts have held that 15–30 seconds is generally reasonable for a residential warrant; shorter times are justified by specific threat factors)
3. If no response and exigent circumstances are documented, proceed with entry

**During service:**
- One designated agent is the warrant agent — holds the physical warrant, responsible for directing the search to items specified
- All officers have a copy or photograph of the warrant prior to entry
- Search is limited to areas and items described in the warrant
- Plain view discoveries are documented but do not extend the search scope without additional warrant process
- No person in the premises may be detained beyond what is reasonably necessary for officer safety during the search

**Post-search:**
- Leave a copy of the warrant and inventory of items seized with a responsible person at the premises, or post it on the door
- Complete the warrant return to the issuing court

### 2.2 Arrest Warrant Service

**Objective:** Locate and arrest the person named in the warrant.

**Entry authority:** An arrest warrant authorizes entry into the residence of the person named in the warrant if officers have a reasonable belief the subject is present. An arrest warrant does not authorize entry into a third party's home — a separate search warrant for that location is required.

**Subject encountered outside the premises:** An arrest warrant authorizes arrest wherever the subject is encountered, including public places and third-party locations.

**Multiple subjects at the location:** Only persons named in the warrant are subject to arrest based on the warrant. Additional arrests require independent probable cause.

### 2.3 No-Knock Entry

A no-knock entry — entry without announcement — requires specific authorization in the warrant based on facts establishing that announcement would:
- Endanger the officer or another person
- Result in the destruction of evidence that cannot be replaced
- Allow the escape of a subject who poses immediate danger

No-knock authority is documented in both the warrant affidavit (when requested in advance) and the operational plan. Post-operation documentation confirms whether the no-knock approach was appropriate given what was actually encountered.

## Part III: Announcement Requirements

### 3.1 Standard Announcement

The standard announcement before forcible entry consists of:
1. **Identity:** "POLICE" (or "SHERIFF," "STATE POLICE," etc. as applicable to the primary serving agency)
2. **Purpose:** "SEARCH WARRANT" or "WE HAVE A WARRANT"
3. **Wait:** A reasonable period for voluntary compliance

The announcement is made loudly enough for occupants to hear it through the door. For a normal residential door at normal interior sound levels, this means projecting the voice forcefully.

**What constitutes a "reasonable wait":**
Courts have been specific that there is no specific minimum time. Relevant factors:
- The size of the structure (larger structures require more time to respond)
- Time of day (sleeping occupants require more time)
- Whether sounds indicating awareness have been heard (footsteps approaching the door = occupant is responding)
- Specific threat factors (sounds suggesting destruction of evidence or arming = shorter wait justifiable)

Document the wait time and the basis for proceeding.

### 3.2 Announcement Exceptions

Announcement may be delayed or eliminated when:
- The subject is visible and the threat is immediate (announcement would delay response to an imminent threat)
- Specific intelligence establishes that announcement will result in firearms use against officers
- The operational plan documents no-knock authority based on affidavit facts

## Part IV: Resistance and Escalation

### 4.1 Verbal Resistance

Occupants who verbally object, argue, or threaten upon entry are subject to clear, calm direction. Verbal resistance does not justify use of force. Officers continue issuing commands and maintain physical control of the entry.

### 4.2 Physical Resistance

Occupants who physically resist are subject to force proportional to their resistance, governed by SACTF use of force policy. All uses of force during warrant service are documented in the Use of Force Report.

### 4.3 Weapons Encounter

If a subject presents a weapon during warrant service:
- Officers immediately take cover
- Officers issue clear verbal commands: "POLICE — DROP THE WEAPON"
- Lethal force is authorized if the subject presents an imminent deadly threat and commands are not complied with
- If the subject complies and drops the weapon, commands continue: "ON THE GROUND — HANDS BEHIND YOUR HEAD"
- The weapon is secured by a designated officer; it does not remain in the reach of the subject or any other person

## Part V: Post-Service Procedures

### 5.1 Scene Documentation

After the premises are secured:
- Photograph all rooms as they were found, before any items are moved
- Document the entry point — condition of the door, any breaching damage
- Document the location of all persons when initially encountered
- Document any weapons found in place

### 5.2 Inventory

Every item seized is inventoried:
- Unique evidence number assigned
- Detailed description recorded
- Location within premises where found recorded
- Value estimated where applicable
- Photographs taken in place before collection

A complete inventory is provided to the warrant return filed with the court.

### 5.3 Damage Documentation

Any damage to the premises caused by entry or search is documented. SACTF does not have a program for compensating property damage caused by lawful warrant service, but documentation of damage is required to address any civil claims and to support responsible post-service assessment.
