## Part I: The Inter-Agency Reality

### 1.1 Why Coordination Is Essential

Terrorism does not respect jurisdictional boundaries. A subject who is of interest to SACTF today may have been a subject of an LSPD investigation last year, may have an active BCSO warrant, may be receiving monitoring from FIB under a different case number, and may have financial connections that the Financial Intelligence Unit at SASP is separately investigating — all without any of those agencies knowing about the others.

The fragmentation of intelligence and investigative effort across agency silos is not a minor inefficiency. It is the condition that terrorist organizations exploit. They route operations through multiple jurisdictions. They use relationships that cross agency boundaries. They specifically study where jurisdictional boundaries create gaps in coverage.

The SACTF integration model exists to close those gaps. But the model only works if the participating agencies actively use it — which requires communication, coordination, and the willingness to share information that an agency might prefer to hold as its own resource.

### 1.2 The Equity Problem

The most persistent obstacle to inter-agency coordination is the equity problem: agencies have investment in their own investigations, their own relationships, and their own prosecutive outcomes. Sharing information creates the risk that another agency will benefit from your work. In a competitive environment — competing for resources, recognition, and prosecutive credit — that risk deters sharing.

SACTF addresses the equity problem through:
- **Shared credit protocols:** Prosecutive outcomes from joint investigations are attributed to all participating agencies
- **Formal sharing agreements:** MOUs establish what will be shared and what will not be
- **Leadership expectation:** Agency leadership that assigned personnel to SACTF has communicated that information sharing is expected, not optional
- **Cultural norm:** The Task Force builds a culture in which sharing is professional conduct and withholding is a violation

No procedural fix fully resolves the equity problem. Personnel must internalize the commitment to sharing.

## Part II: Participating Agencies and Their Roles

### 2.1 Los Santos Police Department (LSPD)

**Role in SACTF:** Primary municipal law enforcement partner. LSPD provides the greatest volume of assigned investigators. LSPD also provides the primary patrol support resource — when SACTF operations require uniformed patrol presence (perimeter control, traffic management, arrest support), LSPD is the primary provider.

**LSPD contributions:**
- Local intelligence — neighborhood contacts, patrol observation, community reporting
- Urban environment expertise — LSPD officers know Los Santos in ways that matter for surveillance and operations
- Detective capacity for long-form investigations
- Patrol and uniformed support
- Access to LSPD records, databases, and informant networks

**LSPD liaison:** The LSPD Liaison Officer at SACTF (a senior LSPD detective) coordinates between the Task Force and LSPD divisions. All requests from SACTF to LSPD divisions (for support, records, patrol assistance) are coordinated through the Liaison Officer unless there is an active emergency.

**Key deconfliction requirement:** LSPD Intelligence Division maintains parallel intelligence collection on organized crime and gang activity that sometimes overlaps with terrorism intelligence. Regular deconfliction meetings between SACTF IAC and LSPD Intelligence prevent duplication and share leads.

### 2.2 Blaine County Sheriff's Office (BCSO)

**Role in SACTF:** Primary rural and county law enforcement partner. BCSO's jurisdiction covers the vast rural terrain of Blaine County — terrain that terrorist organizations have historically used for training, staging, and material storage because of its remoteness from urban law enforcement.

**BCSO contributions:**
- Rural terrain expertise — BCSO deputies know Blaine County terrain, roads, communities, and unusual activities in ways that no urban agency can replicate
- Local community relationships — rural communities report unusual activity to people they trust; BCSO has those relationships
- Remote area surveillance capability — BCSO aviation and rural patrol can cover terrain that LSPD lacks the capacity to patrol
- County detention facility coordination for large-scale detention events

**BCSO liaison:** BCSO provides a senior deputy as SACTF liaison. BCSO's cooperation is critical for any investigation with activity in Blaine County.

### 2.3 San Andreas State Police (SASP)

**Role in SACTF:** Statewide partner. SASP provides capabilities that span municipal and county jurisdictions — particularly critical for investigations where targets move across the state.

**SASP contributions:**
- Statewide database access and integration
- Highway corridor monitoring — SASP's primary operating environment is the state highway system; they provide persistent monitoring of ground transportation
- Criminal Intelligence Division (CID) — SASP's statewide intelligence function has visibility into threats across the state
- Aviation support — SASP aviation provides surveillance and rapid response capability across the state
- SASP SWAT — additional tactical capacity for major operations

**Statewide operations coordination:** For operations that cross LSPD/BCSO boundaries, SASP provides the statewide coordination framework. SACTF works through the SASP CID for state-level intelligence integration.

### 2.4 Federal Investigation Bureau (FIB)

**Role in SACTF:** Federal partner. The FIB provides federal prosecutive authority, national-level intelligence, and specialized capabilities that state and local agencies cannot maintain independently.

**FIB contributions:**
- Federal jurisdiction — certain terrorism-related charges can only be brought federally; FIB provides the prosecutive path for federal charges
- National intelligence access — FIB has access to national terrorism databases, intelligence products, and assessments that no state agency can access independently
- Specialized laboratories — FIB forensic laboratories have capabilities for terrorism-related evidence analysis that exceed what state forensic labs provide
- National fugitive authority — FIB has authority to pursue terrorism-related fugitives across state lines and internationally
- International liaison — for threats with international dimensions, FIB coordinates with international law enforcement partners

**FIB-SACTF relationship:** The FIB maintains a joint duty assignment at SACTF — FIB agents assigned to the Fusion Cell work embedded within the Task Force. This direct assignment is supplemented by a formal liaison relationship with the FIB field office.

**Jurisdictional coordination:** The FIB has the ability to assert federal jurisdiction over state terrorism investigations. The relationship between SACTF and FIB is cooperative, not competitive — jurisdiction is coordinated based on where charges can be brought most effectively, not on agency pride.

## Part III: Information Sharing Protocols

### 3.1 The Duty to Share

Personnel assigned to SACTF have an affirmative duty to share threat-relevant information with the Task Force intelligence system. This is not a passive obligation — it requires active recognition of information that is relevant and active reporting of it.

**What must be shared:**
- Any information about terrorism-related activity or individuals received through any channel
- Leads developed through non-SACTF assignments that are terrorism-relevant
- Information from home-agency intelligence functions that bears on known SACTF subjects or investigations
- Any information suggesting a target is aware of law enforcement interest

**How to share:** Through the SACTF intelligence reporting system. The system provides a simple reporting interface; detailed reports are provided to the IAC.

### 3.2 Classified Information Handling

**Federal classified material:** SACTF personnel with federal clearances may receive classified intelligence products from FIB and federal partners. That material is:
- Not disclosed to personnel without appropriate clearance
- Not introduced into unclassified systems
- Handled under federal classified information handling standards
- Used to inform Task Force operations but protected from discovery in open proceedings unless specifically authorized

**State sensitive material:** SACTF-produced intelligence products are classified at the SACTF standard. Partner agencies that receive SACTF products may not further distribute them without SACTF authorization.

### 3.3 Deconfliction

**Active case deconfliction:** Before initiating surveillance, contact with a potential source, or other active investigative steps involving a specific subject, SACTF agents check the deconfliction database for active investigations involving that subject. Making investigative contact with a subject who is under active surveillance by another agency can compromise that agency's operation.

**Deconfliction database:** The SACTF participates in the state and regional deconfliction systems that allow agencies to register subjects and check for conflicts before taking action.

**Conflict resolution:** When deconfliction identifies that two agencies are actively working the same subject:
- Both agencies are notified through the deconfliction system
- A coordination meeting is convened within 24 hours
- Agencies agree on a coordination approach: joint operation, lead agency designation, or separated investigative threads
- All subsequent activity is coordinated

## Part IV: Joint Operations

### 4.1 Joint Operational Planning

Operations involving personnel from multiple agencies operating under unified SACTF command are joint operations. Planning for joint operations includes:

- **Unified briefing:** All participating personnel attend the same operational briefing. There are no separate briefs for LSPD, BCSO, or FIB components — the plan is briefed once, to all elements, at the same time.
- **Unified ROE:** All participating elements operate under the same Rules of Engagement. SACTF ROE governs SACTF operations; there is no agency-specific ROE during joint operations.
- **Unified command:** For the duration of the joint operation, all elements report to the SACTF On-Scene Commander.
- **Unified communications:** All elements operate on the same communications plan.

### 4.2 Support Requests

When SACTF operations require support from partner agencies:

**Standard support requests:** Submitted through the applicable agency liaison at least 24 hours in advance where possible. Support requests specify: the nature of support needed, the date/time, the operational context (without unnecessary classified detail), and any equipment or qualification requirements.

**Urgent support requests:** When the operational timeline does not permit 24-hour advance notice, the SACTF Operations section contacts the agency directly through established emergency contact numbers. Urgent requests are followed with standard documentation.

**Support integration:** Support personnel from other agencies who are assigned to a SACTF operation receive an appropriate briefing on their specific role and the communications plan. They do not receive full operational briefing unless their role requires it.

### 4.3 Post-Operation Reporting

After joint operations, SACTF provides:
- Operational outcome summary to all participating agencies
- Classified intelligence products (as appropriate) to relevant agency intelligence functions
- Case update to the prosecutive authority
- Documentation of each agency's contribution for purposes of credit attribution

## Part V: External Coordination

### 5.1 Prosecutive Authority

**District Attorney Coordination:** The San Andreas District Attorney's Office (and individual Deputy DAs assigned to terrorism matters) are SACTF's primary prosecutive partners for state charges. Coordination includes:
- Regular case update meetings for active investigations approaching prosecution-ready status
- Warrant review support — DAs review complex warrant applications for legal sufficiency before filing
- Prosecutive strategy development — joint discussion of how to charge defendants to maximize accountability
- Trial preparation support — SACTF provides investigative assistance through the trial process

**Federal Prosecution Coordination:** FIB facilitates coordination with federal prosecutors for matters involving federal charges. SACTF case agents coordinate directly with the applicable federal prosecutor for cases in their portfolio.

### 5.2 Public Health Coordination

For biological threats and incidents with public health dimensions, SACTF coordinates with the San Andreas Department of Public Health. The Public Health liaison relationship is maintained by the WMD Squad supervisor. Key coordination areas:
- Information sharing about potential biological incidents
- Joint response to confirmed biological events
- Epidemiological investigation support

### 5.3 Critical Infrastructure Operators

SACTF maintains relationships with operators of critical infrastructure: power utilities, water systems, communications providers, financial institutions, and transportation operators. These relationships:
- Provide SACTF with contact points for rapid notification if a threat to their infrastructure is identified
- Allow infrastructure operators to report suspicious activity to SACTF directly
- Support joint infrastructure protection planning
