## Part I: Communications Framework

### 1.1 Overview

Effective communications are the nervous system of SACTF operations. An operation with perfect intelligence and perfect tactics can fail because communications broke down at a critical moment. Communications discipline — knowing what to say, when to say it, and how to say it — is as important as any tactical skill.

The SACTF communications architecture is designed with three priorities:
- **Security:** Operational communications must be protected from interception by targets or hostile actors
- **Reliability:** Communications must be available when needed, including in challenging environments (underground, in vehicles, in structures with interference)
- **Clarity:** Communications must convey exactly what is intended, without ambiguity, under conditions of stress and time pressure

### 1.2 Communications Security Principles

**Operational matters on secure channels only.** Specifics of investigations, targets, surveillance activities, operational plans, and source identities are never discussed on non-secure channels.

**Assume unsecured channels are monitored.** Any communication on standard law enforcement radio frequencies, standard telephone, or commercial electronic communications is assumed to be potentially intercepted. Treat all such communications accordingly.

**Personal devices for personal matters.** Personal mobile phones are not used for operational communications.

## Part II: Radio Communications

### 2.1 SACTF Radio Network

The SACTF operates on an encrypted trunked radio network segregated from all contributing agency networks. All personnel are issued SACTF radios upon assignment. These radios:
- Must be on and charged at all times during duty hours
- Must not be left unattended in non-secure locations
- Are returned to SACTF administrative division upon separation

**Network structure:**
| Channel | Designation | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | SACTF-MAIN | Routine intra-Task Force communications |
| 2 | SACTF-TACT | Active tactical operations net |
| 3 | SACTF-INTEL | Intelligence cell communications |
| 4 | SACTF-CMD | Command net — supervisors and above |
| 5 | SACTF-COORD | Inter-agency coordination |
| 6 | SACTF-SUPPORT | Logistics, admin, support elements |
| 9 | SACTF-EMERGENCY | Emergency-use only |

Personnel are assigned a primary channel based on their role. Monitor capability allows supervisors to monitor all channels simultaneously.

### 2.2 Radio Procedure Standards

**Initiating a transmission:**
- Wait for the channel to be clear
- Key the mic, pause briefly (allow encryption to sync), then transmit
- Begin with your call sign, then the call sign of the station you are reaching: *"SACTF-6, this is SACTF-14"*
- Wait for acknowledgment before transmitting the message

**Message structure:**
- Transmit in clear, concise sentences
- Deliver one piece of information per transmission when possible
- Avoid unnecessary preamble ("uh," "I was just wondering")
- End with "over" when expecting a response; "out" when the exchange is complete

**Acknowledgment codes:**
| Code | Meaning |
|---|---|
| AFFIRM / AFFIRMATIVE | Yes / Confirmed |
| NEGATIVE | No / Not confirmed |
| ROGER | Message received and understood |
| COPY | Received; no response needed |
| STAND BY | Wait for further communication |
| SAY AGAIN | Repeat your last transmission |
| BREAK | Pause; new topic or speaker follows |
| WILCO | Will comply with instruction |
| UNABLE | Cannot comply; reason to follow |
| UNKNOWN | Information not available to me |

### 2.3 Priority Traffic

**PRIORITY:** Prefix for time-sensitive operational information that should clear routine channel traffic. Personnel on the channel acknowledge and hold for priority traffic completion.

**BREAK BREAK BREAK:** Emergency interrupt. Clears all traffic on the channel immediately. Used only for safety emergencies or critical operational information. After any "BREAK BREAK BREAK" transmission, all other personnel hold and acknowledge.

**EMERGENCY format:**
*"BREAK BREAK BREAK — [CALL SIGN] — MAN DOWN [LOCATION] — [STATUS IF KNOWN]"*
*"BREAK BREAK BREAK — [CALL SIGN] — COMPROMISE — [ELEMENT] — [LOCATION]"*

### 2.4 Radio Discipline Failures

Personnel who transmit on the wrong channel, fail to use proper procedure, or transmit unnecessarily during active tactical operations are creating operational risk. Supervisors correct radio discipline failures immediately and document persistent violations.

## Part III: Operational Call Signs

### 3.1 Call Sign System

All SACTF operations use a call sign system. Real names are never used on operational radio nets.

**Permanent positions:**
| Call Sign | Position |
|---|---|
| SACTF-1 | Director |
| SACTF-2 | Deputy Director — Operations |
| SACTF-3 | Deputy Director — Intelligence |
| SACTF-4 | TOD Commander |
| SACTF-5 | Investigations Division Commander |
| SACTF-6 | Intelligence Division Commander |
| SACTF-7 | Surveillance Unit Supervisor |
| SACTF-8 | CNU Lead Negotiator |

**Operational call signs:** For specific operations, all participating elements are assigned operational call signs in the operational plan. These are typically phonetic designators (ALPHA, BRAVO, CHARLIE, etc.) or tactical color designations (RED TEAM, BLUE TEAM, etc.).

### 3.2 Target Call Signs

Subjects of investigation are referred to by code names, not real names, on operational communications. Code names are assigned in the case file and used consistently across the investigation.

When a target is not yet identified, a numeric or alpha designator is assigned: TARGET-1, CONTACT-ALPHA, UNKNOWN-MIKE, etc.

## Part IV: Code Word System

### 4.1 Operational Code Words

Each operation is assigned a unique operational code word from the SACTF code word registry. The code word:
- Identifies all communications as belonging to this operation
- Is used on inter-agency channels to protect the operation name
- Is referenced in all documentation relating to the operation

**Example:** Operation IRONWOOD is the active case. On inter-agency coordination channels: *"SACTF-COORD, SACTF-5 with update on IRONWOOD, switch to secure channel."*

### 4.2 Status Code Words

The following code words have standing meanings in all SACTF communications:

| Code Word | Meaning | Response Required |
|---|---|---|
| EXECUTE | Initiate the designated action | Acknowledgment by all elements |
| HOLD | Pause all movement; await further instruction | Acknowledgment by all elements |
| ABORT | Terminate and withdraw | Immediate acknowledgment; execute withdrawal plan |
| SECURE | Objective achieved; location under control | Acknowledgment; transition to post-op procedures |
| COMPROMISE | Operational security potentially or actually breached | Immediate supervisor notification; await instruction |
| PILGRIM | Unauthorized civilian is observing operation | Acknowledge; do not identify as law enforcement without instruction |
| SHEPHERD | Medical emergency; request extraction | Immediate acknowledgment; activate medical plan |
| SUNDOWN | Operation terminated for the day; all elements stand down | Acknowledgment; proceed to debriefing location |
| THUNDER | Weapons discharge has occurred | Immediate acknowledgment; initiate UOF reporting |
| OPEN WATER | Subject has escaped the perimeter | Immediate broadcast of subject description and direction; await instruction |

### 4.3 Intelligence-Specific Codes

For intelligence communications on the SACTF-INTEL channel:

| Code | Meaning |
|---|---|
| EYES ON | Surveillance has visual on subject |
| LOST | Surveillance has lost visual on subject |
| CLEAN | Subject is not displaying counter-surveillance behavior |
| DIRTY | Subject is actively conducting counter-surveillance |
| MEETING | Subject is meeting with an unidentified contact |
| MOBILE | Subject is moving; direction to follow |
| STATIC | Subject has stopped and is at a fixed location |

## Part V: Secure Telephone and Digital Communications

### 5.1 Secure Telephone

SACTF operations facilities are equipped with secure telephone terminals for calls containing classified operational information. Standard telephone — including government-issued mobile phones — is not secure and must not carry classified operational information.

**When to use secure telephone:**
- Discussions of undercover operation status
- Intelligence reporting with classified content
- Inter-agency coordination involving classified sources
- Any communication with FIB on operational intelligence matters

### 5.2 Secure Messaging

The SACTF internal messaging system (on the secure network) may be used for written operational communications. All messages on the system are retained as records and may be reviewed in oversight proceedings.

**Not permitted on secure messaging:** Personal communications, jokes, non-operational content. The secure messaging system is an operational tool, not a general communication platform.

### 5.3 Email and Standard Digital Communications

Standard government email is not secure for operational information. Operational information is not sent by standard email. Administrative information, non-sensitive coordination, and general correspondence may use standard channels.

## Part VI: Communications Plans for Specific Operations

### 6.1 Warrant Execution Communications Plan

Every tactical warrant execution includes a written communications plan as part of the OPORD. The plan specifies:
- Channel assignments for all elements
- Call sign assignments
- Frequency for scheduled check-ins during the operation
- Specific transmissions required at key operational moments (breach, SECURE, etc.)
- Missing check-in protocol

### 6.2 Extended Surveillance Communications

For surveillance operations lasting multiple days:
- Daily encrypted check-in with the Intelligence Cell at a scheduled time
- Secure messaging for routine status reports
- Emergency channel available at all times
- Handler contact protocol for UO if undercover operations are concurrent

### 6.3 Crisis Incident Communications

For crisis/hostage situations, a dedicated communications plan is established at scene. See `12-Hostage-and-Crisis-Negotiation.md` for the full crisis communications protocol.

## Part VII: Communications Failure Contingencies

### 7.1 Primary Communications Failure

If primary SACTF radio communications fail during an operation:
- All elements move to the designated backup channel
- If backup channel is also unavailable, use the hardened backup (typically a dedicated frequency with older encryption standard)

### 7.2 Total Communications Failure

If all electronic communications fail during a tactical operation:
- All elements hold their last position
- Team leaders attempt face-to-face contact with adjacent elements
- Pre-briefed withdrawal plan is executed after a specified hold period

Pre-briefed withdrawal instructions are included in every operational plan. Personnel know the withdrawal plan by memory before they deploy — they do not rely on communications to execute a withdrawal.

### 7.3 Lost Undercover Contact

See `07-Undercover-Operations.md` for the complete protocol for lost contact with an undercover officer.
