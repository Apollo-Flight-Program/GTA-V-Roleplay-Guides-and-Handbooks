# SACTF In-Character Glossary

## San Andreas Counter-Terrorism Task Force — Operational Terminology Reference

*This glossary documents terminology used by SACTF personnel in in-character professional communication. These are the words, phrases, and abbreviations that SACTF operators, investigators, and analysts use to describe what they do.*

---

## A

**AAR** — After-Action Review. Structured post-operation review process.

**Ability-Opportunity-Intent** — The three-factor test for assessing imminent threat justifying use of deadly force. All three must be present.

**Affiant** — The officer who swears to the facts in a warrant affidavit.

**Affidavit** — A sworn written statement of facts submitted to a court in support of a warrant application.

**AOR** — Area of Responsibility. The geographic and functional territory assigned to SACTF.

**Asset** — An intelligence source or capability providing collection value. Used broadly; a "human asset" is an informant or undercover.

**Assaulter** — A TOD operator who is part of the entry element in a tactical operation.

**Attribution** — Identification of the perpetrator of an attack or criminal act.

---

## B

**BOLO** — Be On the Lookout. Alert distributed to law enforcement personnel describing a wanted person or vehicle.

**Breach** — Defeat of an entry obstacle (door, lock, barrier) to enable entry. Also the operator designated to perform this function.

**Broad Daylight Rule** — The principle that an operation that would look bad if reported on the front page of a newspaper should be reconsidered. Not an official standard; a colloquialism for the basic legitimacy test.

**Building Clearance** — The systematic search of all spaces in a structure to establish that no threat remains uncontrolled.

**Button-Up Position** — The position beside a doorway, outside the fatal funnel, from which an operator prepares to enter.

---

## C

**Case Agent** — The lead investigator responsible for an investigation case file.

**Case File** — The complete documentation of an investigation, maintained in the SACTF case management system.

**CD** — Cooperating Defendant. An individual with pending charges who cooperates with investigations in exchange for prosecutive consideration.

**CI** — Confidential Informant. A registered human source providing intelligence.

**CIRT** — Critical Incident Response Team. SACTF's mass-casualty response capability.

**CBRN** — Chemical, Biological, Radiological, Nuclear. Category of weapons capable of mass casualties.

**Chain of Custody** — The documented record of all persons who held custody of evidence from collection to presentation in court.

**CNU** — Crisis Negotiation Unit. SACTF's crisis negotiation capability, part of TOD.

**Cold Hit** — An unexpected identification of a suspect or subject through database comparison (fingerprint, DNA, financial records).

**Cold Zone** — In CBRN or hazardous incident response, the uncontaminated safe area where command and support functions operate.

**Compromise** — A situation where operational security has been breached; the cover of an operation, surveillance, or undercover officer has been discovered by the target.

**Confidential Informant (CI)** — See CI.

**Contact** — In surveillance, an unidentified individual with whom the target meets or communicates. Also: any interaction between an officer and a member of the public.

**Contact Report** — The contemporaneous documentation of a meeting with a confidential informant.

**Controlled Delivery** — A law enforcement technique in which contraband is allowed to complete its delivery under monitoring, to identify the recipient.

**Cordon** — A perimeter of personnel or barriers preventing movement in or out of a designated area.

**Counter-Surveillance (CS)** — Activity by a subject intended to detect surveillance coverage.

**Cover** — The assumed identity and legend of an undercover officer. Also: physical concealment position during tactical operations.

**Cover for Action** — The explanation an officer uses for their presence in a location during a surveillance or undercover operation.

**Crime Scene Perimeter** — The controlled area established to protect a crime scene from contamination or interference.

**CQC** — Close-Quarters Combat. Combat in confined spaces at short range.

---

## D

**Dead-drop** — An indirect communication method in which materials or information are left in a fixed location for another party to collect without direct contact.

**Deconfliction** — The process of checking whether another agency has an active investigation involving the same subject, before taking action that could compromise that investigation.

**Deliberate Entry** — A methodical, controlled entry with announcement, as opposed to a dynamic forced entry.

**Dirty Bomb** — A Radiological Dispersal Device (RDD); a conventional explosive combined with radiological material.

**Dismantlement** — The comprehensive neutralization of an entire threat network, producing a documented result showing the network's loss of capability.

**Disruption** — An operation that interrupts a specific threat without necessarily resolving the broader network.

**DNR** — Dialed Number Recorder / Do Not Record. Context-dependent. In communications intercept, a DNR records numbers dialed.

**Drop Phone** — A prepaid mobile phone purchased anonymously, used by targets to avoid call-record monitoring. Also called a "burner."

**Dynamic Entry** — High-speed, limited-warning forced entry designed to exploit surprise.

---

## E

**Element** — A tactical subunit, such as the assault element, perimeter element, or support element.

**Entrapment** — The unlawful inducement of a person to commit a crime they would not otherwise have committed. A complete defense to criminal prosecution.

**EOD** — Explosive Ordnance Disposal. Specialists trained to identify, neutralize, and document explosive devices.

**ERT** — Evidence Response Team. Specialists trained in crime scene processing under various conditions.

**Exfil / Exfiltration** — The covert withdrawal of personnel from a location, particularly in undercover contexts.

**Exigent Circumstances** — Urgent circumstances that legally permit certain law enforcement actions without prior judicial authorization.

**Eye** — The surveillance operator who currently has direct visual contact on the subject.

---

## F

**Faraday Bag** — A signal-blocking container that prevents electronic devices from transmitting or receiving signals; used to preserve electronic evidence.

**Fatal Funnel** — The doorway and immediate threshold area, which is maximally exposed to fire from inside the room. Operators move through the fatal funnel quickly rather than pausing in it.

**FIB** — Federal Investigation Bureau. The primary federal law enforcement and intelligence partner in the SACTF framework.

**FININT** — Financial Intelligence. Intelligence derived from analysis of financial records and transactions.

**Forced Entry** — Entry into a location using mechanical force to defeat locks, doors, or barriers.

**Fruit of the Poisonous Tree** — Evidence derived from an unlawful search or seizure that is also subject to exclusion, even if collected through otherwise lawful means.

---

## G

**Ghost Character** — Colloquial term for a secondary identity or cover used by intelligence professionals. In the RP context, a fictional character created for intelligence infiltration.

**Ground Truth** — Verified, firsthand information about conditions on the ground, as opposed to analytical assessments.

---

## H

**Handler** — The officer responsible for managing a confidential informant or undercover officer relationship.

**Hard Target** — A well-protected, fortified, or security-conscious target that presents significant resistance to approach or penetration.

**HAZMAT** — Hazardous Materials. Dangerous materials requiring specialized handling.

**Hot Zone** — In CBRN or hazardous incident response, the contaminated area. Only properly equipped specialists operate in the hot zone.

**HRT** — Hostage Rescue Team. A tactical unit trained for high-risk hostage rescue.

**HUMINT** — Human Intelligence. Intelligence derived from human sources.

---

## I

**IAC** — Intelligence Analysis Cell. The SACTF unit responsible for analysis and production of intelligence products.

**IC** — Intelligence Community. The collective of government intelligence agencies.

**IED** — Improvised Explosive Device. Homemade bomb using non-military components.

**Imminent Threat** — A threat that presents immediate danger of death or serious bodily harm.

**Informant** — See CI.

**Inner Perimeter** — The close-in perimeter around a target structure, preventing subject escape.

**In-Extremis** — Emergency action taken when normal procedures cannot be followed due to immediate threat.

**Intelligence Product** — A finished analytical document — assessment, summary, target package — produced by the IAC.

---

## J

**JTF** — Joint Task Force. A multi-agency operational group.

**Judicial Authorization** — Court-issued warrant or order authorizing a law enforcement action.

---

## K

**K-9** — Police working dog. SACTF K-9 teams are used for tracking, explosive detection, and subject location.

**KIA** — Killed in Action. Used in TOD after-action reporting.

**KIO** — Key Intelligence Objective. Specific intelligence target or question the operation is designed to answer.

---

## L

**Lead Agency** — The agency with primary jurisdictional responsibility for a given investigation or operation.

**Legend** — The complete backstory and documentation of an undercover officer's assumed identity.

**Less-Lethal** — Force options intended to incapacitate without causing death, such as Tasers, OC spray, and impact munitions.

**Link Analysis** — Analytical method mapping relationships, associations, and connections among persons, organizations, locations, and events.

**LOCS** — Lines of Communication. Routes used by an organization to move personnel, material, and information.

---

## M

**Material Support** — The legal standard for terrorism-related charges involving provision of resources to designated organizations or for terroristic acts.

**MEDEVAC** — Medical Evacuation. Extraction of an injured person for medical treatment.

**Minimization** — Legal requirement to avoid recording communications not relevant to an authorized intercept; applied in real time during monitored calls.

**Mobile Surveillance** — Surveillance that follows a moving subject, as opposed to static observation of a fixed location.

**MOU** — Memorandum of Understanding. The formal agreement governing relationships between SACTF and contributing agencies.

---

## N

**Need to Know** — The principle that access to sensitive information is limited to those who require it for their specific duties.

**No-Knock Entry** — Entry without announcement, authorized by specific warrant when announcement would create danger or allow evidence destruction.

**Node** — In network analysis, an individual, location, or organization that connects other elements of a network.

---

## O

**Objective** — The specific target or goal of an operation.

**OP** — Observation Post. A fixed position from which surveillance of a target location is conducted.

**OPORD** — Operations Order. The formal briefing document for an operation.

**OPSEC** — Operational Security. Practices designed to protect the confidentiality of operations.

**OSINT** — Open Source Intelligence. Intelligence derived from publicly available information.

**Outer Perimeter** — The larger perimeter around an operation, preventing civilian access and providing a safety buffer.

**Over-the-Shoulder** — Surveillance technique in which the operative follows slightly behind and to the side of the subject.

---

## P

**Package** — A complete intelligence or evidence compilation on a subject. "Building a package" means assembling a target package or evidence package.

**Pattern of Life** — The observable routines, habits, and schedule of a surveillance target, developed through sustained observation.

**PIR** — Priority Intelligence Requirement. A specific question that intelligence collection is directed to answer.

**Plain View** — The legal doctrine allowing seizure of evidence clearly visible to an officer who is lawfully present in a location.

**Point Man / Operator** — The first officer to enter a room during clearing operations.

**Probable Cause** — The standard of evidence required for arrest or search warrants; facts sufficient to cause a reasonable person to believe a crime has been committed or evidence will be found.

**Prosecution Package** — The complete compiled documentation of an investigation prepared for transmittal to prosecutive authorities.

---

## R

**Radiological Dispersal Device (RDD)** — See Dirty Bomb.

**Render Safe** — EOD action to neutralize an explosive device.

**ROE** — Rules of Engagement. The specific conditions under which force may be used in a given operation.

---

## S

**SAC** — Special Agent in Charge. SACTF grade equivalent to captain/lieutenant.

**SBIED** — Suicide-Borne IED. Explosive device worn on the person of a person intending to detonate it.

**Secondary Device** — An explosive or other device placed specifically to target first responders who arrive at the scene of an initial attack.

**Sensitive Compartmented Information (SCI)** — Classified information subject to special handling requirements and limited to specific clearance compartments.

**SIGINT** — Signals Intelligence. Intelligence derived from electronic communications intercept.

**Soft Target** — A target with limited security measures; high-value, low-protection. Shopping centers, public transit, public events.

**Source** — A human intelligence provider; a CI, cooperating defendant, or contact who provides information.

**SSA** — Supervisory Special Agent. SACTF grade equivalent to sergeant/detective sergeant.

**Stack** — The single-file tactical formation used for approach and entry. Also: the act of forming up in this formation.

**Standoff Distance** — The minimum safe distance from a suspected explosive device.

**Surveillance Detection Route (SDR)** — A route specifically designed to expose anyone following the subject; used by surveillance-conscious subjects.

---

## T

**Target Package** — A comprehensive intelligence document on a specific target.

**TECHINT** — Technical Intelligence. Intelligence derived from analysis of technical equipment and materials.

**Technical Collection** — Electronic surveillance, intercept, tracking, or monitoring authorized by court order.

**Tipping** — Warning a target about law enforcement activity; a serious crime if done by law enforcement personnel.

**TOD** — Tactical Operations Division.

**TOU** — Technical Operations Unit.

**Trap and Trace** — A court-authorized method of capturing outgoing and incoming telephone number information without capturing content.

**Trigger** — The event or observation that initiates a specific operational action. "On trigger" means waiting for a specific event to occur.

---

## U

**UO** — Undercover Officer.

**UOP** — Undercover Operations Program.

**Unified Command** — A command structure in which all participating agencies are under a single operational commander for the duration of a joint operation.

---

## V

**VBIED** — Vehicle-Borne IED. Explosive device placed in or constructed as part of a vehicle.

**VIP** — Very Important Person. Subject requiring protective detail or elevated caution.

---

## W

**Warm Zone** — In CBRN response, the buffer area between the hot zone and cold zone where decontamination occurs.

**Warrant Agent** — The officer who holds and is responsible for the warrant during a warrant service operation.

**WMD** — Weapons of Mass Destruction. CBRN materials capable of causing mass casualties.

---

*This glossary is maintained by the SACTF Intelligence Division. Additions and corrections are submitted to the IAC Cell Chief.*
