# State Police Handbook — San Andreas State Police

The San Andreas State Police is the premier law enforcement agency operating under the authority of the State of San Andreas. Unlike municipal police departments or county sheriff's offices, the SASP holds jurisdiction across the entire state — every highway, every county, every unincorporated stretch of desert, mountain, and coastline falls under our authority. Where local agencies are bound by city limits and county lines, the State Police answers to no such boundary.

The agency was established by the San Andreas Legislature to provide a professional, centralized law enforcement body capable of enforcing state law, patrolling the state highway system, conducting major criminal investigations, and providing executive protection to the Governor and other state officials. The SASP operates out of barracks stationed across San Andreas, from the urban corridors of Los Santos to the rural expanses of Blaine County.

This handbook is issued to every sworn Trooper upon completion of the State Police Academy. It defines who you are, what you represent, and how you are expected to conduct yourself. The San Andreas State Police has a reputation for discipline, professionalism, and excellence that was earned over decades — and you are now responsible for upholding it. The campaign hat, the pressed uniform, the marked cruiser — these are symbols that carry weight with the public and with other agencies. Do not diminish them.

---

## Mission Statement

The mission of the San Andreas State Police is to protect and serve the people of the State of San Andreas by enforcing state law, ensuring the safe and efficient flow of traffic on all state roadways, conducting criminal investigations of statewide significance, and providing professional law enforcement services that supplement and support local agencies throughout San Andreas.

**Core Principles:**

- **Statewide Authority** — We go where the mission demands. City limits and county lines do not limit our jurisdiction or our responsibility.
- **Highway Safety** — The state highway system is our primary patrol responsibility. Every mile of freeway, highway, and state route in San Andreas is our beat.
- **Professional Excellence** — The State Police sets the standard. We are expected to be the most disciplined, best-trained, and most professional officers in the state.
- **Impartiality** — We enforce the law without favoritism, prejudice, or political influence. A Senator doing 95 on the freeway gets the same treatment as anyone else.
- **Support to Local Agencies** — We work alongside the LSPD, BCSO, and other departments — not above them. Cooperation, not competition, defines inter-agency relationships.

## Rank Structure

The San Andreas State Police follows a paramilitary rank structure modeled on traditional state police agencies. The chain of command is absolute. Every Trooper must know exactly who they report to and who reports to them.

| Rank | Grade | Authority & Responsibilities |
|---|---|---|
| **Trooper** | T-1 | Entry-level sworn officer. Troopers conduct highway patrol, respond to calls for service on state roadways, enforce traffic law, and assist local agencies as directed. The foundation of the agency. |
| **Senior Trooper** | T-2 | Experienced Trooper with a minimum of two years of service. Eligible for specialized assignments including accident reconstruction, commercial vehicle enforcement, and field training. May mentor junior Troopers. |
| **Corporal** | T-3 | First-line team leader. Corporals supervise small patrol teams, assist Sergeants with shift coordination, and serve as acting shift supervisor when no Sergeant is available. Expected to lead by example in the field. |
| **Sergeant** | S-1 | Primary field supervisor. Sergeants manage daily patrol operations at the barracks level, conduct shift briefings, approve reports, authorize tactical decisions in the field, and handle personnel matters within their shift. |
| **Lieutenant** | S-2 | Barracks commander or unit commander. Lieutenants oversee the operations of an entire barracks or specialized unit, manage staffing, and coordinate with other barracks and outside agencies. Reports to the Captain. |
| **Captain** | C-1 | District commander responsible for multiple barracks and the geographic area they serve. Captains set operational priorities for their district, manage budgets, and represent the agency in regional inter-agency coordination. |
| **Major** | C-2 | Bureau commander. Majors oversee entire operational bureaus such as the Highway Patrol Bureau, the Criminal Investigation Bureau, or the Executive Protection Bureau. Responsible for agency-wide policy within their bureau. |
| **Lieutenant Colonel** | C-3 | Deputy commander of the agency. The Lieutenant Colonel oversees day-to-day operations across all bureaus, implements the Colonel's directives, and assumes command of the agency in the Colonel's absence. |
| **Colonel / Superintendent** | C-4 | The highest-ranking officer in the San Andreas State Police. The Colonel sets agency policy, represents the SASP to the Governor's Office and the State Legislature, and bears ultimate responsibility for everything the agency does. Appointed by the Governor of San Andreas. |

Promotions are earned through competitive examination, time in grade, demonstrated leadership, and review by the promotion board. The State Police does not promote on popularity or politics. You earn your rank, or you do not advance.

## Statewide Jurisdiction

The SASP holds concurrent jurisdiction across the entire State of San Andreas. This means a Trooper has full law enforcement authority in Los Santos, Blaine County, and every unincorporated area in between. There is no corner of this state where your badge does not carry weight.

**Jurisdictional relationships with local agencies:**

| Agency | Primary Jurisdiction | SASP Relationship |
|---|---|---|
| **Los Santos Police Department (LSPD)** | City of Los Santos | SASP operates on state highways within city limits. Assists LSPD on request. Does not conduct routine patrol within city streets unless directed. |
| **Blaine County Sheriff's Office (BCSO)** | Blaine County (incorporated and unincorporated) | SASP shares jurisdiction in Blaine County. Primary authority on state highways; defers to BCSO on county roads and local calls unless assistance is requested. |
| **San Andreas Park Rangers** | State parks and wilderness areas | SASP provides backup and investigative support. Park Rangers handle routine park enforcement. |

**When the State Police takes over:**

- Any crime occurring on a state highway or state property
- Multi-jurisdictional crimes that cross city or county lines
- Cases referred by local agencies that lack the resources or manpower to investigate
- Governor's directive or executive order assigning SASP to a specific operation
- Organized crime and major narcotics operations with statewide reach
- Officer-involved shootings requiring an independent outside investigation

When operating within the City of Los Santos or Blaine County, Troopers are expected to coordinate with local agencies — not steamroll them. Notify LSPD or BCSO dispatch when conducting enforcement in their jurisdiction. Professional courtesy is not optional.

## Highway Patrol Duties

Highway patrol is the backbone of the San Andreas State Police. The state highway system is our primary beat, and every Trooper — regardless of specialty or assignment — must be proficient in highway enforcement.

**Primary highways and routes under SASP authority:**

| Highway | Route | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Los Santos Freeway** | Interstate corridor | The primary freeway system running through Los Santos. Heavy traffic volume. High-speed pursuits and multi-vehicle collisions are common. |
| **Route 1 (Great Ocean Highway)** | Coastal route | Runs along the western coastline from Los Santos through Chumash, past Fort Zancudo, and north toward Paleto Bay. Scenic but narrow with sharp curves. |
| **Route 68** | Cross-state route | Runs east-west through the Senora Desert connecting the Grand Senora Desert to the Zancudo River area. Lower traffic volume but high speeds and remote stretches. |
| **Senora Freeway** | Northern corridor | Connects Los Santos to Blaine County through the eastern hills. Commercial truck traffic is heavy on this route. |
| **Del Perro Freeway** | Western corridor | Runs through western Los Santos and connects to the Great Ocean Highway. Congested during peak hours. |

**Highway patrol responsibilities include:**

- Speed enforcement using radar and LIDAR
- Commercial vehicle inspections and weight enforcement
- DUI detection and enforcement
- Collision response and traffic management
- Disabled motorist assistance
- Criminal interdiction — identifying and stopping vehicles involved in criminal activity based on observable indicators

Troopers are expected to maintain a visible presence on the highways. A marked State Police cruiser parked on the shoulder of the Los Santos Freeway does more for speed compliance than a dozen speed limit signs. Presence is prevention.

## Inter-Agency Cooperation

The State Police does not operate in a vacuum. Effective law enforcement in San Andreas requires cooperation between the SASP, the LSPD, the BCSO, and every other agency in the state. Egos have no place in professional policing.

**Mutual aid protocols:**

- Any agency may request SASP assistance through dispatch or direct contact with the nearest barracks. Troopers will respond to mutual aid calls promptly.
- When assisting a local agency, the local agency retains primary authority unless the incident falls under SASP jurisdiction (state highway, multi-jurisdictional crime, etc.).
- Unified command is established at major incidents involving multiple agencies. The agency with primary jurisdiction assumes command; SASP integrates into the command structure rather than operating independently.

**Task force operations:**

The SASP regularly participates in multi-agency task forces targeting narcotics trafficking, organized crime, auto theft rings, and human trafficking. When assigned to a task force, Troopers operate under the task force commander's authority but remain subject to SASP policy and reporting requirements.

**Authority hierarchy at multi-agency scenes:**

| Scenario | Primary Authority |
|---|---|
| Crime on a state highway | SASP leads; local agency assists |
| Crime within Los Santos city limits | LSPD leads; SASP assists if requested |
| Crime in unincorporated Blaine County | BCSO leads; SASP assists or co-leads |
| Multi-jurisdictional pursuit crossing city/county lines | SASP assumes coordination; originating agency remains primary pursuer |
| Governor's executive directive | SASP leads regardless of location |

When in doubt, communicate. Radio the other agency, establish who has the lead, and work the scene together. Jurisdictional disputes at an active scene are unprofessional and dangerous.

## Executive Protection

The San Andreas State Police is responsible for the protection of the Governor of San Andreas, the Lieutenant Governor, visiting dignitaries, and other state officials as directed. Executive protection is one of the highest-profile assignments in the agency and demands absolute professionalism.

**Executive Protection Unit responsibilities:**

- Close protection of the Governor during all public appearances, travel, and official functions
- Advance team operations — surveying venues, identifying threats, and coordinating with local law enforcement before the Governor arrives
- Motorcade planning and escort, including route selection, counter-surveillance, and emergency contingency planning
- Threat assessment and intelligence coordination with other agencies
- Protection of the Governor's residence and state government buildings

> **Example — Motorcade Escort:**
>
> `/me positions the lead State Police cruiser at the front of the motorcade formation, activates all emergency lighting, and keys the radio.`
>
> **Trooper:** "All units, Vanguard-1. Motorcade is rolling from the State Capitol, northbound on Power Street toward the Maze Bank Tower. Route is clear. All cross-traffic is held. Maintain formation and spacing."
>
> `/me scans the intersections ahead while maintaining a steady pace, watching for any deviation from the secured route.`

Troopers assigned to Executive Protection must pass an advanced background investigation, a psychological evaluation, and a specialized protection course. There is no room for complacency in this assignment — the life of the state's highest elected official depends on your vigilance.

## Major Crime Task Forces

The SASP Criminal Investigation Bureau maintains the authority and capability to investigate crimes of statewide significance. When a case is too large, too complex, or too politically sensitive for a local department to handle alone, the State Police steps in.

**Types of cases handled by SASP Major Crime:**

- Organized crime operations spanning multiple jurisdictions
- Serial violent crimes crossing city or county lines
- Public corruption and crimes committed by elected officials
- Major narcotics trafficking networks
- Officer-involved shootings where an independent investigation is required
- Complex financial crimes and fraud operations with statewide impact

**Task force structure:**

Major Crime task forces are led by an SASP Lieutenant or Captain and staffed with detectives from the SASP and participating local agencies. The task force operates out of a designated command post — often a State Police barracks — and reports through the SASP chain of command.

> **Example — Task Force Briefing:**
>
> **Lieutenant Harmon:** "Here's where we stand. The Madrazo organization has been moving product through three counties using commercial trucks on Route 68 and the Senora Freeway. BCSO flagged the pattern; LSPD Narcotics has a CI inside the Los Santos distribution end. We're taking this statewide. SASP is lead agency. LSPD and BCSO will embed detectives with our team. Warrants go through the State AG's office. Nobody freelances on this — everything goes through this room."

## Barracks System

Unlike municipal departments that operate out of a central station, the SASP operates out of barracks distributed across the state. Each barracks is responsible for patrol coverage of its surrounding area and serves as the operational hub for Troopers assigned to that region.

| Barracks | Location | Area of Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| **Barracks A — Los Santos** | La Mesa, Los Santos | Los Santos Freeway system, Del Perro Freeway, and state routes within the greater Los Santos metro area. |
| **Barracks B — Chumash** | Chumash, west coast | Route 1 (Great Ocean Highway) from Pacific Bluffs north through Chumash to the Lago Zancudo bridge. |
| **Barracks C — Sandy Shores** | Sandy Shores, Grand Senora Desert | Route 68, Senora Freeway (northern section), and state routes through the Grand Senora Desert. |
| **Barracks D — Paleto Bay** | Paleto Bay, northern San Andreas | Great Ocean Highway (northern section), Paleto Bay area, Procopio Beach to Raton Canyon. |
| **Barracks E — Grapeseed** | Grapeseed, Blaine County | Senora Freeway corridor, agricultural zones, and rural routes east of Mount Chiliad. |

Troopers are assigned to a barracks based on departmental need. Transfers between barracks may be requested but are granted based on staffing requirements, not personal preference. You go where the state needs you.

Each barracks is commanded by a Lieutenant and staffed with Sergeants, Corporals, and Troopers sufficient to maintain 24-hour patrol coverage of its area of responsibility.

## Traffic Enforcement

Traffic enforcement is not secondary work — it is the core of what the State Police does every single day. More people die on San Andreas highways each year than in homicides. Every traffic stop is an opportunity to save a life, interdict criminal activity, or remove a dangerous driver from the road.

**Speed enforcement:**

Troopers utilize both stationary and mobile radar and LIDAR to enforce posted speed limits on all state highways. Common enforcement positions include freeway overpasses, highway medians, and concealed turnouts along Route 1 and Route 68. Speed enforcement is not about revenue — it is about reducing fatalities.

**Checkpoints:**

The SASP may establish sobriety checkpoints and safety checkpoints on state highways with authorization from a Captain or above. Checkpoints must comply with state law: they must be publicly announced in advance, use a neutral vehicle selection pattern, and be conducted in a well-lit, safe location.

**Commercial vehicle enforcement:**

Troopers assigned to commercial vehicle enforcement inspect trucks and commercial vehicles for compliance with weight restrictions, cargo securement, driver licensing and log requirements, and mechanical safety standards. Overweight and unsafe commercial vehicles are taken out of service on the spot.

**Accident investigation:**

All collisions on state highways are investigated by the SASP. Troopers are trained in basic collision investigation; Senior Troopers with accident reconstruction certification handle fatal and serious-injury collisions. The scene is documented, measured, photographed, and diagrammed before the roadway is cleared.

## Vehicle Pursuit Authority

Vehicle pursuits are high-risk events that endanger Troopers, suspects, and every civilian on the road. The decision to initiate, continue, or terminate a pursuit rests on a constant evaluation of risk versus necessity.

**Pursuit authorization:**

- Pursuits are authorized only when the suspect is believed to have committed a **violent felony** or presents an **immediate threat to public safety** if not apprehended.
- Pursuits for traffic infractions, misdemeanors, or non-violent felonies are **not authorized** unless there are additional aggravating circumstances approved by a supervisor in real time.
- The pursuing Trooper must broadcast continuously: location, direction of travel, speed, suspect vehicle description, driving behavior, road conditions, and traffic density.

**Pursuit termination:**

A supervisor may terminate a pursuit at any time. The pursuing Trooper must also self-terminate if:

- The suspect's identity is known and can be apprehended later
- Road or weather conditions make continued pursuit unreasonably dangerous
- The pursuit enters a densely populated area with heavy pedestrian traffic
- The Trooper loses visual contact with the suspect vehicle

**Cross-jurisdiction pursuits:**

When a pursuit crosses from a state highway into a municipal jurisdiction, the Trooper must notify local dispatch immediately. The Trooper retains authority to continue the pursuit but must coordinate with responding local units. If local units are in a better position to assume the pursuit, the Trooper may transition to a support role.

**Tactical interventions:**

- **Spike strips** may be deployed with supervisor authorization. Deploying Troopers must be positioned safely and must account for innocent motorists in the area.
- **PIT maneuvers** are authorized only at speeds below 35 mph, only by Troopers who have completed PIT training, and only with supervisor approval.
- **Roadblocks** are not authorized except in extreme circumstances involving an imminent threat to life, and only with Captain-level approval.

## DUI Enforcement

Driving under the influence is one of the leading causes of fatal collisions on San Andreas highways. DUI enforcement is a priority for every Trooper on every shift. You will encounter impaired drivers — your job is to identify them, stop them, and remove them from the road before they kill someone.

**Indicators of impaired driving:**

- Weaving between lanes or drifting across lane markers
- Driving significantly below the posted speed limit without cause
- Delayed response to traffic signals or stop signs
- Erratic braking or acceleration
- Driving without headlights at night
- Near-collisions with other vehicles, guardrails, or road fixtures

**Field sobriety testing:**

Upon suspecting impairment, the Trooper conducts Standardized Field Sobriety Tests (SFSTs):

1. **Horizontal Gaze Nystagmus (HGN)** — Trooper observes the suspect's eyes tracking a stimulus for involuntary jerking.
2. **Walk and Turn** — Suspect walks a straight line heel-to-toe, turns, and walks back. The Trooper observes balance, instruction-following, and coordination.
3. **One-Leg Stand** — Suspect stands on one foot for 30 seconds. The Trooper observes swaying, hopping, or using arms for balance.

> **Example — DUI Stop:**
>
> `/me approaches the driver's window and immediately notes the strong odor of alcohol emanating from the vehicle interior.`
>
> **Trooper:** "Good evening. I'm Trooper Hayes with the San Andreas State Police. The reason I stopped you is you were weaving across lanes back on the Senora Freeway. Have you had anything to drink tonight?"
>
> `/me observes the driver's slurred speech and glassy, bloodshot eyes. Directs the driver to step out of the vehicle and move to a flat, well-lit area on the shoulder.`
>
> **Trooper:** "I'm going to ask you to perform a few simple tests for me. First, I want you to follow this pen with your eyes — just your eyes, don't move your head."
>
> `/me holds the stimulus approximately 12 inches from the subject's face and moves it slowly from center to the left, observing for nystagmus onset before 45 degrees.`

If the SFSTs indicate impairment, the suspect is placed under arrest, transported to the nearest barracks, and administered a breathalyzer or blood test. Refusal to submit to chemical testing is documented and carries its own legal consequences under San Andreas implied consent law.

## State-Level Investigations

Not every crime belongs to the local department. The SASP Criminal Investigation Bureau exists to handle cases that exceed local capacity, cross jurisdictional boundaries, or involve subjects that local agencies cannot investigate impartially.

**The SASP investigates when:**

- The crime occurs on state property or a state highway
- The crime spans two or more local jurisdictions and no single local agency can manage the full scope
- A local agency formally requests SASP investigative assistance due to resource limitations
- The suspect is a public official, law enforcement officer, or politically connected individual where local investigation would present a conflict of interest
- The Governor or State Attorney General directs SASP involvement

**Special Investigations Unit (SIU):**

The SIU is the SASP's plainclothes detective division. SIU detectives handle complex cases including organized crime, public corruption, major narcotics networks, serial crimes, and cold cases of statewide significance. SIU operates out of SASP headquarters and deploys to any location in San Andreas as needed.

SIU detectives carry State Police credentials and retain full law enforcement authority but operate in plainclothes and unmarked vehicles. They coordinate closely with local detectives and federal agencies when cases overlap.

## Standard Procedures

Every Trooper must execute the following procedures with precision. These are not suggestions — they are the standards by which you will be judged by your supervisors, by the courts, and by the public.

### Highway Traffic Stops

1. Activate emergency lights. On high-speed highways, use both lights and siren to ensure the driver is aware.
2. Direct the vehicle to the right shoulder or the nearest safe location off the travel lanes. Never conduct a traffic stop in an active travel lane.
3. Notify dispatch with your location, vehicle description, license plate, and number of occupants before exiting your cruiser.
4. Approach on the passenger side whenever possible — this keeps you away from passing traffic and gives you a tactical advantage.
5. Identify yourself and state the reason for the stop. Be direct and professional.
6. Complete the contact, issue the citation or warning, and clear the stop efficiently. Extended stops on highway shoulders increase risk for everyone.

> **Example — Highway Traffic Stop:**
>
> `/me pulls the cruiser onto the right shoulder behind the stopped vehicle, offsets slightly to the left, and activates the rear takedown lights to create a buffer from passing traffic.`
>
> **Trooper:** "Good afternoon, I'm Trooper Mitchell with the State Police. I clocked you at 87 in a posted 65 zone on the Los Santos Freeway. Can I see your license, registration, and proof of insurance?"
>
> `/me takes the documents back to the cruiser, runs the driver through NCIC and state databases, and prepares the citation.`

### Felony Stops

Felony stops on highways follow the same general procedure as any high-risk stop but with additional considerations for high-speed traffic and limited cover. A minimum of two units must be on scene before initiating. Spike strips may be pre-deployed ahead of the stop location if the suspect is expected to flee.

> **Example — Felony Stop on Highway:**
>
> `/me positions the cruiser at a sharp offset angle behind the suspect vehicle, opens the door, and uses it as cover while drawing the service weapon.`
>
> **Trooper (PA System):** "Driver of the red pickup — this is the San Andreas State Police. Turn off your engine. Drop the keys out the window. Place both hands out the window where I can see them. Do it now."
>
> `/me holds position behind the door and waits for the backup unit to arrive and establish a secondary cover position before issuing further commands.`

### Use of Force

The SASP Use of Force policy mirrors the state standard. Force must be objectively reasonable, proportional to the threat, and the minimum necessary to achieve the lawful objective. Every use of force above verbal commands requires a written report submitted before end of shift and supervisor review within 24 hours. Lethal force is authorized only when the Trooper reasonably believes there is an imminent threat of death or serious bodily injury.

## Final Thoughts

The San Andreas State Police is not just another law enforcement agency. It is the agency that other agencies measure themselves against. When a local department is overwhelmed, they call us. When a case is too big, too complex, or too politically sensitive for anyone else, it lands on our desk. When the Governor needs a motorcade through downtown Los Santos, the troopers standing post at every intersection wear our uniform.

That is not arrogance. That is responsibility — and it is earned every single day by every Trooper who puts on the campaign hat and walks out the barracks door.

You will patrol highways where the speeds are deadly and the shoulders are narrow. You will work cases that stretch across county lines and take months to close. You will stand post at dignitary events for hours without a break. You will back up local officers who need help and never ask for credit. You will enforce the law of this state fairly, firmly, and without compromise.

The campaign hat is not a costume. It is a symbol of the discipline, professionalism, and authority that the people of San Andreas expect from their State Police. Wear it with pride, and conduct yourself in a manner that justifies that pride every day.

Welcome to the San Andreas State Police. The highways are waiting.
