# Aviation Roleplay Overview — Taking to the Skies of Los Santos

Aviation roleplay is one of the most immersive and underappreciated corners of GTA V RP. While most players are content to drive the streets of Los Santos, a dedicated community of pilots, air traffic controllers, and aviation enthusiasts bring the skies to life. Whether you're ferrying passengers between LSIA and Sandy Shores, chasing suspects from a police helicopter, or airlifting a critical patient to Pillbox Medical, aviation RP adds an entirely new dimension to the server.

This guide covers everything you need to know about aviation RP — from the aircraft available to you, to how airspace works, to the radio discipline that separates a serious pilot from someone who just spawned a helicopter.

---

## Why Aviation RP Exists

Aviation RP exists because it fills roles that no other department or job can. Ground ambulances get stuck in traffic — air ambulances don't. Police pursuits stretch across Blaine County at 140 mph — a helicopter stays overhead effortlessly. Cargo needs to move between the city and the county — a plane does it in minutes.

Beyond the practical, aviation RP creates a unique power dynamic. A pilot controls a multi-million dollar machine that everyone on the ground can see and hear. That visibility brings responsibility. You're never anonymous in a helicopter. Every decision you make — where you fly, how low, how fast — is on display for the entire server.

## Types of Aviation RP

Aviation RP breaks down into several distinct categories, each with its own rules, expectations, and career paths.

| Type | Description | Common Aircraft | Typical Employer |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Civilian** | Private flights, sightseeing tours, personal transport | Mammatus, Velum, Cuban 800 | Self-employed, charter companies |
| **Commercial** | Scheduled passenger and cargo flights | Luxor, Velum 5-Seater, Shamal | Airlines, cargo operators |
| **Law Enforcement** | Pursuit support, surveillance, SWAT insertion | Buzzard, Maverick, Police Maverick | LSPD, BCSO Air Support Unit |
| **EMS / HEMS** | Air ambulance, patient transport, medevac | Maverick (EMS livery), Frogger | EMS Department, hospitals |
| **Military** | Fort Zancudo operations, restricted area patrols | Savage, Valkyrie, Buzzard Attack | Military RP factions |
| **News / Media** | Live coverage, traffic reports, event coverage | Frogger, Maverick | News stations |

Each type has different licensing requirements, chain of command, and standard operating procedures. A civilian charter pilot operates very differently from a police aviation observer.

## Airports and Helipads

Los Santos has several key aviation facilities, each with different access levels and RP significance.

### Los Santos International Airport (LSIA)

LSIA is the primary airport. It has two runways, a terminal building, multiple hangars, and a control tower. This is where commercial operations are based, where civilian pilots file flight plans, and where air traffic control operates. Unauthorized aircraft on the runways will receive immediate attention from airport security and law enforcement.

### Sandy Shores Airfield

The county airstrip. Sandy Shores is a single-runway, uncontrolled field with no tower. It's used for civilian general aviation, crop dusters, and sometimes less-than-legal cargo operations. Pilots operating here rely on self-announced radio calls rather than ATC clearance.

### Fort Zancudo

Military airfield. Restricted access — period. Civilian aircraft entering Zancudo airspace will be intercepted and potentially shot down. Only military RP characters with proper clearance operate here. It has a full runway, hangars, and a helipad.

### Helipads

Several helipads exist across the map:

- **Pillbox Medical Center** — primary hospital helipad for EMS operations
- **Central Los Santos Medical Center** — secondary hospital helipad
- **LSPD Headquarters / Mission Row** — police helipad
- **Various rooftop pads** — scattered across downtown Los Santos
- **Vespucci Helipad** — coastal helipad near the beach

## Flight Schools in RP

Most servers require pilots to attend an in-character flight school before receiving any license. Flight schools are typically run by certified instructor characters and cover:

- **Ground school** — aerodynamics basics, weather theory, navigation, radio communication
- **Pre-flight procedures** — walkaround inspections, fuel checks, control surface verification
- **Basic maneuvers** — takeoff, level flight, turns, descent, landing
- **Emergency procedures** — engine failure, autorotation (helicopters), forced landings
- **Navigation** — VFR (Visual Flight Rules) navigation using landmarks, compass headings, altimeter
- **Radio communication** — proper phraseology, frequency management, position reports

> `/me walks around the Mammatus, checking the ailerons, rudder, and elevator for freedom of movement. He crouches under the wing and inspects the fuel drain valve.`
> "All control surfaces are free and correct. Fuel sample is clean — no water contamination. We're good for pre-start."

## Aviation Authority Structure

A well-structured aviation RP operation typically follows a hierarchy:

| Rank / Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| **Director of Aviation** | Oversees all aviation operations, sets policy, approves new pilots |
| **Chief Pilot** | Manages day-to-day flight operations, assigns missions |
| **Senior Pilot / Captain** | Leads complex missions, mentors junior pilots |
| **Pilot / First Officer** | Standard flight operations, licensed and current |
| **Student Pilot** | In training, flies only with an instructor aboard |
| **Air Traffic Controller** | Manages airspace, issues clearances, sequences traffic |
| **Ground Crew** | Fueling, maintenance RP, marshalling aircraft |

## Air Traffic Control Basics

ATC in RP is simplified but still follows real-world principles. The key concept is **communication and separation** — making sure aircraft don't collide and everyone knows who is where.

### Basic ATC Phraseology

> **Pilot:** "Los Santos Tower, this is November-Four-Seven-Bravo, Mammatus, at Hangar 3, request taxi and departure to the north."
> **ATC:** "November-Four-Seven-Bravo, Los Santos Tower, taxi to Runway 03 via taxiway Alpha. Wind zero-three-zero at eight knots. Altimeter two-niner-niner-two."
> **Pilot:** "Taxi Runway 03 via Alpha, November-Four-Seven-Bravo."

At uncontrolled fields like Sandy Shores, pilots self-announce:

> **Pilot:** "Sandy Shores traffic, Velum Two-Echo-Golf, departing Runway 09, northbound departure. Sandy Shores."

### Key Radio Frequencies

| Frequency | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Tower** | Active runway operations at LSIA |
| **Ground** | Taxi instructions at LSIA |
| **Approach / Departure** | Arriving and departing aircraft in the LSIA area |
| **CTAF (Sandy Shores)** | Common Traffic Advisory Frequency for uncontrolled fields |
| **Emergency** | Reserved for emergencies — `MAYDAY` calls |

## Weather Considerations

Weather plays a major role in aviation RP, even within GTA V's simplified weather system.

- **Rain** — Reduced visibility, slippery runways. VFR-only pilots should consider staying grounded.
- **Fog** — Severely limited visibility. Only instrument-rated pilots should fly. ATC may close the airport.
- **Wind** — Crosswind landings are challenging. Pilots should announce wind conditions.
- **Night** — Reduced visibility, harder to identify landmarks. Night-rated pilots only.
- **Thunderstorms** — All aircraft should be grounded. No exceptions in serious RP.

> `/me checks the weather radar on the dispatch terminal and frowns.`
> "We've got a cell moving in from the west. I'm calling a ground stop — nobody takes off until that clears the field."

## Airspace Restrictions

Not all airspace is open to all pilots. Respecting airspace restrictions is critical for immersive aviation RP.

| Airspace | Restriction | Consequence of Violation |
|---|---|---|
| **Fort Zancudo** | Military restricted — no civilian access | Interception, forced landing, arrest, or shoot-down |
| **LSIA Control Zone** | ATC clearance required for all operations | Violation warning, license suspension |
| **Government Buildings** | No-fly zone over City Hall, courthouse | Investigation, license review |
| **Active Crime Scenes** | Police may declare temporary no-fly zones | Obstruction charges |
| **Prison (Bolingbroke)** | Restricted airspace — no overflights below 1000ft | Immediate law enforcement response |

## Common Mistakes in Aviation RP

| Mistake | Why It's a Problem | What to Do Instead |
|---|---|---|
| Spawning a helicopter and immediately flying | No pre-flight, no radio call, no immersion | Always RP the startup sequence and radio check |
| Flying without a license | Breaks server rules and immersion | Complete flight school and earn your license |
| Ignoring ATC | Destroys the experience for ATC players | Always monitor and respond to ATC |
| Buzzing buildings at low altitude | Unrealistic, disruptive, breaks immersion | Maintain realistic altitudes unless operationally required |
| Landing anywhere you want | Helicopters landing in traffic, on sidewalks | Use designated helipads and landing zones |
| No radio communication | Other pilots and ATC can't track you | Always announce intentions on the appropriate frequency |
