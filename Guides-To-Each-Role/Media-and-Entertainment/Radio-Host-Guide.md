# Radio Host Roleplay Guide — Broadcasting Across Los Santos

In a city full of sirens, gunshots, and screeching tires, the radio is the one constant. It's what plays in the background of every taxi ride, every mechanic shop, and every late-night drive along the coast highway. A good radio host doesn't just fill airtime — they create atmosphere, build community, and give Los Santos a voice that everyone recognizes.

This guide covers everything about running a radio station in RP — from show formats and on-air personality to station management, live event coverage, and the business side of broadcasting.

---

## Why Radio RP Matters

Radio RP serves several functions that no other role provides:

- **Atmosphere** — Background audio that makes the city feel alive.
- **Information** — Breaking news, weather, traffic updates that characters react to.
- **Community** — Call-in shows, shout-outs, and dedications create connections between players.
- **Advertising** — Businesses advertise on the radio, creating economic RP.
- **Entertainment** — Music, comedy, debates, and storytelling.
- **Narrative amplification** — A news segment about a gang war or political scandal reaches players who weren't there.

Radio is the connective tissue of the server. It ties together storylines, promotes businesses, and gives every player a shared experience.

## Creating a Radio Character

Your on-air persona is everything. Think about what makes radio hosts memorable — it's voice, attitude, and consistency.

| Element | Considerations |
|---|---|
| **On-air name** | Real name? Stage name? Something catchy and memorable? |
| **Personality** | Smooth and professional? Energetic and chaotic? Dry and sarcastic? Warm and community-focused? |
| **Specialty** | Music DJ? News anchor? Talk show host? Sports commentator? Late-night advice guru? |
| **Voice style** | Calm and measured? Fast-talking? Deep and resonant? Excitable? |
| **Background** | Former journalist? College radio kid? Career broadcaster? Failed musician turned DJ? |
| **Signature elements** | Catchphrases, recurring segments, a specific sign-on/sign-off |

Your character should be recognizable within the first five seconds of hearing them speak.

> `/me leans into the microphone, adjusts the headphones, and watches the ON AIR light flicker to life.`
> "Goooood evening, Los Santos! It's 10 PM and you're listening to The Night Shift with Danny Vega, right here on LS Rock Radio. If you're driving, buckle up. If you're at home, crack a cold one. We've got three hours of the best rock, the worst jokes, and maybe — just maybe — a caller who doesn't hang up on me."

## Show Formats

Different shows serve different purposes and audiences. A well-run station has variety in its programming.

### Music Shows

The most straightforward format — you play music and talk between tracks.

| Show Type | Description | Audience |
|---|---|---|
| **Morning drive** | Upbeat, energetic, traffic and weather, short segments | Commuters, early risers |
| **Midday mix** | Background-friendly, chill, minimal talk | Workers, shop owners |
| **Afternoon drive** | Upbeat, requests, listener interaction | After-work crowd |
| **Evening specialty** | Genre-specific (jazz, hip-hop, country, oldies) | Music enthusiasts |
| **Late night** | Mellow, slower pace, deeper tracks | Night owls, late-shift workers |

### Talk Shows

Talk shows are where the real RP depth happens. You're having conversations that the entire server can hear.

| Format | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Interview show** | One-on-one or panel interviews with notable characters | "The Mayor joins us to discuss the new housing bill" |
| **Call-in show** | Listeners call in with questions, opinions, or stories | "You're on the air — what's on your mind tonight?" |
| **Debate show** | Two guests with opposing views discuss a topic | "Should LSPD be defunded? We have representatives from both sides" |
| **Advice show** | Listeners call in seeking advice | "Love, life, and everything in between — The Night Shift is your therapist" |
| **Comedy show** | Stand-up, sketches, prank calls, humorous commentary | Late-night entertainment programming |

### News Programming

Dedicated news blocks keep the city informed. These can be scheduled (hourly news updates) or breaking (interrupting regular programming).

> `/me glances at the incoming wire from the newsroom and holds up a hand to signal the music operator.`
> "We're going to interrupt the music for a moment, folks. We're getting reports of a major police pursuit on the Los Santos Freeway heading southbound. LSPD has multiple units involved. If you're on the freeway — get off it. We'll bring you updates as they come in."

## Building an Audience

A radio station without listeners is just someone talking to themselves. Building an audience takes effort and consistency.

### Audience Building Strategies

| Strategy | How It Works |
|---|---|
| **Consistent schedule** | Broadcast at the same times so people know when to tune in |
| **Interact with listeners** | Take calls, read messages, acknowledge regulars |
| **Cover events** | Go live at community events, races, fights, concerts |
| **Promote on social media** | IC Twitter/social posts about upcoming shows |
| **Cross-promote** | Partner with businesses — "This hour brought to you by..." |
| **Memorable segments** | Recurring bits that people look forward to |
| **Be the first with news** | Break stories before anyone else and people will tune in |

## Interviewing Guests

Having guests on your show is one of the richest RP interactions available.

### Pre-Interview

- **Book the guest in advance** — Send an IC message or meet them.
- **Research the guest** — Know their role, their recent actions, their controversies.
- **Prepare questions** — Have a list, but be flexible.
- **Brief the guest** — Tell them the show format, approximate length, and general topic areas.

### On-Air Interview

- **Introduce the guest properly** — Name, title, why they're on the show.
- **Start with easy questions** — Let them warm up.
- **Ask follow-ups** — Don't just move to the next question if they say something interesting.
- **Let them talk** — You're the host, not the star. The guest is why people are listening.
- **Manage time** — Keep an eye on the clock. Don't let it drag.

> **Host:** "Joining me in the studio tonight is District Attorney Katherine Price. DA Price, thanks for coming on."
> **DA Price:** "Thanks for having me, Danny."
> **Host:** "Let's get right into it. The Carter case — the whole city's talking about it. Three defendants, organized crime charges, and your office is asking for the maximum. Why this case, and why now?"
> `/me leans back in the chair and gestures for the DA to take her time with the answer.`

## Live Event Coverage

Going live from events is some of the most exciting radio RP — races, community gatherings, protests, crime scenes, grand openings.

### Event Coverage Checklist

1. **Coordinate with event organizers** — Get access and permission.
2. **Set up a broadcast position** — Find a location with a good vantage point.
3. **Announce the remote broadcast** — "We're going live from the LS Car Meet at the docks!"
4. **Interview participants and attendees** — "What brings you out tonight?"
5. **Describe the atmosphere** — Paint a picture for listeners who aren't there.
6. **React to what happens** — If something unexpected occurs, cover it live.

> `/me holds the portable microphone up over the crowd noise as the race cars line up on the starting grid at the Los Santos docks.`
> "Folks, you can hear the engines behind me — eight cars lined up, ready to tear through the streets of Los Santos. The energy down here is unreal. We've got custom builds, imported tuners, and one brave soul in a stock Futo who's about to have the ride of his life."

## Advertisement Sales

Advertising is how a radio station funds itself. It's also great economic RP.

### How Ads Work in RP

| Ad Type | Description | Rate Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Live read** | Host reads the ad copy on air in their own words | Per read |
| **Pre-recorded spot** | Produced ad played between segments | Per play, or per block of time |
| **Sponsorship** | "This segment brought to you by..." | Per show or per week |
| **Event promotion** | Promoting a business's event on-air | Negotiated |

### Example Live Read

> "Before we get back to the music, a quick word from Benny's Original Motor Works down in Strawberry. If your ride needs a new look — custom paint, performance upgrades, complete overhaul — Benny's is the place. Tell 'em Danny Vega sent you and... well, nothing happens, but they'll know you have great taste in radio. Benny's Original Motor Works. Get there."

## Station Management

Running a radio station is a management role with real RP responsibilities.

### Station Structure

| Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| **Station Owner / GM** | Sets direction, hires staff, manages finances, secures advertising |
| **Program Director** | Creates the broadcast schedule, assigns time slots, manages show quality |
| **Music Director** | Curates playlists, selects genres, manages music library |
| **News Director** | Oversees news programming, fact-checks stories, manages reporters |
| **Show Host / DJ** | Broadcasts their assigned show, builds audience, creates content |
| **Field Reporter** | Covers events and stories outside the studio |
| **Engineer / Tech** | Manages broadcast equipment, handles technical issues |
| **Sales Manager** | Sells advertising slots, maintains client relationships |

### Station Operations

- **Maintain a broadcast schedule** — Post it publicly so listeners know what's on and when.
- **Hold staff meetings** — Align on station direction, address issues, brainstorm content.
- **Quality control** — Listen to your hosts. Give feedback. Maintain standards.
- **Handle complaints** — If a listener or advertiser has a problem, address it professionally.

## Breaking News Protocols

When breaking news happens, the station needs a protocol to switch from regular programming to news coverage.

### Breaking News Procedure

1. **Receive the information** — Police scanner, field reporter call-in, social media, tip.
2. **Verify** — Don't go live with unconfirmed rumors. At minimum, verify with one source.
3. **Interrupt programming** — "We're interrupting our regular programming for a breaking news update."
4. **Report what you know** — Stick to facts. Say "we're hearing reports" for unconfirmed info.
5. **Stay live** — Provide updates as they come in. Fill time with context and background.
6. **Return to regular programming** — Once the situation stabilizes, transition back smoothly.

> `/me pulls off the headphones and signals to the producer through the glass.`
> "Cut the music. We've got a ten-fifty at the Pillbox intersection — multiple vehicles involved. I'm going live."
> `/me puts the headphones back on and watches the ON AIR light.`
> "Ladies and gentlemen, we're breaking into our regular programming. We're receiving reports of a serious multi-vehicle accident at the intersection outside Pillbox Medical Center. Emergency services are responding. If you're in the area, please avoid the intersection and give first responders room to work. We'll bring you updates as we get them."

## Station Branding

A radio station needs an identity that players remember.

| Element | Example |
|---|---|
| **Station name** | LS Rock Radio, Blaine County FM, Santos City Talk |
| **Tagline** | "The Soundtrack of Los Santos" / "All Talk, No Filter" |
| **Jingles** | Short audio clips between segments that ID the station |
| **Logo** | Displayed on vehicles, social media, merchandise |
| **Tone** | Professional? Edgy? Community-focused? Political? |

Consistency is key. Every time a listener hears your station, they should immediately know what they're listening to.

## Common Mistakes in Radio RP

| Mistake | Why It's a Problem | What to Do Instead |
|---|---|---|
| Broadcasting once and never again | No consistency means no audience | Set a schedule and stick to it |
| Talking over guests constantly | Makes interviews painful to listen to | Let your guests speak — you're the facilitator |
| Only playing music with no personality | That's a playlist, not a show | Talk between tracks, build a persona, interact |
| Reporting unverified rumors as fact | Destroys station credibility | Verify before broadcasting, label unconfirmed info |
| Ignoring the business side | Station has no funding, feels hollow | Sell ads, seek sponsorships, create economic RP |
| Never leaving the studio | Missing the best content | Do remote broadcasts, cover events, hit the streets |
