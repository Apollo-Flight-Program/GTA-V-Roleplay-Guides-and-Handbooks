# Common RP Terms Glossary

This is a comprehensive glossary of terms, abbreviations, and slang used across GTA V roleplay communities. Whether you are brand new to roleplay or transferring from another server, this reference will help you understand the language everyone around you is speaking.

Terms are organized alphabetically. Where a term has multiple common names, all variations are listed.

---

## A

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Action RP** | Roleplay centered around high-intensity events — chases, shootouts, robberies, and confrontations. Should always be grounded in story context, not just mechanics. |
| **Admin** | A server staff member responsible for enforcing rules, resolving disputes, and managing the community. Also called Staff, Moderator, or Game Master depending on the server. |
| **AFK** | Away From Keyboard. Stepping away from the game while still connected. Extended AFK in active areas is generally discouraged. |
| **AOP** | Area of Play. Some servers restrict roleplay to a specific area of the map at any given time due to player count limitations. |
| **AR** | Active Roleplay. Indicates a scene is currently in progress and should not be interrupted without IC reason. |

---

## B

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Beat** | A patrol zone or assigned area for law enforcement officers. Example: "What beat are you running tonight?" |
| **BOL** | Be On the Lookout. An alert broadcast over radio to notify officers of a suspect, vehicle, or situation. |
| **Breaking Character** | Stepping out of your character's persona during an active scene. Generally prohibited unless using designated OOC channels. |
| **Buff** | Using game mechanics or animations to gain an advantage not available through normal RP, such as eating food mid-combat for health regeneration. |

---

## C

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **CAD** | Computer Aided Dispatch. A system (usually a web app or in-game tablet) used by LEO and dispatch to track calls, warrants, vehicle registrations, and criminal records. |
| **Cadets** | New or trainee officers who have been accepted into a law enforcement department but have not yet completed their field training. Usually ride with a senior officer. |
| **Chain of Command** | The hierarchy within a faction or department that dictates who reports to whom, and who has authority to make certain decisions. |
| **Civ / Civilian** | A player character with no whitelisted role — a regular citizen of the city. The majority of players on any server. |
| **CK (Character Kill)** | A permanent death for a character. Once CK'd, the character cannot be played again. May be voluntary or enforced by server rules. See also: Permadeath. |
| **CL (Combat Logging)** | Disconnecting from the server during an active roleplay scene to avoid consequences such as arrest, death, or loss of items. One of the most severely punished offenses. |
| **Code 1 / 2 / 3 / 4** | Response priority levels. Code 1 = no rush, Code 2 = urgent no lights, Code 3 = emergency with lights and sirens, Code 4 = scene secure. See [Dispatch Codes Reference](Dispatch-Codes-Reference.md). |
| **Cop Baiting** | Intentionally provoking law enforcement to initiate a chase or confrontation without any IC motivation. Examples include doing burnouts in front of a police station or committing crimes directly in front of officers for the sole purpose of getting a reaction. |

---

## D

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Deathmatching** | Killing another player without valid IC reason or adequate roleplay leading up to it. See RDM and VDM. |
| **DOJ** | Department of Justice. The judicial branch in RP servers — includes judges, lawyers, district attorneys, and public defenders who handle court cases, warrants, and legal proceedings. |
| **/do** | A text command used to describe environmental details or observable facts. Example: `/do The alley smells of gasoline and there are fresh tire marks on the pavement.` |
| **Downed** | When a player's character is incapacitated (on the ground, injured, unconscious). The player must wait for medical attention or respawn after a timer. |

---

## E

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **EMS** | Emergency Medical Services. A whitelisted role responsible for responding to medical emergencies, treating injured players, and transporting them to hospitals. |
| **ERP** | Erotic Roleplay. Sexual or explicitly intimate roleplay between characters. Banned on the vast majority of servers. Always check server rules. |

---

## F

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Faction** | An organized group within the server — a gang, a police department, a business, a government body, or any structured group with members and leadership. |
| **FailRP / FRP** | Performing actions that no reasonable person would do in real life, breaking the immersion of the scene. Examples: running after being shot in both legs, pulling a rifle from thin air while handcuffed, or laughing while being robbed at gunpoint. |
| **Fear RP** | The expectation that your character behaves as a real person would when facing threats — showing fear when held at gunpoint, outnumbered, or in life-threatening situations. See [Fear RP and Value of Life](../Roleplay%20in%20General/Fear-RP-and-Value-of-Life.md). |
| **FIB** | Federal Investigation Bureau. The GTA V equivalent of the FBI. Some servers have this as a whitelisted department. |
| **FTO** | Field Training Officer. A senior officer responsible for training cadets during ride-alongs and evaluating their readiness for solo patrol. |

---

## G

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Gang RP** | Roleplay centered around criminal organizations — territory control, drug operations, rivalries, initiations, and internal politics. |
| **Greenzone / Safe Zone** | A designated area on the map where no hostile RP (robbery, assault, kidnapping) is allowed. Common greenzones include hospitals, police stations, and spawn points. |
| **GTA Driving** | Driving unrealistically — treating the game like GTA Online rather than a roleplay server. Includes excessive speeding in the city, ramping off hills, driving on sidewalks, and ignoring traffic laws without IC justification. |

---

## H–I

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Hostage** | A person taken and held by a criminal during a scene (often a robbery) as leverage against law enforcement. Hostage RP requires careful Fear RP from all parties. |
| **IC (In Character)** | Anything said or done as your character within the game world. Your character's knowledge, feelings, and actions. The opposite of OOC. |

---

## J–K

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Jailbreak** | An RP scenario where criminals attempt to break an imprisoned character out of custody. Usually requires significant planning and coordination. |

---

## L

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **LEO** | Law Enforcement Officer. A blanket term for any police, sheriff, trooper, or federal agent character on the server. |
| **LOA (Leave of Absence)** | A formal notice given to a faction or department that a player will be inactive for a period of time. Prevents removal from rosters. |
| **Locals / NPCs** | The AI-controlled pedestrians, drivers, and shopkeepers that populate the GTA world. Players are generally expected to acknowledge their existence rather than ignore them. |

---

## M

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **/me** | A text command used to describe a physical action your character performs. Example: `/me slowly reaches for the wallet in his back pocket, keeping his hands visible.` |
| **Metagaming / Meta** | Using information obtained outside of the game (streams, Discord, OOC chat) to influence your character's IC decisions. One of the most serious rule violations. See [Metagaming & Powergaming](../Roleplay%20in%20General/Metagaming-and-Powergaming.md). |

---

## N

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **NLR (New Life Rule)** | After your character dies and respawns, they lose all memory of the events surrounding their death. You cannot return to the area, seek revenge, or act on information from the scene where you died. |
| **NVL (Not Valuing Life)** | Failing to treat your character's life as precious. Running into gunfire, refusing to comply at gunpoint, or acting invincible. Closely related to Fear RP. |
| **/911** | An in-game command to call emergency services. Sends a message to on-duty LEO and EMS with your location and a brief description of the emergency. |

---

## O

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **OOC (Out of Character)** | Anything said or done as the player behind the character — not as the character themselves. OOC communication should only happen through designated channels. |
| **/ooc** | A text command for speaking out of character to nearby players. Used sparingly and only when necessary. Example: `/ooc Hey, are you okay to continue this scene?` |

---

## P

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Passive RP** | Everyday, low-intensity roleplay — conversations, working jobs, running errands, socializing. The foundation that makes a server feel like a living world. |
| **Perma / Permadeath** | Permanently killing your character so they can never be played again. A significant narrative decision. See [Permadeath Guide](../Roleplay%20in%20General/Permadeath-Guide.md). |
| **PK (Player Kill)** | A death that is not permanent — the character loses memory of the events (NLR applies) but continues to exist and can be played again. |
| **Pocket Wiping** | Taking every single item from a downed or robbed player. Generally considered poor RP — take what makes sense for the scene, not everything they own. Most servers have rules limiting this. |
| **Power Gaming** | Forcing an action or outcome on another player without giving them a chance to respond or resist. Example: `/me tackles the man to the ground, ties him up, and throws him in the trunk` — all in one action with no opportunity for the other player to react. See [Metagaming & Powergaming](../Roleplay%20in%20General/Metagaming-and-Powergaming.md). |
| **Priority** | A large-scale active scene (usually criminal) that takes up significant server resources and staff attention. Most servers limit the number of simultaneous priorities. When a priority is active, other groups may be asked to hold off on major criminal activity. |
| **Priority Queue** | A system that allows certain players (donors, whitelisted members, staff) to join the server ahead of the regular queue. |

---

## Q–R

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **RDM (Random Deathmatch)** | Killing or attacking another player with no IC reason, no prior interaction, and no roleplay leading up to it. One of the most common rule violations. |
| **ROE (Rules of Engagement)** | Guidelines within a faction (especially LEO) that dictate when and how force can be used. Example: "Do not fire unless fired upon." |
| **RP (Roleplay)** | The act of assuming the role of a fictional character and acting, speaking, and making decisions as that character would. |

---

## S

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **/say** | The default in-game voice or text chat that transmits to nearby players in character. |
| **Scene** | A discrete roleplay event or situation involving two or more players. A scene has a beginning, a middle, and should have a clear resolution. Examples: a traffic stop, a robbery, a medical call, a bar conversation. |
| **SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)** | Written guidelines for how a department or faction handles specific situations. LEO SOPs cover traffic stops, pursuits, use of force, etc. |
| **Soft RP** | A more relaxed approach to roleplay with less strict enforcement of realism. Contrast with Serious or Hardcore RP. |

---

## T

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Text RP** | Roleplay conducted primarily through typed commands (/me, /do) rather than voice. Some servers are text-only; most use a mix. |
| **Tsunami** | Server restart. Called a tsunami because many servers RP the restart as a natural event that resets the world. Players are warned before a tsunami so they can wrap up active scenes. |

---

## U–V

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **VDM (Vehicle Deathmatch)** | Using a vehicle as a weapon — intentionally running over or ramming players without adequate IC reason or roleplay. |
| **Voice RP** | Roleplay conducted through voice chat. The standard on most modern GTA V RP servers. |

---

## W

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Whitelist / Whitelisted** | A role or job that requires an application and approval before a player can access it. Common whitelisted roles include LEO, EMS, DOJ, and mechanic. |
| **Win RP** | Playing with the sole goal of "winning" rather than telling a good story. Prioritizing mechanical advantage over narrative quality. The opposite of good roleplay. |

---

## Quick Reference — Most Important Terms for New Players

| Term | One-Line Definition |
|---|---|
| IC | In Character — speaking and acting as your character |
| OOC | Out of Character — speaking as yourself, the player |
| NLR | New Life Rule — forget how you died after respawn |
| RDM | Random Deathmatch — killing without RP reason |
| VDM | Vehicle Deathmatch — using vehicles as weapons |
| FRP | Fail RP — doing something no real person would do |
| CL | Combat Logging — disconnecting during a scene |
| Fear RP | Acting afraid when your life is threatened |
| Meta | Using OOC info for IC decisions |
| Power Gaming | Forcing outcomes without giving others a chance |
| NVL | Not Valuing Life — acting invincible |
| Perma/CK | Permanent character death |
