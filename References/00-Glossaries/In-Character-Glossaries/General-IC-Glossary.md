# General In-Character Glossary

This glossary covers common in-character terms, phrases, and language used across everyday life in Los Santos. These are words and expressions your **character** knows and uses naturally — the vocabulary of living in the city, regardless of role or occupation.

Terms marked with *(slang)* are informal expressions used in casual conversation. Terms marked with *(formal)* appear in official or professional contexts.

---

## A

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **The Avenue** | Any main street in a neighborhood — often used to refer to the main drag of a district without naming it specifically. |
| **The Bay** | The Port of Los Santos and surrounding industrial waterfront. "Stuff moves through the bay." |
| **Bread** *(slang)* | Money. "Making bread." |
| **Burn phone** *(slang)* | A cheap, disposable phone used for brief communication and then discarded to avoid being traced. |

---

## C

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **The City** | Los Santos as a whole. "I've been in the city three years now." |
| **Clean** *(slang)* | Not carrying anything illegal. "I'm clean — search me." |
| **Connect** *(slang)* | A trusted supplier or contact, especially in criminal circles. "He's my connect for hardware." |
| **The Corner** *(slang)* | A street-level drug dealing spot. "He works the corner on Jamestown." |

---

## D

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **The Docks** | The Port of Los Santos / Terminal area. High-traffic area for smuggling and import/export RP. |
| **Downtown** | The Pillbox Hill / Mission Row business district. The civic center of the city. |
| **Drift** *(slang)* | To move between neighborhoods or areas. "I've been drifting between the south side and Vespucci." |
| **Drop** *(slang)* | A designated location to leave or pick up something — money, goods, a message. |

---

## F

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Fed** *(slang)* | A federal agent or undercover officer. "Watch him — he might be a fed." |
| **The Five** *(slang)* | The police. Derived from "Five-O" — old slang for law enforcement. |
| **Flush** *(slang)* | Having plenty of money. "They're flush right now — just moved a shipment." |
| **Front** *(slang)* | A legitimate business used to conceal illegal activity. "The car wash is just a front." |

---

## G

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Ghost** *(slang)* | To disappear, go quiet, or avoid being seen. "He ghosted after the shooting — nobody's heard from him." |
| **Green** *(slang)* | Money. Also used to mean a newcomer or inexperienced person. "He's still green." |
| **Grind** *(slang)* | Day-to-day hustle — working, making money, building something. "Back on the grind." |

---

## H

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **The Hills** | Vinewood Hills / Rockford Hills — the wealthy residential area. Associated with money, influence, and legitimate power. |
| **The Hood** *(slang)* | A neighborhood, usually a working-class or gang-controlled area. Specific to the speaker's home territory. |
| **Hot** *(slang)* | Under police surveillance or currently being sought by law enforcement. "That block is hot right now." |
| **The House** | Jail or prison. "He spent three years in the house." |

---

## I

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **IC** | In-character. Used rarely in speech — usually only when a player needs to gently signal something is happening in the story versus real life. |

---

## J

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Jake** *(slang)* | Police officer. Old street slang for a cop. |
| **Jump** *(slang)* | To rob or assault someone, usually a surprise attack. |

---

## L

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Lay low** | To stay out of sight, avoid attention, and wait until a situation cools down. |
| **Legit** *(slang)* | Legitimate — not criminal in nature. "He runs a legit business now." |
| **Look-out** *(slang)* | A person posted to watch for police or rivals while others conduct an operation. |

---

## M

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Mark** *(slang)* | A target for robbery or a gullible victim. |
| **The Mission** | The Mission Row area — home to the police station and several legal businesses. |
| **Move** *(slang)* | A transaction or business action, especially in criminal commerce. "They're making a move tonight." |

---

## N

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **The Neighborhood** | A character's home turf — the area they live in, operate in, and feel responsible for. |
| **Numbers** *(slang)* | Money, finances. "The numbers are looking good this week." |

---

## O

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **On paper** *(formal)* | Officially documented — a criminal record, a business license, a warrant. "He's got a sheet — he's on paper." |
| **On the clock** | Working, currently on duty. "I'm on the clock — can't talk long." |
| **On the level** | Honest, legitimate, not deceptive. "Is this on the level?" |
| **Op** *(slang)* | Short for opponent or operation, depending on context. In gang RP: a rival. In organized RP: an operation. |

---

## P

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Paper** *(slang)* | Money. Also: legal paperwork such as a warrant or court summons. |
| **Plug** *(slang)* | A supplier or connection for goods — especially drugs or weapons. "Who's your plug?" |
| **Put in work** *(slang)* | To carry out tasks or criminal acts on behalf of a group. |

---

## R

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Rat** *(slang)* | A person who informs to police, betrays their group, or cooperates with law enforcement. |
| **Run** *(slang)* | A trip or delivery, usually of illegal goods. "Making a run tonight." |

---

## S

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **The Scene** | Any ongoing or significant event — a crime scene, an active situation, a gathering. |
| **Set** *(slang)* | A gang's territory or specific sub-group within a larger gang. |
| **Shake** *(slang)* | To be searched by police, or to give someone a fright. "Got shook at a checkpoint." |
| **Shorty** *(slang)* | A young or junior member of a group. |
| **Skim** *(slang)* | To take a small cut of money off the top, usually without authorization. |
| **Spot** *(slang)* | A location used for a specific purpose — a drug corner, a meeting place, a stash location. |
| **Strapped** *(slang)* | Armed — carrying a firearm. |

---

## T

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **The Talk** | The social conversation happening on the street — rumors, word of mouth, IC information spread. "Word on the street is..." |
| **Ten-toes** *(slang)* | On foot, walking. "I'm ten-toes to the spot." |
| **Territory** | An area claimed and controlled by a gang or faction. Entering someone else's territory without permission invites conflict. |
| **Trap** *(slang)* | A location used for drug sales or criminal operations. Also used as a verb: "trapping." |

---

## U

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Under the radar** *(slang)* | Operating without attracting police or rival attention. |
| **Uptown** | The wealthier northern districts — Vinewood, Rockford Hills, the hills area. |

---

## V

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Vest** *(slang)* | Body armor. "He was vested up." |
| **Vic** | Victim. Used casually by police and criminals alike. |

---

## W

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Washed** *(slang)* | Money that has been laundered — cleaned of its criminal origin. |
| **Watching the block** | Standing guard over a territory or stash location. |
| **Work** *(slang)* | Drugs prepared for sale. "He had work on him." |
