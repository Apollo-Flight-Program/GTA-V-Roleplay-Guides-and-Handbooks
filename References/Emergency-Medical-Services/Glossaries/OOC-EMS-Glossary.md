# Emergency Medical Services — Out-of-Character Glossary

Terms that **players** use when discussing EMS roleplay outside of character — in Discord, OOC channels, and community discussions. Covers the mechanics, systems, and conventions of playing a medic in GTA V roleplay.

---

## Whitelisting and Roles

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Whitelisted EMS** | An EMS character that requires an approved application. The player must meet activity requirements and maintain role standards. |
| **EMT** | Emergency Medical Technician — an entry-level EMS role. First on scene, basic life support only. |
| **Paramedic** | A higher-level EMS role with advanced treatment options. Usually requires additional training or time in service. |
| **Supervisor / Field Supervisor** | A senior EMS character who oversees units on scene and handles escalated situations. |
| **Ride-along / Observer** | A non-EMS player joining an EMS unit for scene observation without clinical authority. |
| **Chief / Director** | The highest command role within the EMS faction, responsible for department policy. |
| **Duty roster** | The list of EMS members currently on active duty for a given shift. |

## In-Game Mechanics

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Revive / Revive script** | The in-game mechanic by which an EMS player brings a downed player back to a playable state. Usually requires proximity and animation. |
| **Downed player** | A player whose character has been incapacitated and is waiting on the ground for EMS response. |
| **White screen** | The state a player experiences when their character is critically downed — screen turns white as they bleed out. |
| **Bleed-out timer** | The countdown before a downed player is forced to respawn at a hospital if no EMS arrives. |
| **Hospital TP** | The mechanic that teleports a player to a hospital when they respawn from incapacitation. |
| **Self-revive / Auto-revive** | Respawning without EMS intervention — usually means the player chose to not wait or bled out. |
| **Pen / Injection** | The in-game item (EpiPen, syringe) used to perform a revive or stabilize a patient. |
| **Medical bag** | The in-game item that stores medical supplies required for treatments. |
| **Stitch / Bandage** | In-game healing item used for minor wounds between scenes. |
| **Splint** | In-game item for treating leg injuries that prevent a player from running. |

## Scene Management

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Scene safety** | OOC check that the area is controlled before EMS enters — especially relevant when LEO haven't secured the scene yet. |
| **Staging** | EMS waiting at a safe distance until LEO confirms the scene is safe to enter. |
| **Mass casualty** | A scene with multiple downed players — requires triage and coordination between EMS units. |
| **Priority call** | A high-urgency dispatch requiring immediate EMS response, usually Code 3. |
| **Bus call** | Any call dispatched to EMS requiring ambulance response. |
| **DOA RP** | Roleplaying a character as deceased on scene — the EMS player performs examination and documentation rather than reviving. |
| **Trauma RP** | Sustained, detailed medical roleplay at a scene — treating injuries step by step rather than a quick revive. |
| **Passive revive** | A quick, minimal RP revive used when neither party wants a detailed medical scene. Usually only appropriate when time is short. |
| **Full trauma** | A detailed revive RP — full assessment, treatment narrated via /me and /do, proper packaging and transport. |

## Documentation and Communication

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **PCR (Patient Care Report)** | The written record of a patient encounter, treatments, and transport. In-game equivalent is a written RP report filed after a scene. |
| **CAD entry** | Logging the call in the Computer Aided Dispatch system — required by most servers for active EMS. |
| **Tone out** | A notification in Discord or CAD alerting on-duty EMS to a new call. |
| **Going out of service (OOS)** | EMS player marking themselves unavailable — used when taking a break or ending a session. |
| **Return to service (RTS)** | EMS player marking themselves available again after a call or break. |
| **Mutual aid** | Requesting assistance from another EMS unit when current resources are overwhelmed. |
