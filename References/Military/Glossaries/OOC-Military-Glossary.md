# Military — Out-of-Character Glossary

Terms used by **players** when discussing military roleplay outside of character. Covers the conventions, mechanics, and community concepts specific to playing a military character on an RP server.

---

## Roles and Whitelisting

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Military RP / MilRP** | Roleplay centered on military characters and operations. Requires strict realism, chain of command adherence, and formal protocols. |
| **Whitelisted military** | A military role that requires an approved application. Usually involves background checks, interviews, and probationary periods IC. |
| **SAMAF** | San Andreas Military Armed Forces — the in-universe military organization for GTA V RP servers in San Andreas. |
| **National Guard** | A semi-military, semi-civilian force that can be activated by the governor. Often has dual-role players (civilian + military). |
| **Active duty** | A player whose character serves full-time in the military. |
| **Reserve** | A military character who is part-time — has a civilian character as primary and military as secondary. |
| **Joint operations** | A mission involving both military and civilian law enforcement (LSPD, BCSO) working together. |
| **PCS (Permanent Change of Station)** | An IC term for a character being transferred to a new duty station. Often used OOC to explain a player changing from one military sub-faction to another. |
| **Enlistment / Commission** | How a character enters military service. Enlisted = lower ranks. Commissioned = officer track. |

## Operations and Events

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Military event / Op** | A planned, GM or faction-leader-run operation with structure, objectives, and participants. |
| **FTX (Field Training Exercise)** | A training event held in the field rather than a classroom. Used IC and OOC to describe in-world training RP. |
| **War games** | A large training simulation involving opposing forces. |
| **CAS (Close Air Support)** | Air assets (jets, helicopters) supporting ground troops. An event type requiring coordination between air and ground units. |
| **MEDEVAC scene** | A coordinated EMS and military RP scene — a military casualty requiring helicopter medical extraction. |
| **AAR (After Action Review)** | A structured debrief after an operation — what happened, what worked, what needs improvement. Used OOC for event reviews and IC as formal debriefs. |
| **Stand-to** | An alert status event — all military personnel called to positions. Often used to initiate a large-scale event. |
| **Deployment** | An IC event where military characters are sent to a specific mission location. |

## Conduct and Standards

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Chain of command enforcement** | The expectation that orders flow properly through hierarchy — players must not bypass their superiors OOC or IC. |
| **Dress uniform** | Formal IC attire for ceremonies, official events, and formal inspections. |
| **PT (Physical Training)** | IC morning exercise RP, often used to build camaraderie and fill passive time on base. |
| **FOB RP** | Roleplay centered around life on base — maintenance, drills, administration, social time. |
| **Saluting** | An IC show of respect toward superior officers. Usually done with a /me command and verbal acknowledgment. |
| **At ease / Parade rest** | Formal standing positions — used during formal inspections and command addresses. |
| **Insubordination** | An IC disciplinary offense — disobeying orders or showing disrespect to a superior. |
| **UCMJ (Uniform Code of Military Justice)** | The body of law governing military conduct. Adapted IC as the rules and discipline system for the military faction. |
| **Article 15 / NJP** | Non-Judicial Punishment — disciplinary action handled within the military without a formal court-martial. |
| **Honorable discharge** | An IC character leaving military service in good standing. |
| **Dishonorable discharge** | An IC character expelled from military service due to misconduct. |
