# Military — In-Character Glossary

Language used by **military characters** in the world of Los Santos. These are the words, phrases, and abbreviations used by SAMAF personnel, National Guard, and any military faction operating on the server. This covers ground forces, combined arms, and command terminology.

---

## General Military Language

### Rank and Hierarchy

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **The CO / Commanding Officer** | The officer in command of the unit, operation, or installation. |
| **The XO / Executive Officer** | Second in command. Runs day-to-day operations; takes command in the CO's absence. |
| **The TOC / Tactical Operations Center** | The command hub where leadership coordinates operations. |
| **NCOIC** | Non-commissioned officer in charge. The senior NCO responsible for a section or task. |
| **The Chain** | The chain of command — the hierarchy of authority from highest to lowest. "Take it up the chain." |
| **Higher** | Levels of command above your unit. "Higher wants this resolved by end of day." |
| **The Brass** | Senior officers — majors and above. Refers to their rank insignia. |
| **The Old Man** | Informal reference to the commanding officer. |
| **Top** | The unit's senior NCO — typically a First Sergeant. "Ask Top about the vehicle rotation." |

### Time and Location

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Zero-dark-thirty** | Very early morning hours, usually pre-dawn. Implies an early-start or covert operation. |
| **Zulu time / UTC** | Coordinated Universal Time — the military standard for synchronizing operations across locations. |
| **Local** | The local time zone of the current area of operations. "What's that in local?" |
| **Grid** | A map coordinate reference. "Meet at grid 4-7-2-8." |
| **AO (Area of Operations)** | The geographic zone where a unit is assigned to operate. |
| **FOB (Forward Operating Base)** | A temporary, secure base established in the field closer to the operational area. |
| **RV Point / Rally Point** | A designated location where units gather after an operation or if separated. |
| **Exfil point** | The location for extraction after an operation. |
| **LZ (Landing Zone)** | A cleared area for helicopter insertion or extraction. |
| **DZ (Drop Zone)** | An area designated for parachute or equipment drops. |
| **Objective** | The specific task, location, or target for a mission. "Objective is secured." |
| **Perimeter** | The defensive boundary around an area. "Set up a perimeter." |

### Operations Language

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Op / Operation** | A planned military mission or task. |
| **OPORD (Operations Order)** | A five-paragraph field order outlining: Situation, Mission, Execution, Support, Command/Signal. |
| **FRAGO (Fragmentary Order)** | A quick update to an existing order — changes to plan in the field. |
| **ROE (Rules of Engagement)** | The specific rules under which force may or may not be used. |
| **ROC (Rules of Conduct)** | Behavioral standards for personnel during operations. |
| **SITREP (Situation Report)** | A brief update on current status. "Give me a SITREP." |
| **CASREP (Casualty Report)** | A report of injured or killed personnel. |
| **MEDEVAC** | Medical evacuation — helicopter or vehicle extraction of wounded personnel to a medical facility. |
| **CASEVAC** | Casualty evacuation — any evacuation of casualties, not necessarily by dedicated medevac assets. |
| **EXFIL** | Exfiltration — leaving the area of operations, especially covertly. |
| **INFIL** | Infiltration — entering the area of operations, especially covertly. |
| **Boots on the ground** | Infantry or ground forces physically present in the AO. |
| **Actions on** | What to do if a specific event occurs. "What are your actions on contact?" |
| **Contact / Taking contact** | Engaging or being engaged by a hostile force. "We've got contact from the north." |
| **Broken contact** | Successfully disengaging from an enemy engagement. |
| **White flag / Surrender** | Cessation of hostilities by one side. |
| **Cease fire** | An order to stop firing immediately. |
| **Weapons free** | Authorization to engage any confirmed hostile target. |
| **Weapons tight** | Fire only at confirmed hostile targets — positive identification required. |
| **Weapons hold** | Do not fire unless fired upon or unless ordered. |

### Tactical Language (Ground)

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Bounding overwatch** | A movement technique where one element moves while another provides covering fire. |
| **Flanking** | Attacking from the side rather than the front of an enemy position. |
| **Envelopment** | Surrounding an enemy position to cut off escape and attack from multiple directions. |
| **Suppress** | Use fire to prevent the enemy from moving or shooting effectively. |
| **Advance to contact** | Move toward the enemy until contact is made. |
| **Consolidate** | Reorganize after an objective is taken — account for personnel, redistribute ammunition, establish security. |
| **Exploitation** | Taking advantage of a success to press further. |
| **Phase line** | A geographic line used to coordinate movement. Units report when they cross each phase line. |
| **Breach** | Forcibly entering a fortified position or obstacle. |
| **Clear** | Systematically removing hostile forces from a building or area. |
| **Secure** | Hold a position after clearing it. "The building is clear and secured." |
| **Link up** | Two separated units physically meeting at a planned point. |

### Communication

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Net / Radio net** | The radio frequency or channel a unit operates on. "Are you on the command net?" |
| **Traffic** | Radio transmissions. "I've got traffic for you — stand by." |
| **Flash** | The highest-priority radio traffic. Interrupts all other communication. |
| **Encode / Decode** | Using and interpreting coded communication to prevent interception. |
| **Authentication** | Verifying the identity of a station on the radio. |
| **COMMS check** | Testing that communications equipment is working. |

## Common Slang

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Bird** | A helicopter. "We've got a bird inbound." |
| **Track** | An armored vehicle. |
| **Kit** | Equipment and gear. "Full kit for this op." |
| **Ammo** | Ammunition. |
| **Mag** | A magazine for a weapon. |
| **Draw** | Going to the armory to pick up issued weapons or equipment. |
| **In the field** | Away from base, on an active assignment. |
| **Downrange** | In the operational zone, away from base and safety. |
| **Stand to** | Alert status — all personnel at their fighting positions. |
| **Stand down** | End of alert — return to normal readiness. |
| **Hurry up and wait** | The military experience of rushing to a location only to wait for something to happen. Universally understood humor. |
