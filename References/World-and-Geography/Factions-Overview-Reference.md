# Factions Overview Reference

A reference guide to the major faction types present in a GTA V roleplay server. Covers each faction's purpose, IC identity, interaction style, and their relationships to other factions in the server ecosystem.

---

## What Is a Faction?

A faction is any organized group of characters operating with a shared identity, hierarchy, and purpose. Factions create the structural backbone of a server's living world. They interact, compete, collaborate, and create the stories that other players experience.

---

## Law Enforcement Factions

### LSPD — Los Santos Police Department
**Role:** Patrol, criminal investigation, and emergency response within city limits.
**IC Identity:** Professional, uniformed law enforcement. The most visible government presence in the city.
**Hierarchy:** Chief → Deputy Chief → Commanders → Captains → Lieutenants → Sergeants → Officers
**Primary Interaction:** Responds to calls, conducts traffic stops, investigates crimes, arrests suspects, and testifies in court.
**Relationship to Other Factions:**
- Criminal factions: adversarial by nature; investigation and arrest are the core dynamic
- EMS/Fire: cooperative; shares scenes regularly
- Government: reports to the Mayor and City Council through the Chief
- Military: rarely interact; military has separate jurisdiction

---

### BCSO — Blaine County Sheriff's Office
**Role:** Law enforcement for Blaine County's unincorporated areas.
**IC Identity:** Rural patrol officers; close to the community; stretched thin geographically.
**Hierarchy:** Sheriff (elected) → Undersheriff → Lieutenants → Deputies
**Primary Interaction:** Rural patrol, highway enforcement, search and rescue in backcountry.
**Relationship to Other Factions:**
- LSPD: mutual aid partner; may assist in each other's jurisdictions
- Criminal factions operating in rural areas: the primary LEO adversary for those crews
- Sandy Shores residents: often community RP interaction

---

### SASP — San Andreas State Police
**Role:** Statewide law enforcement; highway patrol; major crimes.
**IC Identity:** Elite statewide presence; uniform and professional; often investigative role.
**Hierarchy:** Superintendent → Deputy Superintendents → Majors → Captains → Lieutenants → Troopers

---

## Emergency Services Factions

### LSFD — Los Santos Fire Department
**Role:** Fire suppression, rescue, and supporting EMS at major incidents.
**IC Identity:** Skilled rescue professionals; close-knit crew culture; station-based operations.
**Hierarchy:** Fire Chief → Deputy Chiefs → Captains → Lieutenants → Firefighters / Paramedics

### EMS — Emergency Medical Services
**Role:** Emergency medical response; patient transport; trauma and medical scene management.
**IC Identity:** Clinical and mission-focused; the people who run toward every kind of chaos.
**Hierarchy:** EMS Director → Medical Director → Supervisors → Paramedics → EMTs

---

## Military Faction

### SAMAF — San Andreas Military Forces
**Role:** National defense; restricted installation security; deployment during declared emergencies.
**IC Identity:** Disciplined, structured, formal chain of command. Operate under rules of engagement.
**Hierarchy:** Generals → Colonels → Majors → Captains → Lieutenants → Sergeants → Enlisted
**Primary Interaction:**
- Operate primarily from Fort Zancudo
- Activated by the Governor or State for declared emergencies
- Do NOT intervene in civilian law enforcement outside declared emergencies
- Aviation assets may support LEO by request

---

## Criminal Factions

### Street Gangs
**Role:** Territory control, drug distribution, street-level crime.
**IC Identity:** Neighborhood-based; identity tied to specific blocks, colors, signs.
**Hierarchy:** OG / Shot Caller → Lieutenants → Members → Prospects / Associates
**Primary Interaction:**
- Territorial conflict with rival gangs
- Street-level drug trade (buying from up-chain, selling to public)
- Adversarial with LEO patrol

---

### Organized Crime (Mafia / Crime Family)
**Role:** High-level criminal enterprise; legitimate businesses as fronts; city-wide influence.
**IC Identity:** Old-world structure; values loyalty, protocol, respect.
**Hierarchy:** Boss → Underboss → Consigliere → Capos → Soldiers → Associates
**Primary Interaction:**
- Business and political corruption
- Controls or taxes street-level criminal operations
- Adversarial with LEO at the investigative level; avoids direct confrontation
- May have IC contacts within government and law enforcement

---

### Cartel / International Organization
**Role:** Drug trafficking and importation; supply chain for other criminal factions.
**IC Identity:** Violent, hierarchical, international reach.
**Hierarchy:** Patrón / Cartel Leader → Regional Bosses → Plaza Bosses → Sicarios / Enforcers → Lookouts
**Primary Interaction:**
- Supply source for street gangs and mafia operations
- Operates through the port and smuggling routes
- Extreme violence is the enforcement mechanism

---

### Independent Criminals / Fixers
**Role:** Individual characters who operate outside organized structure.
**IC Identity:** Freelancers; hired for specific jobs; often serve as connective tissue between factions.
**Interaction:** Work with any faction depending on profit; no faction loyalty.

---

## Civilian Factions

### Businesses
**Role:** Create economic activity; serve as social hubs; some operate as criminal fronts.
**IC Identity:** Varies by type; professional environments.
**Interaction:** Interact with all factions — customers from every walk of life.

### Government / Political Faction
**Role:** Legislative, executive, and administrative power.
**IC Identity:** Bureaucratic or elected officials; public servants or corrupt politicians.
**Interaction:** Creates the policy environment everyone operates in.

### Media
**Role:** Document and broadcast events; hold power accountable; drive information.
**IC Identity:** Journalists, reporters, broadcasters.
**Interaction:** Interacts with all factions; has adversarial relationship with those who prefer secrecy.
