# World and Geography — In-Character Glossary

How **characters in Los Santos talk about their city** — the neighborhoods, landmarks, and places that make up daily life. These are the nicknames, local names, and geographic vocabulary that residents use naturally.

---

## Neighborhoods and Districts

| IC Name | What It Refers To |
|---|---|
| **Downtown / The Row** | The Pillbox Hill and Mission Row area — civic center, police station, courthouse, banks. The heart of the city. |
| **The Hills** | Vinewood Hills and Rockford Hills — wealthy residential area, mansions, old money. |
| **Vinewood** | The entertainment district. Celebrities, clubs, the Walk of Fame. Hollywood equivalent. |
| **The South Side / South LS** | Davis, Strawberry, and Chamberlain Hills. Working-class and gang-heavy areas. |
| **Vespucci** | The coastal neighborhood near the beach — boardwalk, bars, tourism. |
| **Del Perro** | The upscale coastal district west of Vespucci. Pier, luxury apartments. |
| **La Mesa / The Mesa** | Industrial and commercial area east of downtown — warehouses, rail yard, chop shop territory. |
| **El Burro** | El Burro Heights — industrial east side. Docks-adjacent, working class. |
| **The Port / The Terminal** | The Port of Los Santos and Elysian Island — shipping docks, industrial, smuggling. |
| **LSIA** | Los Santos International Airport and the surrounding terminal area. |
| **Little Seoul** | The Korean district west of downtown — restaurants, businesses. |
| **Palomino** | Palomino Highlands — suburban, east of the city proper. |
| **Sandy** | Sandy Shores — the desert community near the Alamo Sea. Methamphetamine territory, off-grid living. |
| **Grapeseed** | Agricultural community near the Alamo Sea. Farming, quiet, isolated. |
| **Paleto** | Paleto Bay — the northernmost settlement. Small town, logging, Blaine County culture. |
| **The Alamo** | The Alamo Sea — the desert lake area. Isolated, lawless reputation. |
| **The Mountains** | Mount Chiliad and surrounding wilderness — remote, hiking, the cable car, legends. |
| **Blaine County / The County** | The rural area outside Los Santos proper — Sandy Shores, Grapeseed, Paleto. Often contrasted with "the city." |

## Landmarks and Locations

| IC Name | What It Refers To |
|---|---|
| **The Precinct / Mission Row** | LSPD Mission Row Police Station — the main police hub. |
| **Pillbox** | Pillbox Hill Medical Center — the main hospital. |
| **The Maze Bank** | Maze Bank Tower — the tallest building downtown. A status symbol. |
| **The Bank** | Fleeca Bank branches scattered across the city — common robbery targets. Also Pacific Standard downtown. |
| **The Court** | The Rockford Hills courthouse — where legal proceedings occur. |
| **The Palms** | The Rockford Hills residential enclave — gated, wealthy, guarded. |
| **The Pier** | Del Perro Pier — amusement area, social gathering spot. |
| **The Promenade / The Boardwalk** | The Vespucci Beach boardwalk — vendors, joggers, tourists. |
| **The Grove** | A road or neighborhood depending on server lore — often associated with gang history. |
| **The Alley** | Any back alley — used to mean an off-the-record, unseen location. "Meet me in the alley." |
| **The Warehouse** | Any large industrial storage building — used for criminal operations, stash locations. |
| **The Facility / The Fort** | Fort Zancudo — military installation. Restricted access. "Don't go near the fort." |
| **The Strip** | The main commercial or entertainment street of a district — context dependent. |
| **Rockford** | Rockford Hills, particularly its commercial strip and upscale residences. |

## Geography and Navigation

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Uptown** | The northern, wealthier districts — Rockford Hills, Vinewood, the Hills. |
| **Across town** | On the other side of the city — implies a significant distance. |
| **The highway** | The freeway network running through and around Los Santos. |
| **Off the grid** | In a remote, unwatched location — Sandy Shores, the mountains, the desert. |
| **The desert** | The inland dry areas of Blaine County — associated with isolation and criminal activity. |
| **The coast** | The Pacific Ocean shoreline — Del Perro, Vespucci, Pacific Bluffs. |
| **The outskirts** | The edges of the city proper — transitional areas between urban and rural. |
| **Out of county** | Beyond Blaine County — not a location your character should need to reference in normal RP. |
