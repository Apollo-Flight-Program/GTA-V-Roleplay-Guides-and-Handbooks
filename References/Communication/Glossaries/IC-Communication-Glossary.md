# IC Communication Glossary

Terms characters use over radio, telephone, and in-person when referring to communication procedures, equipment, and protocol. These are the words that come out of a character's mouth — not the words players use when talking about the game.

---

## Radio Terms

| **Term** | Definition |
|---|---|
| **The air / the radio** | The shared radio channel currently in use. *"Keep it off the air."* |
| **On the air** | Currently broadcasting; can be heard by all monitoring that channel. |
| **Go to tac** | Switch to a tactical/secondary channel away from main dispatch. |
| **Break** | Interrupting ongoing traffic to transmit an urgent message. *"Break, break — shots fired."* |
| **Clear** | Finished with a transmission; channel is free. |
| **Copy** | Acknowledged; message received and understood. |
| **Say again** | Please repeat your last transmission. |
| **Say your location** | State your current position. |
| **Loud and clear / Lima Charlie** | Excellent signal and clarity. |
| **Broken / you're broken up** | Signal is intermittent or audio is degraded. |
| **Last** | Refers to the previous transmission. *"Confirm your last."* |
| **Static** | Interference on the channel. Metaphorically: resistance or trouble. |
| **The wire** | Covert listening device. *"The feds have us on the wire."* |
| **Scrambled** | Encrypted transmission; cannot be easily intercepted. |
| **Frequency** | The specific radio channel or band. |
| **All units** | Broadcast directed to everyone on that channel. |
| **Secure channel** | Private frequency not monitored by outsiders. |

---

## Telephone Terms

| **Term** | Definition |
|---|---|
| **The phone** | Any mobile device. |
| **Burner** | Prepaid, disposable phone not linked to a real identity. |
| **Landline** | Fixed telephone line. |
| **Encrypted line** | A phone call using a scrambled or secured app — harder to tap. |
| **Burn it** | Dispose of a phone to prevent tracing. |
| **Reach out** | Make initial contact with someone. |
| **Touch base** | Brief, non-critical contact to maintain connection. |
| **The line is hot** | The phone or line is being monitored. |
| **Don't say names** | Instruction to avoid identifying information on a call. |

---

## In-Person Meeting Terms

| **Term** | Definition |
|---|---|
| **A meet** | A prearranged in-person meeting. |
| **Face to face** | Meeting in person rather than by phone or radio. |
| **The sit-down** | A formal meeting between factions or leadership to resolve disputes. |
| **Neutral ground** | Location where neither party has a home advantage. |
| **Eyes on** | Someone is physically watching the location. |
| **Countersurveillance** | Checking whether you are being followed before a meeting. |
| **Clean** | Not being followed; no tail. *"You clean?"* |
| **Dead drop** | Covert message or item exchange at a prearranged location without meeting. |
| **The cutout** | An intermediary passing messages between two parties who cannot safely meet. |
| **Pass the word** | Spread a message informally through a network. |

---

## Coded Language

| **Term** | Definition |
|---|---|
| **The package** | A person, item, or shipment discussed without specifics. |
| **Merchandise** | Contraband or goods being handled discreetly. |
| **Our friend** | A mutual contact or third party. Avoids naming them. |
| **The other thing** | A specific action too sensitive to name directly. |
| **Business** | Criminal operations or deals — used neutrally over monitored channels. |
| **The usual spot** | An understood prearranged meeting location, not stated aloud. |
