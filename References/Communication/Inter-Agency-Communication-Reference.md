# Inter-Agency Communication Reference

A guide for coordinating between different departments and agencies during multi-faction roleplay scenes. Covers shared channels, joint operation protocols, and communication etiquette when LSPD, BCSO, EMS, Fire, and other agencies work a scene together.

---

## Agency Communication Overview

Each agency maintains its own primary radio channels. During joint operations, agencies use designated shared or "tac" (tactical) channels to coordinate without cluttering their internal comms.

| Agency | Primary Channel | Common Abbreviation |
|---|---|---|
| Los Santos Police Department | LSPD Primary | P1 |
| Blaine County Sheriff's Office | BCSO Primary | S1 |
| San Andreas State Police | SASP Primary | SASP |
| Los Santos Fire Department | LSFD Primary | F1 |
| Emergency Medical Services | EMS Primary | E1 |
| San Andreas Military Forces | SAMAF Command | MIL |
| Los Santos Customs / Port Authority | Customs Channel | PORT |

## Shared Tactical Channels

When multiple agencies respond to the same scene, a shared tac channel is used.

| Channel | Use |
|---|---|
| **TAC 1** | Default joint operations channel (LEO + EMS) |
| **TAC 2** | Large-scale events involving 3+ agencies |
| **TAC 3** | Covert or specialized task force operations |
| **FIRE/EMS Bridge** | Fire and EMS coordination at mass casualty events |

## Chain of Command in Joint Operations

When multiple agencies are on scene, **Incident Command** protocols clarify who is in charge.

### Incident Command (IC) System

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Incident Commander (IC)** | Single authority over all responders at the scene. Typically the highest-ranking official of the primary agency. |
| **Operations Section Chief** | Manages all tactical operations under the IC. |
| **Logistics Section Chief** | Manages resources, equipment, staging. |
| **Information Officer (PIO)** | Handles media and public information. |
| **Safety Officer** | Monitors scene for hazards; authority to halt operations for safety. |

**Who becomes IC:**
- At crime scenes: LSPD or BCSO, depending on jurisdiction
- At fires or mass casualty incidents: LSFD becomes IC; EMS falls under LSFD ICS
- At military operations on civilian property: LEO IC, military advisory role
- At multi-jurisdiction scenes: The agency with primary jurisdiction, or highest rank present

## Scene Coordination Procedures

### When Multiple Agencies Arrive

1. First unit on scene establishes IC and broadcasts size-up on shared channel
2. Incoming units acknowledge and stage until directed
3. IC assigns staging areas for each agency
4. Each agency lead checks in with IC on TAC channel
5. IC assigns functional zones (hot zone, warm zone, cold zone / perimeter)

### Zone Definitions

| Zone | Access | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Hot Zone (Inner Perimeter)** | LEO, SWAT/tactical only | Active threat area |
| **Warm Zone (Treatment/Staging)** | EMS with escort, tactical medics | Casualty treatment staging |
| **Cold Zone (Outer Perimeter)** | All agencies, civilians blocked | Command post, media, resources |

## Communication Protocols on Shared Channels

### What to Say on Shared Channels

- Scene updates affecting multiple agencies
- Resource requests (requesting additional units from another agency)
- Zone changes (opening access for EMS)
- Casualty counts for multi-agency awareness
- Significant tactical changes (suspect in custody, scene secured)

### What NOT to Say on Shared Channels

- Internal agency traffic (that goes on your primary channel)
- Routine status updates unrelated to the joint scene
- Personal radio chatter or non-essential commentary

## Inter-Agency Handoffs

### Law Enforcement → EMS Patient Handoff

When LEO hands a patient to EMS:

1. LEO clears or secures the patient (no threat)
2. LEO gives EMS the briefing: **"Patient is a [M/F], approximately [age], [mechanism of injury], [status — conscious/unconscious, responsive/unresponsive], scene is secure."**
3. EMS acknowledges and assumes patient care
4. LEO maintains scene security while EMS works

### EMS → Hospital Handoff

When EMS arrives at LSMC:

1. EMS gives receiving team: **"[Gender/age] arrived via ambulance, mechanism of injury [what happened], vitals [blood pressure, pulse, GCS], treatments rendered en route [list], current status [stable/critical]."**
2. Hospital staff assume care
3. EMS documents the run

### LEO → Fire Scene Handoff

At active fire scenes:

1. LEO establishes outer perimeter and crowd control
2. LSFD IC assumes overall scene command
3. LEO holds perimeter, does not enter structure without LSFD clearance
4. Shared tac channel for zone coordination

## Requesting Aid from Another Agency

### Request Format (Radio)

**"[Your agency/unit] to [Target agency], requesting [what you need] at [location]."**

Examples:
- *"Unit 4-Adam-12 to EMS, requesting one ambulance at Strawberry Ave and Davis, one patient."*
- *"LSPD dispatch to BCSO, requesting K-9 unit at [location] for track and search."*
- *"LSFD IC to LSPD, requesting perimeter extension to include the east parking lot."*

### Acknowledgement

Target agency acknowledges and states ETA or availability.

## Common Inter-Agency Scenarios

### High-Speed Pursuit Crossing Jurisdictions
- Originating agency maintains primary responsibility
- Entering agency notifies dispatch on their channel
- Both agencies coordinate on TAC or shared dispatch

### Active Shooter
- LEO clears hot zone; EMS stages at cold zone
- Tactical medics (if available) may enter warm zone
- LSFD on standby for trauma / fire hazard
- Single IC (LEO) coordinates all movement into and out of hot zone

### Mass Casualty Incident (MCI)
- LSFD assumes IC role
- EMS implements START triage at warm zone
- LEO holds outer perimeter, manages crowd, assists with casualty tracking
- Hospitals notified for surge preparation

### Pursuit Ending in Crash
- LEO secures scene and manages traffic
- EMS treats injured occupants
- LSFD for vehicle fires or extrication
- LEO handles evidence collection and accident report

## Communication Glossary (Inter-Agency)

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Tac channel** | Tactical channel used for a specific operation |
| **Scene secure** | Threat neutralized — safe for EMS/Fire to enter |
| **Stage** | Hold position nearby but do not commit to scene yet |
| **Code 4** | No further assistance needed |
| **Go mutual** | Switch to shared/mutual aid channel |
| **ICS** | Incident Command System |
| **NIMS** | National Incident Management System (the overarching framework) |
