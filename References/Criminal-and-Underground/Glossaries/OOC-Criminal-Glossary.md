# Criminal and Underground — Out-of-Character Glossary

Terms used by **players** when discussing criminal roleplay outside of character. Covers the conventions, mechanics, and community concepts unique to playing a criminal character on an RP server.

---

## Role Types and Application

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Criminal RP / CrimRP** | Roleplay centered around criminal characters — gangs, organized crime, individual criminals. |
| **Gang RP** | Criminal RP focused on street gangs — territory, rivalry, drug operations, internal hierarchy. |
| **Cartel RP** | Organized crime at a higher level — supply chains, political corruption, large-scale operations. |
| **Mafia / Mob RP** | Structured criminal organization RP with strict hierarchy, business fronts, and long-term power plays. |
| **Whitelisted criminal** | A criminal faction or role that requires an approved application — usually for organized crime groups with defined membership and territory. |
| **Unwhitelisted criminal** | Any player can play a criminal character — no application required. Most common for street-level crime. |
| **Independent criminal** | A criminal character not affiliated with any organized faction. Operates alone or in loose groups. |

## Server Rules and Crime Mechanics

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Priority / Active priority** | A major criminal scene that takes up a server priority slot — usually a bank robbery, hostage situation, or organized heist. Most servers limit simultaneous priorities. |
| **Priority cooldown** | The waiting period after a priority scene ends before the same group can initiate another. |
| **Initiation** | The in-game action or statement that gives you IC justification to become hostile toward another player. Must occur before violence. |
| **Robbery rules** | Server-specific rules about how robberies must be conducted — how many players, what can be taken, time limits, etc. |
| **Pocket wipe** | Taking all items from a downed or robbed player. Usually limited by server rules — you may only take what is relevant to the scene. |
| **Chop shop** | An in-game or RP mechanic for stripping stolen vehicles for parts and money. |
| **Drug script / Drug mechanic** | The server's in-game system for growing, cooking, transporting, and selling drugs. |
| **Money laundering mechanic** | An in-game system that converts dirty money to clean money, usually through a business NPC or interaction. |
| **Carjacking rules** | Rules governing when and how vehicle theft can occur — usually requires RP initiation, no carjacking in greenzone, etc. |

## Roleplay Conventions

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Initiate / Give initiation** | To issue a credible threat that establishes hostile intent — "hands up or you get shot." Required before shooting in most servers. |
| **Fear RP in robbery** | The expectation that a robbery victim behaves realistically — complies when outnumbered or at gunpoint. |
| **Drive-by rules** | Rules governing shooting from a moving vehicle — usually requires a minimum number of participating vehicles or prior engagement. |
| **Stash house RP** | Roleplay involving a character's hidden supply location — counting product, organizing a stash, receiving deliveries. |
| **Business RP (criminal)** | Roleplaying the operational side of criminal enterprise — negotiations, supply chain management, employee management. |
| **Kidnapping rules** | Rules governing when and how a character can be held against their will. Usually requires consent or specific conditions. |
| **Torture RP** | Extended scene involving a captive character. Requires explicit consent from all players and usually a server-specific rule check. |
| **Execution** | Killing a character at the end of a captivity scene. Requires IC justification and often consent from the victim's player. |
| **Betrayal / Defection** | A character switching sides or informing on their organization. One of the most narrative-rich criminal RP scenarios. |
| **Turf war event** | A GM or faction-organized conflict scenario over territory control. |

## Criminal Faction Management (OOC)

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Faction roster** | The list of characters belonging to an organized criminal group. |
| **Chain of command (criminal)** | The hierarchy within a criminal faction. Members are expected to follow it IC. |
| **Faction leader** | The player responsible for managing the criminal organization's roster, standards, and RP direction. |
| **Prospect / Hang-around** | A player whose character is in the entry phase of joining a faction — not yet a full member. |
| **Full member / Made** | A character who has completed initiation and is a full member of the faction. |
| **Kicked / Purged** | A character or player removed from a criminal faction, IC or OOC. |
| **Mergers** | Two criminal factions combining. Usually handled through extended IC negotiations. |
| **War** | A declared IC conflict between two factions — usually governed by server rules about how it escalates and ends. |
