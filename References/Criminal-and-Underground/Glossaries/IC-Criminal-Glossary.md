# Criminal and Underground — In-Character Glossary

The language of the streets, criminal organizations, and the underground economy of Los Santos. These are the words characters use in criminal circles — coded language, slang, gang terminology, and organized crime vocabulary.

This language exists to obscure meaning from outsiders and communicate efficiently within a group. Using it correctly signals belonging and experience. Using it incorrectly or with the wrong crowd signals inexperience — or worse, an undercover cop.

---

## Money and Finance

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Bread / Bands / Paper / Rack** | Money. "We're about to make some serious bread." One band / one rack = $1,000. |
| **Stack** | $1,000 or a large amount of money. "I saved a stack for this." |
| **Blue faces** | $100 bills — modern US bills with the blue security strip. |
| **Dirty money** | Cash obtained through criminal activity that has not been laundered. Cannot be deposited or spent freely without drawing attention. |
| **Clean money** | Laundered cash — money that has passed through a legitimate business and appears legal. |
| **Wash / Launder** | To process dirty money through a front business to make it appear legitimate. |
| **Front** | A legitimate business used to disguise criminal income. "The barbershop is just a front." |
| **The books** | Financial records of a criminal operation. Keeping or cooking the books. |
| **Cut** | A share of profits. "Everyone takes a cut." |
| **Tax** | A fee demanded from businesses or individuals by a gang for "protection" or territory rights. |
| **Skim** | To secretly take a portion of money before it reaches its intended destination. |
| **Juice** | Interest on a loan, especially an illegal one. "The juice on that loan doubles every week." |
| **Loan shark** | A person who provides illegal loans at extremely high interest rates, using violence as enforcement. |

## Drugs

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Work** | Drugs prepared for street sale. "He had work on him when they stopped him." |
| **Product / Merch** | Drugs, treated as a commodity. |
| **Brick** | A compressed kilogram block of drugs — usually cocaine or heroin. |
| **Key / Kilo** | A kilogram of drugs. |
| **Eight ball** | 3.5 grams of cocaine. |
| **Dime / Dime bag** | A $10 quantity of drugs. |
| **Nickel bag** | A $5 quantity of drugs. |
| **Sack** | A quantity of marijuana. |
| **Powder** | Cocaine. |
| **Rock / Crack** | Crack cocaine. |
| **Ice / Crystal / Glass** | Methamphetamine. |
| **Boy** | Heroin. |
| **Pills / Mollies / Beans** | MDMA / ecstasy. |
| **Green / Grass / Herb / Bud** | Marijuana. |
| **Plug / Connect** | A supplier. The person who provides drugs to a dealer. |
| **Flip** | To buy drugs and resell them quickly at a higher price. |
| **Re-up** | Restocking the supply. "I need to re-up before the weekend." |
| **Stash** | Hidden supply. "The stash is in the wall." |
| **Trap house** | A location used as a base for drug sales. |
| **Bust a move** | Go make a transaction. |
| **Runner** | A person who physically transports drugs or money between locations. |

## Weapons

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Strap / Heat / Iron / Tool** | A firearm. "You strapped?" |
| **Steel** | A gun, usually a pistol. |
| **Stick / Drum** | A high-capacity magazine or extended magazine. |
| **Blick / Glizzy** | A handgun, particularly a Glock-style pistol. |
| **Chop / Draco** | An AK-style pistol or short-barrel assault weapon. |
| **Launcher** | A rocket or grenade launcher. |
| **Banger** | A handgun. |
| **Piece** | Any firearm. |
| **Hardware** | Weapons, especially illegal ones. "I know a guy who moves hardware." |

## Gang and Territory

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Set** | A specific sub-group or chapter within a larger gang. |
| **Hood / Block** | A gang's home territory. |
| **Turf** | Controlled territory — where a gang operates and demands respect. |
| **Op / Opp** | An opponent — a rival gang member. |
| **Beef** | A conflict or ongoing rivalry between individuals or groups. |
| **Squabble** | A fight or confrontation. |
| **Slide** | To go to an enemy's territory with the intent of confrontation. "We're gonna slide on them tonight." |
| **Hit / Spin the block** | To drive through or shoot at a rival's territory. |
| **Pull up** | To confront someone directly, usually in an aggressive context. |
| **Jump in / Jump out** | Gang initiation rite — being beaten by members as an entry requirement. |
| **BG (Baby Gangster)** | A new or junior gang member. |
| **OG (Original Gangster)** | A veteran, long-standing senior member of a gang. Commands respect. |
| **Rider** | A member willing to commit violence on behalf of the gang. |
| **Snitch / Rat / Cheese-eater** | Someone who cooperates with police or informs on their group. The worst label in criminal circles. |
| **Green light** | Authorization or order for violence against a specific person. "He's got a green light." |
| **Red light** | A cancellation of a green light. |
| **On sight / OS** | Immediate violence upon seeing a person, no conversation needed. |
| **Dead homie** | A deceased member of the group — referenced in oaths and to explain motivation for conflict. |

## Operations and Crime

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Lick / Move / Job** | A criminal operation — robbery, theft, or deal. "We've got a lick lined up." |
| **Caper** | A robbery or heist. |
| **Score** | A successful theft or robbery haul. "It was a good score." |
| **Kick** | A cut or share of the proceeds. |
| **Boost** | To steal a vehicle. |
| **Clout** | Reputation, influence, and power within criminal circles. |
| **Burn phone** | A disposable phone used briefly and then discarded. |
| **Clean car** | A vehicle with no connection to criminal activity — used for surveillance or getaways. |
| **The spot** | A designated location for a meeting, transaction, or operation. |
| **Counter-surveillance** | Checking if you are being followed or watched before moving to a sensitive location. |
| **Burn (verb)** | To compromise a location, person, or operation. "That spot is burned — police know about it." |
| **Lay low** | To hide and avoid attention until a situation cools down. |
| **Ghost** | To disappear — cut all contact and become unreachable. |
