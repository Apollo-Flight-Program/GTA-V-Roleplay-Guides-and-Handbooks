# NATO Phonetic Alphabet

The NATO phonetic alphabet — formally called the International Radiotelephony Spelling Alphabet — is a system where each letter of the alphabet is replaced by a specific word. It exists to eliminate confusion when spelling out names, license plates, locations, or codes over radio, where similar-sounding letters (like B and D, or M and N) can be easily misheard.

It is used by military forces, law enforcement, aviation, and emergency services worldwide. In GTA V roleplay, using the NATO alphabet on radio immediately elevates the realism and quality of your communications.

## Numbers

Numbers are spoken digit by digit with specific pronunciation to avoid confusion:

| Digit | Pronunciation |
|---|---|
| 0 | Zero |
| 1 | One |
| 2 | Two |
| 3 | Tree |
| 4 | Fower |
| 5 | Fife |
| 6 | Six |
| 7 | Seven |
| 8 | Ait |
| 9 | Niner |

> Note: "Tree" instead of "Three", "Fower" instead of "Four", "Fife" instead of "Five", "Ait" instead of "Eight", and "Niner" instead of "Nine" are deliberate — they reduce confusion with similar-sounding words in other languages and over poor radio quality.

---

## Practical Examples

**Spelling a license plate:**
> *"Suspect vehicle, license plate — Kilo, Alpha, Niner, Romeo, Oscar, Sierra — over."*

**Spelling a name:**
> *"Suspect name — Juliet, Oscar, Hotel, November — over."*

**Giving a callsign:**
> *"This is Unit Bravo-7, en route to the location."*

**Confirming a street:**
> *"Turning onto Sierra, India, Echo, Romeo, Romeo, Alpha street — confirm?"*

---

## In Roleplay

You do not need to use the NATO alphabet for every single word. Use it when:

- Spelling out names, license plates, or codes over radio
- Communicating in noisy or high-stress scenes where clarity matters
- Confirming information that could be misheard

Using it naturally and selectively sounds far more professional than forcing it into every transmission. A real officer does not say "I am heading to Hotel-Oscar-Tango-Echo-Lima" when they can just say "I am heading to the hotel."
