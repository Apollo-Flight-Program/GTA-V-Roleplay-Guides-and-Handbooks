# Media and Journalism — In-Character Glossary

Language used by **journalists, reporters, broadcasters, and media characters** in the world of Los Santos. These are the professional terms used in the newsroom, in the field, and in broadcast — the vocabulary of telling the city's story.

---

## Journalism Fundamentals

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **The Story** | An active piece of reporting being developed. "I'm working a story on the Moretti case." |
| **The Beat** | A journalist's regular area of coverage. A crime reporter "works the crime beat." |
| **Source** | A person who provides information for a story — identified or anonymous. |
| **Deep throat / Confidential source** | A source whose identity is protected. The journalist will not reveal their name. |
| **On the record** | A statement made with the understanding it can be published and attributed. |
| **Off the record** | Information shared with the understanding it will not be published or attributed. |
| **On background** | A statement that can be used in reporting but not attributed by name — "a source familiar with the matter." |
| **Not for attribution** | Similar to background — information can be used but the speaker is not named. |
| **Embargo** | An agreement to delay publishing information until a specified time. "This is under embargo until noon tomorrow." |
| **Exclusive** | A story that only one outlet has — obtained before competitors. High professional value. |
| **Scoop** | Breaking a major story before anyone else. |
| **Tip** | A piece of information provided to a reporter that may lead to a story. |
| **Tip line** | A contact channel for the public to submit tips anonymously. |
| **Freelance** | Working independently for multiple outlets rather than being employed by one organization. |
| **Stringers** | Freelance journalists who cover events for news organizations on a per-story basis. |

## Reporting and Production

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Byline** | The author's name on a published story. "Getting a byline" means having your name on the piece. |
| **Lede / Lead** | The opening line or paragraph of a story — must be compelling and contain the most critical information. |
| **Five Ws** | The core elements of any news story: Who, What, When, Where, Why. Plus How. |
| **Inverted pyramid** | A story structure: most important information first, supporting details second, background last. |
| **Quote** | A direct verbatim statement from a source, set in quotation marks. |
| **Paraphrase** | Summarizing a source's statement in the journalist's own words. |
| **Context** | Background information that helps readers understand the significance of a story. |
| **Attribution** | Identifying who said or provided information. "According to..." |
| **Rebuttal** | A response given by a party that is the subject of negative reporting. Good practice: get the other side. |
| **No comment** | A source declining to speak. Can be reported as a response. "The suspect declined to comment." |
| **Press release** | An official statement distributed by an organization to media. |
| **Copy** | Written news content ready for editing or publication. "I'm filing copy by six." |
| **Deadline** | The latest time copy can be submitted for publication or broadcast. |
| **Killed** | A story that was prepared but not published. "The editor killed the piece." |
| **Spiked** | Same as killed — a story removed from publication. |
| **Above the fold** | The most prominent placement in a newspaper — major story treatment. |
| **Breaking news** | An event currently unfolding that is being reported in real time. |
| **Follow-up** | A subsequent story continuing coverage of an earlier report. |
| **Series** | Multiple related stories published over time on the same subject. |

---

## Broadcasting

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **On air** | Currently broadcasting live. |
| **Off air** | Not currently broadcasting. |
| **Live shot** | A live broadcast from a location other than the studio. |
| **Stand-up** | A reporter speaking directly to the camera at the scene of a story. |
| **Package** | A pre-produced video news story with narration, interviews, and footage. |
| **Anchor** | The studio host who presents the broadcast and tosses to reporters. |
| **Tosses** | The anchor handing over to a reporter on location. "Back to you, Sandra." |
| **IFB** | The earpiece through which anchors and reporters receive direction from the control room. |
| **Two-way / Live hit** | A live interview between a studio anchor and a reporter in the field. |
| **B-roll** | Supplementary footage used over narration — not the main interview or stand-up. |
| **Sound bite / SOT (Sound on Tape)** | A short clip of an interview used in a package. |
| **Chyron / Lower third** | The text graphic at the bottom of the screen identifying a person or situation. |
| **Sign off** | A reporter's closing statement on a live piece. "Sandra Reeves, 5-Star News, downtown Los Santos." |

## Ethics and Standards

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Verification** | Confirming information through at least one independent additional source before publishing. |
| **Retraction** | A published correction or withdrawal of previously reported information that was incorrect. |
| **Defamation** | Publishing false information that harms someone's reputation. |
| **Public interest** | The justification for reporting on private matters — the public's right to know outweighs individual privacy. |
| **Fourth estate** | The press as the fourth pillar of democracy — an independent check on government power. |
