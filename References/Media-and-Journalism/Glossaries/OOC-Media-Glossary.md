# Media and Journalism — Out-of-Character Glossary

Terms used by **players** when discussing media and journalism roleplay outside of character. Covers the conventions, mechanics, and community concepts around playing a journalist, broadcaster, or media figure on an RP server.

---

## Roles and Types

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Media RP / JournalismRP** | Roleplay centered around journalists, news anchors, photographers, and broadcasters covering the city's events. |
| **Press whitelist** | A role requiring application — in some servers, the press badge grants IC access to crime scenes, official events, and government briefings. |
| **Independent journalist** | A player running their own media outlet — blog, paper, or broadcast — without faction membership. |
| **News outlet** | An IC organization with multiple reporters, an editor, and a publication or broadcast channel. |
| **Streamer character** | A player character who streams their life IC — equivalent to a YouTuber or social media personality in-world. |
| **Paparazzi character** | A photographer character who follows famous IC figures — celebrities, politicians, criminals. |
| **Crime journalist** | A reporter specializing in covering criminal incidents, law enforcement operations, and court cases. |
| **Investigative journalist** | A character who conducts deep research over time, seeking to expose wrongdoing. High narrative value, requires sustained RP commitment. |

## Publishing and Broadcasting

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **IC newspaper / IC article** | A written piece published in the server's Discord, forums, or website — formatted as a newspaper article, treated as world lore. |
| **IC broadcast** | A live or pre-recorded video of a news report, usually shared as a clip or hosted on a server-designated platform. |
| **Server lore publication** | Media content that becomes part of the server's official history — referenced by other characters and GMs. |
| **Breaking news post** | A quick IC social media or news post about an event happening right now. |
| **News channel** | A dedicated Discord channel where IC media organizations post their content. |
| **Press pass** | An IC item or server permission that grants access to restricted scenes or government events as media. |
| **Media pool** | Multiple journalists sharing access to a restricted event, with a designated pool reporter sharing content with all. |

## Conduct and Ethics

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **IC/OOC source protection** | Not revealing a source's identity both in character and out of character. Even if asked OOC, a journalist player should not reveal who gave them IC information if it was given confidentially. |
| **Metagaming risk in journalism** | Journalists are particularly vulnerable to metagaming — a reporter should only know things their character could have discovered IC, not things overheard in OOC channels. |
| **Fair game vs. off limits** | Establishing what IC events can be reported on. Active pursuit / chase scenes are typically fair game; private conversations require consent. |
| **Consent for interview** | Asking the other player OOC if they are comfortable with their character being interviewed or quoted in an IC publication. |
| **Defamation rules** | Some servers have rules against IC publications making false factual claims about characters — to prevent media RP being used as harassment. |
| **Editorial standards** | The OOC agreement within a media faction about what their outlet will and will not publish, and how stories are reviewed before release. |

## Community Value of Media RP

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **World documentation** | IC media creates a public record of server events — new players can read past articles to understand history. |
| **Server history** | A published IC article that becomes part of the server's recorded lore, referenced by future RP. |
| **Narrative amplification** | Media RP that takes a player-driven story and broadcasts it to the wider server — increasing awareness of events and inviting more players to engage. |
| **IC reputation system** | Characters who are covered by media become more widely known — criminals become infamous, heroes become celebrated, politicians become accountable. |
