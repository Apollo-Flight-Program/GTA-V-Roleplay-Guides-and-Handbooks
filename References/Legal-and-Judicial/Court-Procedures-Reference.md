# Court Procedures Reference

A guide to how court proceedings are conducted in GTA V roleplay. This covers the sequence of events in criminal and civil cases — from first appearance through trial and sentencing. Use this as a blueprint for organizing and running realistic court RP.

---

## Pre-Trial Phase

### 1. Arrest and Booking
After a character is arrested and processed:
- Officer submits an arrest report with charges, evidence, and booking information
- Defendant is held pending arraignment (unless bail is set)
- Defense counsel is notified or assigned

### 2. Arraignment
**First formal court appearance.**

| Step | What Happens |
|---|---|
| Opening of court | Judge is seated, parties identified, case number called |
| Reading of charges | Prosecutor reads each charge against the defendant |
| Plea entry | Defendant enters a plea: Guilty / Not Guilty / No Contest |
| Bail hearing | Judge sets bail amount or remands defendant in custody |
| Future date set | Next court date is scheduled |

**If defendant pleads Guilty or No Contest:** Proceed directly to Sentencing. No trial required.

**If defendant pleads Not Guilty:** Proceed to Pre-Trial Motions, then Trial.

---

### 3. Discovery
The legal exchange of evidence before trial.

- Prosecution must disclose all evidence they intend to use
- Defense must disclose their witness list and any evidence they plan to introduce
- Both sides review each other's materials
- Neither side is permitted to "surprise" the other at trial with undisclosed evidence

---

### 4. Pre-Trial Motions
Either side may file motions before trial:

| Motion | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Motion to Suppress** | Exclude evidence obtained illegally |
| **Motion to Dismiss** | End the case due to insufficient evidence or procedural error |
| **Motion in Limine** | Prevent specific topics or evidence from being raised at trial |
| **Motion for Continuance** | Postpone the trial date |
| **Motion for Change of Venue** | Move trial to a different location for fair jury selection |

The judge rules on each motion before trial begins.

---

## Trial Phase

### Court Decorum

- All parties stand when the judge enters and exits
- Address the judge as "Your Honor"
- Do not speak without being recognized
- Objections are stated concisely — do not argue; wait for the judge's ruling
- Witnesses do not speak unless directed to

### Jury Selection (Voir Dire)
*If a jury trial is used*

Attorneys for both sides question potential jurors to identify and remove those with potential bias. Each side has unlimited challenges for cause (bias proven) and a limited number of peremptory challenges (no reason required).

---

### Trial Sequence

#### 1. Opening Statements
Each side presents a preview of their case.
- Prosecution goes first
- Defense follows
- Not evidence — a roadmap of what the evidence will show

#### 2. Prosecution's Case-in-Chief
Prosecution presents evidence and calls witnesses.

**Examination sequence:**
1. **Direct examination** — Prosecution questions their own witness
2. **Cross-examination** — Defense questions the witness
3. **Redirect** — Prosecution addresses new issues raised in cross
4. **Recross** — Defense may question again on redirect topics

After all prosecution witnesses: **Prosecution rests.**

*Defense may move to dismiss at this point if prosecution has not met its burden.*

#### 3. Defense's Case-in-Chief
Defense presents evidence and calls witnesses, including optionally calling the defendant.

Same examination sequence applies.

**After all defense witnesses: Defense rests.**

#### 4. Rebuttal
Prosecution may present limited evidence to rebut specific defense claims. Defense may then surrebut.

#### 5. Closing Arguments
Each side summarizes the evidence and argues why the verdict should favor them.
- Prosecution goes first
- Defense follows
- Prosecution has a final rebuttal (they bear the burden of proof)

#### 6. Jury Instructions / Judge's Instructions
The judge explains the law the jury must apply and what standards of proof apply.

---

## Verdict and Sentencing

### Verdict Delivery
- Jury foreman or judge announces the verdict on each count
- **Not Guilty:** Defendant is acquitted — released immediately. Cannot be tried again for the same crime.
- **Guilty:** Proceed to sentencing

### Sentencing Hearing
May occur immediately after verdict or at a scheduled future date.

Both sides may present:
- **Victim impact statements** — statements from those harmed by the crime
- **Character witnesses** — speaking to the defendant's character
- **Sentencing recommendations** — what each side believes is appropriate
- **Mitigating factors** — reasons for a lighter sentence (first offense, cooperation, remorse)
- **Aggravating factors** — reasons for a heavier sentence (prior record, violence, targeting vulnerable victims)

**Judge pronounces sentence:**
- Jail or prison time (concurrent or consecutive on multiple counts)
- Probation
- Fines
- Community service
- Combination of the above

---

## Civil Cases

Civil cases differ from criminal cases:
- Initiated by a **plaintiff** against a **defendant** (not the state vs. defendant)
- Standard of proof: **preponderance of the evidence** (more likely than not) — lower than criminal
- No jail time — remedies are monetary or injunctive (ordering someone to do or stop doing something)
- Cases include: property disputes, contract violations, personal injury claims

### Civil Case Sequence
1. Filing of complaint by plaintiff
2. Service on defendant
3. Defendant's answer (and possibly counterclaim)
4. Discovery
5. Pre-trial motions
6. Trial (same format as criminal, adjusted for civil standard)
7. Verdict and damages award

## Common Objections

| Objection | Basis |
|---|---|
| **Hearsay** | Witness is repeating what someone else said, offered for the truth |
| **Leading** | Attorney is putting the answer in the witness's mouth (only allowed on cross) |
| **Relevance** | The evidence or question does not relate to the case |
| **Speculation** | Witness is guessing rather than speaking from personal knowledge |
| **Badgering the witness** | Attorney is being abusive or repetitive |
| **Compound question** | Two questions asked as one |
| **Assumes facts not in evidence** | Question assumes something hasn't been proven yet |
