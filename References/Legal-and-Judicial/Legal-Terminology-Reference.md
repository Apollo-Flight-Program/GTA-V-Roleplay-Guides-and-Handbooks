# Legal Terminology Reference

A comprehensive glossary of legal terms used in court RP, document drafting, and judicial proceedings. These are the technical terms attorneys, judges, and legal clerks use both in and out of court.

---

## Foundational Legal Concepts

| **Term** | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Burden of proof** | The obligation on a party to prove their claim. In criminal cases, this rests with the prosecution. |
| **Standard of proof** | The level of certainty required. Criminal: beyond a reasonable doubt. Civil: preponderance of the evidence (more likely than not). |
| **Beyond a reasonable doubt** | The highest standard. The jury must be firmly convinced of guilt; not merely probable. |
| **Preponderance of the evidence** | Civil standard. More than 50% likely. |
| **Clear and convincing evidence** | Intermediate civil standard. Higher than preponderance; lower than criminal. |
| **Presumption of innocence** | Every defendant is presumed innocent until proven guilty. The state must overcome this presumption. |
| **Due process** | The right to fair treatment under the law. Procedural due process = proper procedures followed. Substantive due process = laws must be fair. |
| **Equal protection** | All persons must be treated equally under the law. |
| **Jurisdiction** | The court's authority to hear a particular type of case or cases from a specific geographic area. |
| **Venue** | The geographic location of the court hearing the case. |

---

## Parties in a Case

| **Term** | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Plaintiff** | The party who initiates a civil lawsuit. |
| **Defendant** | The party against whom a case is brought. Used in both civil and criminal. |
| **Prosecution / The People / The State** | Government attorney representing the public interest in a criminal case. |
| **Defense** | Attorney representing the defendant. |
| **Petitioner** | Party initiating an appeal or petition. |
| **Respondent** | Party responding to an appeal or petition. |
| **Third party** | A person or entity not directly a party to the case but affected by or involved in it. |
| **Co-defendant** | Two or more defendants charged in the same case. |

## Criminal Law Terms

| **Term** | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Felony** | Serious crime; typically punishable by more than one year in prison. |
| **Misdemeanor** | Lesser offense; typically punishable by fines and/or jail under one year. |
| **Infraction / Violation** | Minor offense; typically a fine only. |
| **Charge** | A formal accusation of a crime. |
| **Count** | Each individual charge in an indictment or complaint. A defendant may face multiple counts. |
| **Indictment** | Formal charge by a grand jury. |
| **Information** | Formal charge filed by a prosecutor without a grand jury. |
| **Complaint** | Initial document charging a defendant; used at the arrest stage. |
| **Arraignment** | First court appearance; charges read, plea entered. |
| **Plea** | The defendant's formal response to charges: Guilty, Not Guilty, or No Contest. |
| **No contest (Nolo contendere)** | Accepts the punishment without admitting guilt. Cannot be used against the defendant in civil proceedings. |
| **Plea bargain** | Agreement between prosecution and defense to resolve the case with a lesser charge or sentence. |
| **Statute of limitations** | The time window within which charges must be filed after the crime. |
| **Mens rea** | Criminal intent; guilty mind. Most crimes require both a criminal act and criminal intent. |
| **Actus reus** | The physical act of committing the crime. |
| **Conspiracy** | Agreement between two or more persons to commit a crime, plus an overt act in furtherance. |
| **Accessory** | Person who assists with a crime but is not the principal actor. Accessory before the fact: planning. Accessory after the fact: helping after the crime. |
| **Accomplice** | Person who actively participates in committing the crime alongside the principal. |
| **Aiding and abetting** | Helping someone commit a crime. |
| **Attempt** | Trying to commit a crime without completing it. |
| **Solicitation** | Requesting or encouraging another person to commit a crime. |

## Civil Law Terms

| **Term** | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Tort** | A civil wrong that causes harm; grounds for a civil lawsuit. |
| **Negligence** | Failure to exercise reasonable care, resulting in harm to another. |
| **Damages** | Monetary compensation awarded to the winning plaintiff. |
| **Compensatory damages** | Reimburse the plaintiff for actual loss. |
| **Punitive damages** | Additional award meant to punish the defendant for egregious conduct. |
| **Injunction** | Court order requiring a party to do or stop doing something. |
| **Contract** | Legally binding agreement between parties. |
| **Breach of contract** | Failure to perform a contractual obligation. |
| **Liability** | Legal responsibility for an act or failure to act. |
| **Statute** | A law enacted by a legislature. |
| **Ordinance** | A law enacted by a local government (city, county). |

---

## Evidence and Procedure Terms

| **Term** | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Admissible** | Evidence that is legally allowed to be presented at trial. |
| **Inadmissible** | Evidence that cannot be used because it was obtained illegally or violates a rule. |
| **Hearsay** | An out-of-court statement offered to prove the truth of the matter. Generally inadmissible with many exceptions. |
| **Foundation** | The preliminary showing required before evidence is admitted. |
| **Subpoena** | Court order requiring a person to testify or produce documents. |
| **Discovery** | Pre-trial exchange of evidence between parties. |
| **Deposition** | Out-of-court sworn testimony recorded for later use. |
| **Stipulation** | An agreed fact that both parties accept; does not need to be proven at trial. |
| **Voir dire** | Jury selection; questioning potential jurors to identify bias. Also used for preliminary questioning of a witness about their qualifications. |
| **Exhibit** | A physical piece of evidence introduced at trial. |

---

## Sentencing and Post-Trial

| **Term** | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Sentence** | The punishment imposed after a conviction. |
| **Concurrent sentences** | Multiple sentences served at the same time. |
| **Consecutive sentences** | Multiple sentences served one after another. |
| **Probation** | Supervised release in the community instead of incarceration. |
| **Parole** | Early release from prison under supervision. |
| **Suspended sentence** | A sentence that is not immediately carried out; the defendant must comply with conditions or serve it. |
| **Restitution** | Payment ordered to compensate a victim for harm suffered. |
| **Acquittal** | A not guilty verdict; defendant is free. Cannot be re-tried for the same offense. |
| **Conviction** | A guilty verdict or guilty plea. |
| **Appeal** | A request for a higher court to review a lower court's decision. |
| **Remand** | Order to return the case to a lower court with instructions. |
| **Habeas corpus** | A petition challenging the legality of imprisonment. |
