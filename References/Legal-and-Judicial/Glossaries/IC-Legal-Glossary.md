# Legal and Judicial — In-Character Glossary

Language used in **courtrooms, legal proceedings, and by legal professionals** in the world of Los Santos. These are the terms lawyers, judges, prosecutors, public defenders, and court staff use — formal legal vocabulary as it exists inside the RP world.

---

## Court Structure and Personnel

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **The Bench** | The judge's raised platform — also used to refer to the judge's authority. "Approach the bench." "Take it to the bench." |
| **The Bar** | The dividing railing in a courtroom separating the public gallery from the legal space — also used to refer to the legal profession as a whole. "Admitted to the bar" means licensed to practice law. |
| **The Court** | The judge, or more broadly, the formal legal institution. "The court has ruled." |
| **Counsel** | The attorney or legal representative for a party. "Counsel may proceed." |
| **Opposing counsel** | The attorney representing the other party. |
| **The State / The Crown / The People** | The prosecution — representing the government and public interest in a criminal case. "The People versus James Moretti." |
| **The Defense** | The party representing the accused. |
| **Plaintiff** | In civil cases, the party initiating the lawsuit — the one claiming harm. |
| **Defendant** | The person being accused or sued. |
| **Witness** | A person called to give testimony about what they know. |
| **Expert witness** | A witness with specialized knowledge who provides opinion testimony. |
| **Bailiff** | The court officer responsible for maintaining order and security in the courtroom. |
| **Court reporter / Stenographer** | The person recording every word spoken during proceedings. |
| **Clerk of Court** | Administrative officer who manages court records and scheduling. |

---

## Proceedings and Process

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Arraignment** | The first formal court appearance after arrest — the defendant is read their charges and enters a plea. |
| **Plea** | The defendant's formal response to charges. Options: Guilty, Not Guilty, No Contest (Nolo Contendere). |
| **Preliminary hearing** | A hearing to determine if there is enough evidence to proceed to trial. |
| **Grand jury** | A panel reviewing evidence to decide whether to formally charge a defendant. |
| **Indictment** | A formal charge issued by a grand jury. |
| **Information** | A formal charge filed by a prosecutor directly, without a grand jury. |
| **Motion** | A formal request to the court for a specific ruling or action. |
| **Motion to suppress** | A request to exclude specific evidence from trial, usually because it was obtained illegally. |
| **Motion to dismiss** | A request to end the case entirely due to lack of evidence or procedural error. |
| **Discovery** | The pre-trial process of exchanging evidence between prosecution and defense. Both sides must share what they plan to use. |
| **Subpoena** | A legal order requiring a person to appear in court or produce documents. |
| **Voir dire** | The jury selection process — questioning potential jurors to assess bias. |
| **Opening statement** | Each side's introduction to the jury at the start of trial — what the evidence will show. |
| **Examination / Direct examination** | Questioning a witness called by your own side. |
| **Cross-examination** | Questioning the opposing side's witness. |
| **Redirect** | Questioning your own witness again after cross-examination, to address new issues raised. |
| **Recess** | A temporary break in proceedings ordered by the judge. |
| **Sidebar** | A private conference between the judge and attorneys at the bench, away from the jury. |
| **Closing argument** | Each side's final statement to the jury summarizing the evidence and their interpretation. |
| **Verdict** | The jury's (or judge's) final decision: Guilty or Not Guilty. |
| **Sentencing** | The hearing at which the judge determines punishment after a guilty verdict or plea. |

## Evidence and Legal Standards

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Burden of proof** | The obligation to prove one's claim. In criminal cases, the prosecution bears this burden. |
| **Beyond a reasonable doubt** | The standard of proof in criminal cases — the jury must be firmly convinced of guilt. The highest legal standard. |
| **Preponderance of the evidence** | The standard in civil cases — more likely true than not (greater than 50%). Lower than criminal standard. |
| **Probable cause** | A reasonable basis to believe a crime was committed or that evidence will be found. Required for arrest or search. |
| **Reasonable suspicion** | A lower standard — enough basis for a brief stop and investigation, but not arrest. |
| **Admissible** | Evidence that may be presented to the court. |
| **Inadmissible** | Evidence that cannot be used at trial — usually because it was obtained illegally or is unreliable. |
| **Hearsay** | Out-of-court statements offered as proof of the matter stated. Generally inadmissible with exceptions. |
| **Chain of custody** | Documentation tracking evidence from collection to court — any gap may make evidence inadmissible. |
| **Exhibit** | A physical item or document entered into evidence. "Exhibit A, Your Honor." |
| **Circumstantial evidence** | Evidence that implies a fact but does not directly prove it. |
| **Direct evidence** | Evidence that directly proves a fact — eyewitness testimony, a confession. |
| **Testimony** | Spoken or written evidence given under oath. |
| **Affidavit** | A written sworn statement. |
| **Perjury** | Lying under oath. A serious crime. |

---

## Verdicts and Sentencing

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Acquittal** | A not-guilty verdict — the defendant is cleared of the charge. |
| **Conviction** | A guilty verdict — the defendant is found to have committed the crime. |
| **Sentence** | The punishment ordered by the judge. |
| **Probation** | A supervised period in the community instead of prison. |
| **Parole** | Early release from prison under supervised conditions. |
| **Suspended sentence** | A sentence that is not immediately served — activated only if the defendant violates conditions. |
| **Time served** | A sentence that equals the time already spent in custody — the defendant is released immediately. |
| **Expungement** | The legal removal of a conviction from a person's record. |
| **Appeal** | A request to a higher court to review and overturn a lower court's decision. |
| **Mistrial** | A trial invalidated due to a fundamental error — must be retried. |
| **Double jeopardy** | A person cannot be tried twice for the same crime after acquittal. |
