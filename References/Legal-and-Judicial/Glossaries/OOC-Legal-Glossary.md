# Legal and Judicial — Out-of-Character Glossary

Terms used by **players** when discussing legal and judicial roleplay outside of character. Covers the systems, mechanics, and conventions of court RP, attorney roles, and judicial proceedings on an RP server.

---

## Roles and Whitelisting

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **DOJ (Department of Justice)** | The judicial branch faction on most RP servers — encompasses judges, prosecutors, defense attorneys, and court staff. Usually whitelisted. |
| **Judge whitelist** | A role requiring an application and vetting process. Judges have authority to run court proceedings, issue warrants, and sentence defendants. |
| **Attorney whitelist** | A role for players who want to represent clients in court proceedings. May require a written test or knowledge of server legal procedures. |
| **Public Defender** | A whitelisted attorney role representing defendants who cannot afford private counsel. |
| **District Attorney / Prosecutor** | A whitelisted attorney who represents the state in criminal trials. Works with law enforcement. |
| **Private Attorney** | A player character offering legal services independently — not employed by the DOJ. |
| **Court staff** | Non-legal roles supporting proceedings — bailiff, stenographer, clerk. |
| **Legal faction** | Any DOJ sub-group or independent law firm operating on the server. |

## Court RP Mechanics

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Court RP** | Roleplay involving formal legal proceedings — arraignments, hearings, trials, sentencing. |
| **Court session / Hearing** | A scheduled RP event where legal proceedings take place. Usually requires scheduling, a judge, and both parties. |
| **Case file** | The document package for a legal case — charges, evidence, witness list, statements. Must be prepared before trial. |
| **Filing charges** | The OOC/IC action of a prosecutor or officer formally charging a defendant with a crime. |
| **Warrant** | An in-game or CAD document authorizing arrest or search, usually requiring judicial approval. |
| **Bail** | An IC system where a defendant is released pending trial in exchange for a monetary deposit. Some servers script this; others RP it manually. |
| **Plea deal** | An agreement where the defendant pleads guilty to a lesser charge in exchange for a reduced sentence. Requires both the prosecutor and judge to agree. |
| **Continuance** | Postponing a scheduled court hearing to a later date. |
| **In chambers** | A private meeting between a judge and attorneys, out of public courtroom. Used for procedural discussions. |

## Evidence and Documentation

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Evidence submission** | The process of formally adding evidence to a case file. On some servers, physical evidence must be logged in CAD or a separate system. |
| **Chain of custody log** | Documentation proving evidence was handled properly and not tampered with between collection and court. |
| **Body cam footage** | IC reference to video evidence from a police officer's worn camera. On some servers, this is a scripted mechanic. |
| **Dashcam footage** | Video evidence from a vehicle's camera. Similar to body cam — scripted or RP-implied. |
| **Exhibit list** | The complete list of evidence both parties plan to present at trial, shared during discovery. |
| **Witness list** | The list of people each side plans to call to testify. Must be disclosed to opposing counsel before trial. |
| **Redacted** | Information removed from a document before disclosure — portions not visible for one party. |

## Post-Proceedings

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Time served** | An IC sentence equal to time already spent in custody — defendant leaves immediately after verdict. |
| **Sentence served in DOC** | Sentence served in the Department of Corrections facility, requiring the player to stay offline or in character at a jail location for a defined period. |
| **Concurrent / Consecutive sentences** | Multiple charges either served at the same time (concurrent) or back-to-back (consecutive). |
| **Expungement process** | An IC/OOC system for removing charges from a character's record — usually requires a petition, time elapsed, and no new offenses. |
| **Appeal process** | A formal request to a senior judge to review a lower court ruling. |
| **Case closed** | A case that is fully resolved and archived — no further proceedings scheduled. |
| **Cold case** | A criminal case with no current active proceedings — may be reopened with new evidence. |

## Common Legal RP Acronyms

| Acronym | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **DOJ** | Department of Justice |
| **DA** | District Attorney |
| **PD** | Public Defender (also Police Department — context determines meaning) |
| **ADA** | Assistant District Attorney |
| **BOE** | Beyond a Reasonable Doubt (shorthand in legal discussions) |
| **MTS** | Motion to Suppress |
| **MTD** | Motion to Dismiss |
| **P&S** | Plea and Sentencing |
