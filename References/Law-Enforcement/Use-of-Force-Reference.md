# Use of Force Reference

A guide to the use of force framework for law enforcement roleplay. This outlines the escalation ladder, justification standards, and documentation expectations for realistic LEO RP interactions involving force.

---

## Use of Force Continuum

The use of force continuum is a framework describing levels of force and when each is appropriate. Each level should only be used when lower levels have been exhausted or are clearly insufficient given the threat.

| Level | Force Type | When Appropriate |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Officer Presence | Mere presence of a uniformed officer may deter or resolve a situation |
| **2** | Verbal Commands | Commanding compliance: "Stop," "Get on the ground," "Drop the weapon" |
| **3** | Empty Hand Soft | Non-damaging physical control: guiding, wrist locks, escort holds |
| **4** | Empty Hand Hard | Strikes, takedowns, physical control involving injury risk |
| **5** | Less Lethal | Taser, pepper spray, rubber rounds, bean bag rounds, baton |
| **6** | Deadly Force | Firearm; only when there is an imminent threat of death or serious bodily harm |

---

## Deadly Force Justification

Deadly force is authorized only when an officer has **reasonable belief** that a subject poses an **imminent threat of death or serious bodily injury** to the officer or another person.

### Threat Indicators (IC)
These conditions support the use of deadly force:

- Subject is actively firing a weapon or pointing it at an officer/civilian
- Subject is charging with a melee weapon at close range with clear intent to use it
- Subject is in a vehicle attempting to run over officers or civilians
- Subject has control of an officer's firearm

### Does NOT justify deadly force alone:
- Running away (unless fleeing felon doctrine applies — known to have committed a violent crime)
- Verbal threats without capability or imminence
- Being in possession of a weapon while compliant

---

## Less Lethal Options

| Tool | IC Name | Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| **Taser / CEW** | Conducted Energy Weapon | Actively resisting subject; no deadly threat but physical control failed |
| **Pepper spray / OC** | Oleoresin Capsicum | Subject is physically resisting or aggressive; less than deadly threat |
| **Baton / ASP** | Expandable baton | Physical control; strikes to large muscle groups |
| **K-9** | Police dog | Track and locate, building search, active fleeing felon |

---

## Resistance Classification

| Category | Description | Appropriate Response |
|---|---|---|
| **Passive resistance** | Not moving, going limp, refusing to comply but not physically aggressive | Verbal, escort, soft hands |
| **Active resistance** | Pulling away, tensing, attempting to break free | Soft or hard hands, consider less lethal |
| **Aggressive resistance** | Striking, grabbing, fighting the officer | Hard hands, less lethal |
| **Deadly resistance** | Weapon, vehicle as weapon, imminent lethal threat | Deadly force authorized |

---

## Force Documentation

After any use of force beyond soft-hands, an officer is expected to complete:

1. **Use of Force Report** — Describing the subject's behavior, the officer's response at each level, and the outcome
2. **Witness statements** — Other officers or civilians present
3. **Evidence notes** — Any physical evidence documenting the subject's threat (weapon, vehicle, injuries to officer)
4. **Medical check** — Any subject taken into custody after force should be evaluated by EMS

## Common Roleplay Scenarios

### Routine Stop Escalation
- Subject initially compliant → officer maintains Officer Presence
- Subject refuses to step out → Verbal commands
- Subject physically resists → Soft-hand techniques
- Subject strikes officer → Hard hands or less lethal
- Subject produces weapon → Deadly force consideration

### Vehicle Pursuit Ending in Foot Chase
- Officer maintains verbal commands during vehicle stop
- Subject flees on foot → Verbal commands, pursuit
- Subject is non-violent fleeing felon → Do not deploy deadly force for flight alone
- Subject stops and produces weapon → Deadly force consideration

### Mental Health Crisis
- De-escalate when possible — time, distance, verbal
- Avoid force escalation unless imminent danger
- Request EMS / mental health resources as early as possible
- If subject has a weapon but is stationary and not actively threatening → Time + containment preferred over immediate force

---

## De-escalation Principles

When time and safety allow, officers should use de-escalation:

1. **Slow down** — Do not rush the encounter
2. **Create distance** — Distance gives time and reduces immediate threat
3. **Use a calm voice** — Match the communication style to the person's state
4. **Offer choices** — "You can walk over here and we'll talk about this" gives the person agency
5. **Listen** — Acknowledge what the person is saying
6. **Call for help** — Supervisors, EMS, mental health resources
7. **Be patient** — The goal is resolution, not speed
