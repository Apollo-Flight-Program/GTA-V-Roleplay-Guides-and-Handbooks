# Law Enforcement — Out-of-Character Glossary

This glossary covers terms that **players** use when discussing law enforcement roleplay outside of character — in Discord, OOC chat, staff tickets, and community conversations. These are the mechanics, systems, and community concepts unique to playing a law enforcement character on an RP server.

---

## Whitelisting and Roles

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Whitelisted LEO** | A law enforcement character whose role requires an approved application. The player must meet requirements and maintain standards to keep the role. |
| **Ride-along** | A session where a new or prospective officer joins an existing officer's patrol without independent authority. Used for training and evaluation. |
| **Civilian observer** | A non-LEO player permitted to accompany police for RP purposes only — no enforcement powers. |
| **FTO (Field Training Officer)** | An experienced officer player who oversees a new officer's field training period and evaluates their readiness for solo patrol. |
| **Cadet / Recruit** | A player who has been accepted into a law enforcement faction but has not yet completed training. |
| **Reserve / Part-time** | A LEO role with fewer requirements than full-time officers — often limited powers or limited hours. |
| **Supervisor** | An officer of sergeant rank or above with authority to oversee other officers, authorize certain actions, and handle escalated situations. |
| **SWAT / Tactical** | A whitelisted sub-unit within law enforcement for high-risk situations. Usually requires additional application and training. |
| **Unmarked / Plain clothes** | Playing a LEO character without a uniform or marked patrol car. Usually detective or undercover roles. |

## Systems and Tools

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **CAD (Computer Aided Dispatch)** | The web application or in-game system used by LEO to log calls, run records, create warrants, file arrests, and track units. |
| **MDT (Mobile Data Terminal)** | In-game tablet or computer used by officers to access CAD from their vehicle or on foot. |
| **Dispatch** | Either the automated system or a designated player who routes calls to officers and tracks unit status. |
| **BOLO** | Be On the Lookout — an alert broadcast to all on-duty officers via CAD or radio. |
| **Warrant** | An in-game or CAD-filed document authorizing arrest or search of a specific person or location. |
| **10-speed** | Colloquial for checking your CAD / MDT notifications. |
| **Priority scene** | A high-intensity criminal event (robbery, hostage, chase) that requires active LEO response and takes up server "priority slots." |
| **Soft priority** | A lower-intensity incident that is active but does not require the full attention of all available units. |

## Patrol and Scenes

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Traffic stop** | A basic LEO scene involving stopping a vehicle for a violation or suspicion. One of the most common daily RP scenes. |
| **Code 3 chase / Pursuit** | A vehicle chase scene. Usually scripted in servers to have limits on participating units and resolution requirements. |
| **Foot chase** | A chase conducted on foot after a vehicle stop or on-foot encounter. |
| **Search and detain** | Temporarily stopping and searching a suspect. Requires IC justification and follows server-specific rules. |
| **Felony stop procedure** | The multi-officer procedure for stopping a potentially armed vehicle. Includes positions, commands, and escalation. |
| **Scene control** | Managing an active incident — keeping civilians out, organizing units, coordinating with EMS. |
| **Perimeter** | Officers positioned around an area to prevent suspect escape. |
| **Pull-over** | Initiating a traffic stop. |
| **Book / Booking** | The process of logging an arrest in CAD with charges, evidence, and personal information. |
| **Processing** | Taking a suspect through the formal intake procedure at the police station. |

## Discipline and Standards

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **IA (Internal Affairs)** | A server faction or process that investigates misconduct by law enforcement characters. |
| **Complaint** | An OOC report or IC formal complaint filed against an officer's behavior. |
| **Roster** | The official list of active members in a law enforcement faction. Being off-roster means not being permitted to play the role. |
| **Inactivity strike** | A mark against an officer's record for not meeting the server's activity requirements for their role. |
| **Demotion** | A reduction in IC rank, usually triggered by rule violations or sustained IA findings. |
| **Termination (IC)** | A character being fired from the department. The player may be removed from the whitelist or permitted to reapply. |
| **Powergaming a stop** | Using a traffic stop as an excuse to search players without any real IC reason — a common complaint in LEO RP. |
| **Code 4 fishing** | Clearing a scene prematurely (Code 4) to move on without resolving the RP properly. |
