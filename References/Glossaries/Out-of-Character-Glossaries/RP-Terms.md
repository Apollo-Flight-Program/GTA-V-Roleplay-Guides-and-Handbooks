# Glossary — Roleplay Terms

This glossary covers the core terminology used across GTA V roleplay communities regardless of platform, server, or culture. These are the terms every player should know before joining any serious RP server.

---

| Term | Full Form | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| RP | Roleplay | The act of playing a fictional character in a shared story world |
| IC | In-Character | Speaking, acting, or thinking as your character — inside the fiction |
| OOC | Out-of-Character | Speaking or acting as the player — outside the fiction |
| NLR | New Life Rule | After a character dies and respawns, they forget the events leading to their death |
| FRP | Fear Roleplay | The requirement that characters realistically fear death, injury, and threats |
| CK | Character Kill | The permanent, voluntary death of a character — their story ends forever |
| PK | Player Kill | Killing another player's character — does not necessarily mean permanent death |
| Perma | Permanent Death | Same as CK — the character is retired permanently |
| RDM | Random Deathmatch | Killing another player without a valid roleplay reason or prior interaction |
| VDM | Vehicle Deathmatch | Using a vehicle as a weapon to injure or kill players without RP justification |
| Metagaming | — | Using out-of-character information to influence in-character decisions |
| Powergaming | — | Forcing unrealistic or unfair outcomes that give your character an unfair advantage |
| Godmodding | — | A form of powergaming — playing your character as invincible or all-knowing |
| Fail RP | Failed Roleplay | Acting in ways that make no realistic sense for the situation or character |
| Combat Log | — | Disconnecting from the server during an active hostile RP scene to avoid consequences |
| Cop Bait | — | Deliberately provoking law enforcement repeatedly with no story purpose |
| Stream Snipe | — | Watching a live stream to gather real-time IC information about another player's character |
| Mixing | — | Blurring the line between IC and OOC — letting player knowledge or emotions affect character decisions |
| Breaking Character | — | Stepping out of the fiction mid-scene to speak as the player rather than the character |
| Green Zone | — | A designated server area where hostile actions are prohibited |
| Safe Zone | — | Same as Green Zone — a protected area, typically hospitals and spawn points |
| Whitelist | — | An access control system requiring players to apply and be approved before joining a server |
| Blacklist | — | A ban or block preventing a player from accessing a server |
| Lore | — | The established history, world details, and background of a server's fictional universe |
| Canon | — | Events and facts that are officially part of the server's story |
| SOP | Standard Operating Procedure | A documented set of steps for how a department or organization handles specific situations |
| Character Arc | — | The development and change a character undergoes over the course of their story |
| Backstory | — | A character's history before entering the current game world |
| Passive RP | — | Everyday civilian roleplay — conversations, jobs, daily life — with no conflict or action |
| Active RP | — | Roleplay involving direct conflict, crime, police interaction, or high-stakes situations |
| Emote | — | A text command describing a physical action or observable detail — typically /me or /do |
| /me | — | Command used to describe a physical action your character performs |
| /do | — | Command used to describe something observable in the environment around your character |
| Dark RP | — | Roleplay involving heavy, mature, or morally complex themes — crime, trauma, violence |
| ERP | Erotic Roleplay | Sexual roleplay — prohibited on virtually all serious GTA V RP servers |
| Faction | — | An organized in-game group — gang, department, business, organization |
| Whitelist Role | — | A faction or job that requires a separate application beyond the server whitelist |
| Scripted Event | — | A GM or admin-run planned scenario introduced into the server world |
| Organic RP | — | Roleplay that develops naturally from player interactions without admin planning |
| Rule of Six | — | A NoPixel-originated rule limiting any scenario or situation to a maximum of six participants |
| DAR | Double Action Rule | A rule preventing a player from performing two significant actions simultaneously — each action must allow the other player time to respond |
| Hot Mic | — | Accidentally leaving your transmit key active — broadcasting ambient sound and conversation unintentionally |
| Zerg | — | Overwhelming opponents through sheer numbers rather than skill, planning, or story |
| Griefer | — | A player whose primary goal is ruining other players' experiences rather than participating in the story |
| Troll | — | A player who deliberately disrupts RP for amusement with no intention of genuine participation |
| Ban Wave | — | A mass enforcement action removing multiple rule-violating players simultaneously |
| Appeal | — | A formal request to have a ban or punishment reviewed and potentially reversed |
| Permaban | — | A permanent ban with no expiry — may or may not be eligible for appeal after a waiting period |
| Staff | — | The administrative team responsible for server management and rule enforcement |
| GM | Game Master | A staff member responsible for creating and running narrative events within the server world |
| Admin | Administrator | A staff member responsible for enforcing rules, handling reports, and managing players |
| Mod | Moderator | A lower-tier staff member handling chat, minor violations, and player assistance |
| Ride-Along | — | A civilian or new player observing a department's operations without participating in enforcement |
| Escalation | — | The process by which a situation legitimately develops from lower to higher levels of conflict or force |
| Initiation | — | The required roleplay interaction that must precede hostile action against another player |
| Comply First | — | The practice of following server rules during a scene and raising disputes afterward through proper channels |
| IC Consequence | — | A story-level result of a character's actions — arrest, injury, reputation damage |
| OOC Consequence | — | A server-level result of a player's conduct — warning, ban, demotion |
| Debrief | — | A post-event or post-scene discussion among staff or players about what happened and how it was handled |
| Lore Friendly | — | Content or behavior consistent with the established fiction of the server world |
| Lore Break | — | An action or statement that contradicts or ignores the established server lore |
| Server Hop | — | Switching between multiple servers — sometimes used to evade bans or exploit different economies |
| Alt Account | — | A secondary account used to bypass bans or play multiple characters under different identities |
| Ban Evade | — | Using an alt account or other means to return to a server after being banned |
| Character Sheet | — | A document summarizing a character's background, personality, appearance, and key details |
| Voice RP | — | Roleplay conducted primarily through voice chat |
| Text RP | — | Roleplay conducted entirely through typed text — standard on servers like GTA World |
| In-game | — | Anything happening within the game world as part of the fiction |
| Out-of-game | — | Anything happening in the real world — Discord, Twitch, social media |
