# Business Types Reference

A guide to the types of businesses that exist in GTA V roleplay — their IC purpose, how they operate, staffing structure, and their potential roles in the broader server economy and storylines.

---

## Legitimate Businesses

### Food and Beverage

| Business Type | IC Description |
|---|---|
| **Restaurant / Diner** | Serves food and drinks; civilian social hub. Kitchen staff, servers, manager. |
| **Bar / Nightclub** | Entertainment and drinks; social RP hotspot. Bartenders, bouncers, DJ, manager. |
| **Coffee Shop / Café** | Relaxed social venue. Baristas, cashier. |
| **Food Truck** | Mobile food service; lower barrier to entry. Owner-operator model. |

**Business dynamics:** Food and beverage establishments are the #1 venue for passive civilian RP. They serve as neutral meeting places for characters across all factions.

---

### Automotive

| Business Type | IC Description |
|---|---|
| **Mechanic / Auto Shop** | Vehicle repair, modification, and resale. Mechanics, service manager. |
| **Car Dealership** | Sales of new or used vehicles. Salespeople, finance manager, lot staff. |
| **Towing Company** | Vehicle recovery and impound services. Tow operators, dispatcher. |
| **Taxi / Rideshare Company** | Civilian transport. Drivers, dispatch. |
| **Trucking Company** | Freight and cargo hauling. Drivers, logistics, dispatcher. |

**Story potential:** Auto shops are prime targets for chop shop front operations. Car dealerships create economic story — sales, fraud, high-value vehicle theft.

---

### Legal Services and Finance

| Business Type | IC Description |
|---|---|
| **Law Firm** | Legal representation. Partners, associates, paralegals, clerks. |
| **Accounting Firm** | Financial management. Accountants, auditors, administrators. |
| **Bank** | Financial services, loans, secure storage. Tellers, loan officers, security. |
| **Real Estate Agency** | Property sales and rental. Agents, brokers, administrative staff. |
| **Insurance Company** | Risk management and claims. Agents, adjusters. |

**Story potential:** Banks are frequent robbery targets. Law firms bridge criminal and legal RP. Accounting firms intersect with money laundering narratives.

## Criminal Fronts

Businesses that appear legitimate but serve as a cover for criminal operations.

| Front | Criminal Use |
|---|---|
| **Car wash** | Cash-heavy business; easy to launder income through |
| **Pawn shop** | Move stolen goods; launder proceeds |
| **Restaurant** | Cash revenue; layered through food sales |
| **Strip club / Gentleman's club** | Cash-based entertainment; cover for organized crime |
| **Import/Export warehouse** | Smuggling through legitimate freight channels |
| **Construction company** | Fake invoices and projects to launder money |
| **Pharmacy** | Prescription fraud, pill distribution |
| **Cleaning service** | Disposable labor structure; cash flow obfuscation |

## Business Structure Reference

### Small Business (1–5 employees)
- **Owner-operator** runs most functions
- Flat structure; all staff answer directly to owner
- IC: personal, relationship-based; owner knows every customer

### Medium Business (6–25 employees)
- Owner + **Manager** layer introduced
- Departments start forming (front of house / back of house; sales / operations)
- IC: staff may not all know each other; hierarchy becomes important

### Large Business / Corporation (25+ employees)
- Multiple management tiers
- **Board / Partners** → **Executive** → **Department Heads** → **Staff**
- IC: formal processes, meetings, power politics
