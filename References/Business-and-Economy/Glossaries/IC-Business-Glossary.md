# Business and Economy — In-Character Glossary

Language used by **business owners, entrepreneurs, employees, and economic characters** in the world of Los Santos. These are the terms characters use when running legitimate (or legitimately-fronted) enterprises.

---

## General Business Language

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **The Books** | Financial records of a business — income, expenses, payroll. "Keep the books clean." |
| **The Ledger** | A formal accounting record. "Everything goes in the ledger." |
| **Overhead** | Fixed operating costs — rent, utilities, payroll — that occur regardless of revenue. |
| **Margin / Profit margin** | The percentage of revenue remaining after costs. "We're running tight margins." |
| **In the black** | Operating at a profit. Financially healthy. |
| **In the red** | Operating at a loss. Spending more than earning. |
| **Turnover** | Total revenue generated before deductions. Also: staff turnover — rate of employees leaving. |
| **Capital** | Money invested or available to invest in a business. |
| **Cash flow** | The movement of money in and out of a business. "Cash flow is the lifeblood of any operation." |
| **Float** | A small amount of cash kept available for immediate expenses or change. |
| **Payroll** | The total of employee wages and the process of distributing them. |
| **Cut** | A share of profits. "Everyone who puts in work takes a cut." |
| **Commission** | A percentage of a sale paid to the person who made it. |
| **Invoice** | A bill issued by a business to a client for goods or services rendered. |
| **Contract** | A formal agreement between two parties — service, employment, supply. |
| **Vendor** | A supplier who provides goods or services to a business. |
| **Client / Customer** | A person paying for goods or services. |
| **Account** | A formal ongoing relationship with a customer or supplier. |
| **Supplier** | The source of goods for a business — the person or organization providing what you sell or use. |

---

## Real Estate and Property

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Commercial property** | A location used for business rather than residential purposes. |
| **Lease** | A rental agreement — paying for the right to use a property for a defined period. |
| **Deed** | The legal document proving ownership of a property. |
| **Equity** | The value of ownership in a property or business after debts are subtracted. |
| **Tenant** | A person renting a property. |
| **Landlord** | The owner of a property being rented. |
| **Storefront** | A retail-facing business location. |
| **Warehouse** | A large space used for storage, manufacturing, or distribution. |

---

## Negotiation and Deals

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Terms** | The conditions of an agreement. "What are your terms?" |
| **Counter** | A counteroffer in a negotiation. "I'll counter with 60-40." |
| **Good faith** | Acting with honest intent in a deal. "I'm acting in good faith here." |
| **Take it or leave it** | A final non-negotiable offer. |
| **Handshake deal** | An informal verbal agreement without documentation. |
| **Going rate** | The standard market price for something. "What's the going rate for that?" |
| **Lowball** | An intentionally low offer to start negotiations. |
| **Premium** | A price above the standard rate, reflecting quality, exclusivity, or urgency. |
| **Bulk discount** | A price reduction for large-quantity orders. |
| **Retainer** | A pre-paid fee securing a service provider's availability. Common for lawyers. |
| **Deposit** | Money paid in advance as security or part-payment. |
| **Default** | Failing to meet the obligations of a contract or loan. |

---

## The Underground Economy (Coded Language)

These terms appear in legitimate conversation but carry coded meaning in criminal business contexts.

| Term | IC Coded Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Consulting** | Providing criminal services or expertise. "He does consulting for the right people." |
| **Import / Export** | Smuggling. "They run an import-export operation out of the port." |
| **Procurement** | Acquiring goods illegally. "He handles procurement." |
| **Off the books** | Not recorded in official financial records — untaxed, untraced. |
| **Overhead costs** | Criminal operating expenses — bribes, protection payments, materials. |
| **Associate** | A person with loose ties to an organization, not a full member. Also used in business. |
| **Silent partner** | A person with financial stake in a business who is not publicly known. |
| **Investor** | In criminal contexts, someone providing criminal capital for a share of the proceeds. |
| **Distribution** | Moving product — criminal goods — to markets. |
