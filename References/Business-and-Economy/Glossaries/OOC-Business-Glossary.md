# Business and Economy — Out-of-Character Glossary

Terms used by **players** when discussing business and economy roleplay outside of character. Covers server mechanics, player conventions, and community concepts around running a legitimate or semi-legitimate business.

---

## Business RP Types

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Business RP** | Roleplay centered around operating a legitimate business — a restaurant, mechanic shop, dealership, legal firm, nightclub, etc. |
| **Player-run business** | A business operated by a player character, with real customers from the player base, rather than an NPC-operated service. |
| **Passive business** | A business that generates income without requiring constant active player involvement — often through server scripts. |
| **Active business** | A business that requires the owner or staff to be present and engaged to generate income or RP. |
| **Business whitelist** | A role requiring application to operate a specific type of business — often mechanic, dealer, legal services. |
| **Business license** | An IC document (and sometimes an OOC server permission) required to operate a business. |
| **Front business** | A legitimate business IC used to launder criminal proceeds. The criminal side is the real operation; the legitimate business is cover. |

## Server Economy Mechanics

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Economy script** | The server-side system managing money, pricing, and transactions. |
| **Money wipe / Economy reset** | A server decision to reset all or some player balances to address inflation or exploitation. |
| **Inflation** | An in-game economy condition where the value of in-game currency degrades because too much has been generated. |
| **Wealth cap** | A maximum amount of money a character can hold, used to prevent runaway accumulation. |
| **Passive income** | Money generated by scripts without active player involvement — rental income, business scripts, dividends. |
| **Dirty money** | In-game currency flagged as illegal — cannot be deposited at banks or spent at legitimate stores without laundering. Tracked by some economy scripts. |
| **Money laundering mechanic** | An in-game script or RP interaction that converts dirty money to clean money. |
| **Tax system** | A server mechanic where characters pay IC taxes — on income, property, or business operations. |
| **Bank robbery / Vault heist** | A scripted criminal event targeting the in-game banking system. Usually a priority scene with LEO response. |

## Player Business Operations

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Mechanic script** | An in-game system allowing mechanic characters to repair other players' vehicles, sometimes for in-game currency. |
| **Impound** | A mechanic or LEO-operated location where seized vehicles are stored. Owners pay to recover them. |
| **Dealership RP** | A player-run car dealership — selling vehicles to other players for in-game money, often with test drives and negotiations. |
| **Restaurant / Food RP** | Running a food service business. In some servers, food items restore health or have mechanical benefits. |
| **Club / Bar RP** | Operating a social venue — a nightclub, bar, or lounge where players gather. Primarily passive RP and social scenes. |
| **Real estate RP** | Buying, selling, and renting properties. On some servers, properties generate passive income or have storage functionality. |
| **Farming / Gathering** | An in-game mechanic to harvest raw materials — crops, ores, fish — used as input for crafting or selling. |
| **Crafting script** | An in-game system to convert raw materials into finished goods. |

## Common Business RP Acronyms

| Acronym | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **PRB** | Player-Run Business |
| **LEF** | Legal Entity / Legal Front |
| **POS** | Point of Sale (script/system) |
| **NPC vendor** | Non-player-character operated shop or service |
| **P2P** | Player to Player (transaction directly between two characters) |
