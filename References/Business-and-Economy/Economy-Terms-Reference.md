# Economy Terms Reference

A reference for economic terms used in business roleplay, criminal finance, and the broader in-game economy. Covers legitimate business finance, money laundering mechanics, and the IC/OOC language around economic systems.

---

## Basic Financial Terms

| **Term** | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Revenue** | Total money coming in from sales or services before deducting any costs. |
| **Expenses / Costs** | What it costs to operate the business. Rent, staff wages, supplies. |
| **Profit** | Revenue minus expenses. What is actually earned. |
| **Net profit** | Profit after all deductions including taxes and interest. |
| **Gross profit** | Revenue minus the direct cost of goods or services; before overhead. |
| **Cash flow** | The movement of money in and out over time. A profitable business can still fail if it runs out of cash. |
| **Capital** | Money or assets used to start or grow a business. |
| **Overhead** | Ongoing fixed costs — rent, utilities, staff — regardless of how much is produced or sold. |
| **Break-even point** | The level of sales at which revenue equals expenses; no profit or loss. |
| **Margin** | The percentage difference between cost and sale price. |
| **Invoice** | A document sent to a client requesting payment for goods or services rendered. |
| **Receipt** | Proof of payment issued to a buyer. |
| **Ledger** | The record of all financial transactions. |
| **Audit** | A review of financial records to verify accuracy and compliance. |
| **Balance sheet** | A snapshot of what a business owns (assets), owes (liabilities), and the owner's stake (equity). |

## Criminal Finance and Money Laundering

### The Three Stages of Money Laundering

| Stage | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Placement | Introducing illegal cash into the financial system — cash deposits, currency exchange, purchase of assets |
| **2** | Layering | Creating complex transactions to obscure the origin — transfers between accounts, purchasing and selling assets, shell companies |
| **3** | Integration | The money re-enters the legitimate economy as clean funds — investments, salaries, business income |

### Laundering Methods (IC Reference)

| Method | How It Works |
|---|---|
| **Cash-intensive business** | Inflate revenue of a real business (restaurant, bar, car wash) by mixing illegal cash with legitimate sales |
| **Shell company** | A company with no real operations; exists only to move money and issue fake invoices |
| **Loan-back** | Criminal lends their own dirty money to themselves or an associate through a front company; repays the "loan" with laundered funds |
| **Real estate** | Purchase property with dirty cash; sell it and receive clean proceeds |
| **Trade-based laundering** | Over/under invoice international shipments to move value across borders |
| **Smurfing** | Breaking large cash deposits into multiple smaller deposits to avoid detection thresholds |

## Business Economic Terms

| **Term** | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Supply** | The quantity of a good or service available in the market. |
| **Demand** | The desire and ability of buyers to purchase a good or service. |
| **Market price** | The price at which a good is currently trading in the market. |
| **Black market** | Illegal trade of goods; outside normal regulation and taxation. |
| **Grey market** | Trade that is technically legal but operates outside normal channels. |
| **Cartel** | A group of producers who agree to fix prices or production levels to control a market. |
| **Monopoly** | One seller controls the entire supply of a good or service. |
| **Price fixing** | Two or more competitors agree to set a minimum price, eliminating competition. |
| **Bribery / Kickback** | A payment made to secure a contract, decision, or favorable treatment. |
| **Embezzlement** | Theft or misappropriation of funds by someone trusted to manage them. |
| **Extortion / Protection** | Demanding payment under threat of violence or harm. |

## Taxation (IC Concept)

| **Term** | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Tax** | Compulsory payment to the government based on income, sales, or property. |
| **Income tax** | Tax on earnings. |
| **Sales tax** | Tax on purchases. |
| **Property tax** | Tax on owned land or buildings. |
| **Tax evasion** | Illegally hiding income or assets to avoid paying taxes. |
| **Tax avoidance** | Legally minimizing taxes through deductions and structuring. |
| **Shell account** | A financial account with no traceable beneficial owner; used to hide funds. |
| **Offshore account** | An account held in another jurisdiction to avoid taxes or scrutiny. |

## Market Dynamics in RP

In a living RP economy, prices and availability shift based on player actions:

| Situation | Effect |
|---|---|
| Large drug bust | Supply drops → price rises on the street |
| New gang moves in | Competition → price war or violent conflict |
| Law enforcement crackdown | Risk increases → prices rise to compensate |
| New smuggling route opened | Supply increases → prices drop |
| Luxury goods seized | Creates scarcity in legitimate market |
| Stock market manipulation (IC) | Drives story — characters short a stock, then trigger the event |
