# Government Structure Reference

A reference guide to the structure of government in GTA V roleplay. Covers the city of Los Santos, the county of Blaine County, the state of San Andreas, and federal presence — with the IC roles each level provides and how they interact.

---

## Overview: Levels of Government

| Level | Jurisdiction | Key Agencies |
|---|---|---|
| **City of Los Santos** | Urban municipal area | Mayor's Office, City Council, LSPD, LSFD |
| **County of Blaine County** | Rural unincorporated areas | Board of Supervisors, BCSO |
| **State of San Andreas** | All cities and counties | Governor's Office, State Legislature, SASP, Courts |
| **Federal** | National jurisdiction | FIB (equivalent of FBI), Federal Courts, Military |

---

## City of Los Santos Government

### Mayor's Office
The Mayor is the highest executive authority in the city.

| Role | Function |
|---|---|
| **Mayor** | Elected chief executive; sets city policy, signs ordinances, oversees departments |
| **Deputy Mayor** | Second in command; acts as Mayor in their absence |
| **Chief of Staff** | Manages the Mayor's office operations and scheduling |
| **Press Secretary** | Handles media communications and public statements for the Mayor |
| **Policy Advisors** | Advise on specific policy areas (public safety, economic development, etc.) |

### City Council
The legislative body of the city.

| Role | Function |
|---|---|
| **Council Members** | Elected representatives for city districts; vote on ordinances, budgets, resolutions |
| **Council President** | Presides over council sessions; sets the agenda |
| **City Clerk** | Records and maintains official city documents and meeting minutes |
| **City Attorney** | Legal counsel for the city; defends the city in lawsuits |

### City Departments

| Department | Function |
|---|---|
| **LSPD (Police)** | Law enforcement within city limits |
| **LSFD (Fire)** | Fire suppression and rescue |
| **EMS (Emergency Medical Services)** | Emergency medical response |
| **Department of Public Works** | Roads, utilities, infrastructure maintenance |
| **Department of Health** | Public health, code enforcement, licensing |
| **Planning and Zoning** | Land use approvals, building permits |
| **Department of Finance** | City budget, taxation, financial management |

---

## Blaine County Government

### Board of Supervisors
The legislative and executive body for the county.

| Role | Function |
|---|---|
| **Board of Supervisors** | Elected representatives for county districts |
| **Chairman** | Presides over board meetings |
| **County Executive / Administrator** | Professional manager overseeing day-to-day county operations |
| **County Counsel** | Legal counsel for the county |

### County Departments

| Department | Function |
|---|---|
| **BCSO (Sheriff's Office)** | Law enforcement for unincorporated county areas |
| **County Assessor** | Property valuation for taxation |
| **County Recorder** | Records deeds, property transfers, vital records |
| **Department of Social Services** | Welfare, housing assistance |

---

## State of San Andreas Government

### Executive Branch
| Role | Function |
|---|---|
| **Governor** | Elected chief executive of the state |
| **Lieutenant Governor** | Second in command |
| **Attorney General** | Top law enforcement officer of the state; prosecutes major cases |
| **Secretary of State** | Elections, official records |
| **State Treasurer** | State financial management |

### Legislative Branch
**The State Legislature**
- **Senate** — Upper chamber; longer terms
- **Assembly / House of Representatives** — Lower chamber; represents districts

Functions: passes state laws, approves state budget, confirms appointments.

### Judicial Branch
**The Court System**
| Court Level | Jurisdiction |
|---|---|
| **Trial Courts (Superior Courts)** | First-instance trials; where most cases are heard |
| **Court of Appeal** | Reviews trial court decisions for legal error |
| **Supreme Court of San Andreas** | Highest state court; final authority on state law |

### State Agencies

| Agency | Function |
|---|---|
| **SASP (State Police)** | Statewide law enforcement; highways, major crimes |
| **State Corrections** | Operates state prisons |
| **Department of Motor Vehicles** | Licenses, registrations, vehicle records |
| **Department of Taxation** | State tax collection |

---

## Federal Government Presence

| Agency | Function |
|---|---|
| **FIB (Federal Investigation Bureau)** | Serious organized crime, counterterrorism, corruption investigations |
| **IAA (International Affairs Agency)** | Intelligence; national security |
| **Federal Courts** | Crimes under federal law; major organized crime prosecutions |
| **SAMAF (San Andreas Military Forces)** | National defense; military operations |
| **Federal Reserve / Treasury** | Currency, financial regulation |

---

## How Government Levels Interact in RP

| Situation | Who Has Jurisdiction |
|---|---|
| Crime within city limits | LSPD primary |
| Crime in unincorporated county | BCSO primary |
| Crime crossing multiple jurisdictions | SASP coordinates; may involve joint task force |
| Major organized crime or terrorism | FIB takes lead; LEO agencies support |
| City law conflicts with state law | State law prevails |
| Emergency declaration | Governor activates SAMAF / National Guard |

---

## The Role of Government RP

Government RP generates:
- **Policy storylines** — budget fights, police reform debates, corruption investigations
- **Political campaigns** — elections, endorsements, scandals
- **Bureaucratic RP** — licensing hearings, permit disputes, city council drama
- **Criminal × government crossover** — bribery, corruption, underworld influence on politics
