# Government and Politics — Out-of-Character Glossary

Terms used by **players** when discussing government and political roleplay outside of character. Covers the conventions, mechanics, and community concepts around civic and governmental RP.

---

## Roles and Whitelisting

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Government RP / GovRP** | Roleplay centered on holding or interacting with elected or appointed government positions — mayor, city council, department heads. |
| **Mayor whitelist** | A role requiring server approval — the mayor character holds significant IC power over city decisions, law, and policy. |
| **City Council** | A group of player characters holding legislative seats — usually multiple applications, with defined terms and voting power. |
| **Department head** | An appointed or elected official leading a city department IC — often requiring separate whitelist or appointment by the mayor. |
| **Civil servant** | A character employed by city government in an administrative or service capacity. May be whitelisted or open. |
| **Election event** | A planned IC event in which the server community participates in selecting a mayor or council member. A major narrative and community engagement tool. |
| **Appointed position** | A government role filled by the mayor's selection rather than public vote. |
| **Term** | The defined IC period for which an official holds their position before another election or reappointment. |

## Government Event Types

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Council session** | A formal scheduled RP event where the city council convenes to debate and vote on ordinances and policy. |
| **Press conference** | An IC event where government officials address media and the public. Often GM-supported. |
| **Town hall** | An IC public forum where citizens interact directly with their elected officials. |
| **Signing ceremony** | An IC event where the mayor formally signs a new ordinance into law. |
| **State of the City** | An annual IC address by the mayor summarizing achievements and plans — a major narrative event. |
| **Budget hearing** | An IC session where departments present funding requests and the council allocates resources. |
| **Impeachment** | An IC process to remove an elected official for misconduct. Requires investigation, council vote, and significant narrative setup. |
| **Recall** | A mechanism for citizens (players) to vote to remove an official before their term ends. |

## Conduct and Standards

| Term | OOC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Separation of powers** | The principle that legislative, executive, and judicial branches are independent. Important for maintaining IC government balance. |
| **Abuse of power** | An official using their IC authority for personal gain or to target players unfairly. Both an IC violation and an OOC staff concern. |
| **Breaking the fourth wall** | A government player using OOC knowledge (who is a criminal, what crimes have occurred) to make IC government decisions. A form of metagaming. |
| **Roleplaying corruption** | Playing an IC corrupt official — requires explicit server permission and careful OOC coordination. High narrative value but high risk of abuse. |
| **Lore-friendly policy** | An IC policy that fits the established world setting — does not break immersion or contradict server lore. |
| **GM-coordinated governance** | Government RP events supported or narratively guided by the GM team — elections, major policy events, crises. |
