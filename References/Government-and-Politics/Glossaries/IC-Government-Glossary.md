# Government and Politics — In-Character Glossary

Language used by **government characters** — mayors, council members, department heads, civil servants, and political figures — in the world of Los Santos. These are the terms that appear in official proceedings, political discourse, and civic administration.

---

## Government Structure

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **City Hall** | The administrative center of Los Santos municipal government — where the mayor's office, city council chambers, and administrative departments are located. |
| **The Administration** | The mayor's leadership team and the executive branch of city government. |
| **The Council** | The city council — the legislative body responsible for passing ordinances and budgets. |
| **The Floor** | The main chamber where council debates and votes occur. "The motion is on the floor." |
| **The Chair** | The presiding officer of a meeting — usually the mayor or council president. "The chair recognizes..." |
| **Constituent** | A member of the public whom an elected official represents. |
| **Constituent service** | Helping individual citizens resolve problems with city government. |
| **The Department** | Any city agency — police, fire, public works, health. "The department has submitted their budget request." |
| **Department head / Director** | The senior official leading a city department. |
| **The Bureau** | A specialized division within a department. |
| **Interagency** | Involving or between multiple departments or agencies. "An interagency task force." |

---

## Legislative Process

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Ordinance** | A law passed by the city council. Local equivalent of a statute. |
| **Resolution** | A formal statement of policy or intent by the council — less binding than an ordinance. |
| **Bill** | A proposed law being considered by the council. |
| **Motion** | A formal proposal to take action, introduced during a meeting. "I move that we..." |
| **Second** | Formal agreement by a second council member to bring a motion to discussion. "I second the motion." |
| **Amendment** | A proposed change to a bill or ordinance during the legislative process. |
| **Reading** | A formal stage in passing legislation. Bills typically go through first and second readings. |
| **Majority vote** | A decision carried by more than half of the voting members. |
| **Supermajority** | A vote requiring a higher threshold — often two-thirds — for passage. |
| **Veto** | The mayor's power to reject a bill passed by the council. |
| **Override** | The council's ability to reverse a veto, usually requiring a supermajority. |
| **Tabling** | Postponing consideration of an item to a later meeting. |
| **Quorum** | The minimum number of members required to conduct official business. Without quorum, votes cannot be taken. |
| **Adjourn** | Formally ending a meeting. |
| **Executive session** | A closed, private session of the council — closed to the public. |

## Political Language

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Platform** | A politician's stated set of policy positions and priorities. |
| **Campaign** | The organized effort to win an election — speeches, rallies, public appearances. |
| **Constituency** | The body of voters and residents a politician represents. |
| **Incumbent** | The current holder of an elected office. |
| **Challenger** | A candidate running against the incumbent. |
| **Coalition** | An alliance of different factions or interest groups supporting a candidate or policy. |
| **Endorsement** | A public expression of support for a candidate from an organization, prominent figure, or publication. |
| **Lobbying** | Formally advocating for a specific interest or policy to elected officials. |
| **Special interest** | A group that advocates specifically for its own benefit, often through lobbying. |
| **Public record** | Official documents and actions of government, legally accessible to the public. |
| **Transparency** | The principle that government actions and decisions should be open and visible to citizens. |
| **Accountability** | The obligation of officials to explain and take responsibility for their decisions. |
| **Conflict of interest** | A situation where an official's personal interests may improperly influence their official decisions. |
| **Recusal** | Removing oneself from a vote or decision due to a conflict of interest. |

---

## Civil Service and Administration

| Term | IC Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Directive** | An official order from an executive authority. "The mayor issued a directive." |
| **Memo** | A formal internal communication. |
| **Policy** | An official statement of how the government will handle a recurring issue or situation. |
| **Regulation** | A rule issued by a department with the force of law, implementing a broader statute. |
| **Permit** | An official authorization to do something. |
| **License** | Official permission to operate a business or perform a regulated activity. |
| **Citation** | A formal notice of a violation — not criminal, but requiring a response or payment. |
| **Appropriation** | The formal allocation of funding for a specific purpose. |
| **Budget** | The government's plan for income and spending over a fiscal period. |
| **Fiscal year** | The 12-month accounting period for government finances. |
| **Surplus** | When income exceeds expenditure. |
| **Deficit** | When spending exceeds income. |
| **Audit** | A formal review of financial records for accuracy and compliance. |
